The Heron Symbol

Brand Guidelines for ScaleUp Ventures

This guide helps content creators use heron imagery and language effectively. The goal: convey our methodology without overdoing the metaphor. Use sparingly, use purposefully.

1

Why the Heron?

We didn't choose the heron because it looks nice. We chose it because it embodies exactly how we work with clients - and how successful business transformations actually happen.

Patience

The heron stands motionless, sometimes for hours. We don't rush to solutions. We observe, understand, then act.

Precision

When the heron strikes, it doesn't miss. Our recommendations are targeted, specific, and actionable - not vague advice.

Timing

The heron knows when to strike. Business transformation isn't about doing everything at once - it's about the right moves at the right moments.

The Core Insight

"Most consultants rush in with solutions. We believe the best results come from understanding first, then acting decisively. Like the heron, we watch before we strike."

How the Heron Connects to Our Method

Patient Observation Our discovery phase - we don't assume we know your business. We learn it.
Stillness Before Action We build complete understanding before making recommendations.
Precise Strike Our solutions are targeted. One right move beats ten wrong ones.
Perfect Timing We help you prioritize - what to do now, what to do next, what can wait.
2

Visual Guidelines

When to Use the Heron Logo

Use the Heron

  • Primary brand mark on website
  • Business cards and letterhead
  • Presentation title slides
  • Social media profile images
  • Email signatures
  • Proposal covers

Use Wordmark Instead

  • When space is very limited horizontally
  • Text-heavy documents where icon adds clutter
  • Co-branded materials with multiple logos
  • Contexts where icon meaning is unclear

Placement Rules

Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the heron's head around all sides of the logo. No text, images, or other elements should intrude into this space.

Minimum Size

Print: 0.75 inches (19mm) wide minimum. Digital: 32 pixels wide minimum. Below these sizes, use the simplified icon version or wordmark only.

Preferred Positions

Top-left (primary position), centered for hero sections, or bottom-right for secondary placement. Avoid bottom-left and mid-page floating positions.

Color Variations

Primary
Reversed
Mono Light
Mono Dark

Usage: Primary for most uses. Reversed on gold backgrounds. Mono versions for single-color printing or when color reproduction is limited.

3

Language Guidelines

Heron-Inspired Phrases

These phrases connect our methodology to the heron symbol. Use them sparingly - they're most powerful when they appear occasionally, not constantly.

  • "Watch. Then Strike." Our tagline. Use on homepage hero, presentation openers, email signatures. This is the one phrase that can appear frequently.
  • "Patience before precision" Use when explaining our discovery process. Why we don't rush to solutions.
  • "The patient observer" Describing our role. Works in about pages, methodology explanations.
  • "We stand still so you can move forward" Explaining the value of our observation phase. Use in proposals, case studies.
  • "One precise move beats ten rushed ones" Justifying focused strategy over scattered tactics. Sales conversations, proposals.

When to Use Heron Language

Good Moments

  • Opening a presentation or proposal
  • Explaining our methodology
  • Differentiating from competitors
  • Website hero sections
  • About page philosophy section
  • One memorable moment per piece

Skip the Metaphor

  • Every paragraph of a blog post
  • Tactical how-to content
  • Email subject lines (too clever)
  • Error messages or confirmations
  • When the audience wants facts, not poetry
  • More than once per page/section

The Golden Rule

If you've used a heron reference in the last three paragraphs, you don't need another one. The metaphor works because it's memorable - overuse makes it forgettable.

4

Storytelling with the Heron

Weaving the Metaphor Naturally

The heron metaphor works best when it emerges naturally from the content, not when it's forced. Here's how to integrate it effectively:

Set Up the Problem First

Describe the rushed, reactive approach most take. Let the reader feel the chaos of acting without understanding. Then introduce the heron as contrast.

Connect Behavior to Benefit

Don't just say "we're like the heron." Explain what that means for the client: better outcomes, less waste, more confidence in decisions.

Exit the Metaphor Gracefully

After using the heron image, return to concrete language. The metaphor opens the door; specifics walk through it.

Sample Paragraph

Most business consultants show up with a playbook. They've seen your industry before, they think they know the answers, and they're eager to implement. We take a different approach. Like the heron standing motionless at the water's edge, we begin by watching. We learn your business - not just the numbers, but the rhythms, the relationships, the unwritten rules that actually drive decisions. Only then do we act. And when we do, our recommendations are precise, because they're built on genuine understanding.

Notice: The heron reference is one sentence. It's surrounded by concrete business language. The metaphor illuminates; it doesn't dominate.

5

Do/Don't Examples

Real examples to guide your usage. The difference between memorable and cringey is often subtle - study these patterns.

Do This

"We believe in watching before we strike - understanding your business deeply before making recommendations."

Simple, clear, connects metaphor to benefit.

"Like the patient observer, we spent three weeks learning your operations before proposing changes."

Specific timeframe makes it concrete.

"Watch. Then Strike. It's not just our tagline - it's our methodology in action."

Tagline connected to real application. Direct and confident.

"Most agencies rush to execute. We've found that patience in the beginning creates precision in the end."

Subtle heron values without naming the bird.

Avoid This

"We're the herons of the consulting world, standing tall and majestic as we survey your business landscape!"

Too literal, too excited, too much.

"Like a heron catches fish, we'll catch all your business problems and eat them up!"

Extended metaphor gone wrong. Just... no.

"At ScaleUp, we're herons. We heron your strategy. We heron your operations. We heron everything."

Turning the noun into a verb. Please don't.

"Join the flock! Our heron methodology will help you spread your wings and soar!"

Mixed bird metaphors. Herons don't flock or soar. Know your animal.

Frequency Check

How Many Heron References Per Asset?

Homepage 1-2 references (hero tagline + optional about section)
Blog Post 0-1 references (only if naturally relevant to the topic)
Proposal 1-2 references (methodology section, possibly conclusion)
Email Sequence 1 reference across entire sequence (not every email)
Social Post Rarely - most posts should be value-focused, not brand-focused
Case Study 0-1 references (results matter more than metaphor)
6

The Philosophy in Action

Our heron methodology isn't abstract - it shows up in concrete decisions we make every day. Here are two examples that demonstrate "Watch. Then Strike." in practice:

Ad Campaign ROI: Certainty Before Spending

Before any paid advertising campaign begins, we quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads to ensure expected results meet client needs. This is patience in action - we don't strike (spend) until we understand exactly what we're aiming for.

The Heron Principle

We project the outcomes before committing the resources. If the numbers don't work on paper, they won't work in practice.

The No-Build Decision: When Not to Strike

Sometimes the heron watches... and decides not to strike. That's wisdom, not hesitation.

The Situation Client had $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment for a major build project.
Our Approach ScaleUp proposed a $5,000 discovery phase (4 weeks) instead of jumping into the full engagement.
What We Found Market saturation, hidden costs, and 80% of the desired outcome achievable with existing solutions.
The Result Saved the client $150,000+ and 12+ months of wasted effort.

Our Philosophy

"'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you."

These examples embody why the heron is our symbol: patient observation leads to better outcomes, even when - especially when - the right answer is "don't do it."

Quick Reference for Content Creators

The Heron Represents

Patience, precision, perfect timing. We observe before we act. We strike only when we're certain. We prioritize the right moves at the right moments.

Primary Phrase

"Watch. Then Strike." - This is our tagline. Use it confidently. It can appear on every major brand touchpoint.

Tone When Using

Confident, not cute. Insightful, not clever. The heron is about substance, not style. Let the metaphor illuminate, not decorate.

The Test

Before using a heron reference, ask: "Does this help the reader understand our value, or does it just sound nice?" If it's the latter, cut it.
