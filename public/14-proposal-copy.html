Introduction to ROI Model

How You'll Make Your Money Back

This is the part most agencies skip. They ask you to trust that their work will "pay off eventually." We think you deserve better than hope.

On the following page, you'll find an ROI model built specifically for [Client Company Name]. It uses your actual business numbers, not industry averages, to show exactly how this investment generates returns.

If the numbers don't work, we'll tell you. Even if it means we don't get this project. We'd rather earn your trust with honesty than close a deal that won't deliver.

How to Read the Projection

Understanding Your ROI Model

The projection on the next page shows:

  • Current State: Where you are today based on the metrics you shared
  • Projected Improvement: Conservative estimates of what this project will achieve, based on comparable results from similar businesses
  • Financial Impact: What that improvement means in dollars: new revenue, cost savings, or both
  • Payback Period: How many months until you've recovered your investment
  • 12-Month ROI: Total return on investment after one year

We use conservative estimates throughout. Most clients exceed these projections. But we prefer to under-promise and over-deliver.

Assumptions Stated

What This Model Assumes

Every projection rests on assumptions. Here are ours:

  • Your current metrics are accurate: We used the numbers you provided: [X monthly leads / $X average deal size / X% close rate / etc.]
  • Market conditions remain similar: No major industry disruptions or economic shifts
  • You implement as planned: The project is completed as scoped, on timeline
  • Your team follows through: Leads are followed up, processes are adopted, training is applied

Conservative Adjustments We've Made:

  • Used [X%] improvement estimates when comparable businesses saw [higher X%]
  • Assumed [X-month] ramp-up period before full results
  • Did not include [secondary benefits that are harder to quantify]

If any of these assumptions are wrong, let's discuss. The model only works if it reflects reality.
