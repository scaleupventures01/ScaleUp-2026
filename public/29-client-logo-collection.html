Why Client Logos Matter for Trust

Client logos serve as powerful social proof that validates your business credibility. When prospects see recognizable brands associated with your company, it creates an immediate trust signal that words alone cannot achieve.

92%
of consumers trust recommendations from others over brand content
37%
increase in conversion when client logos are displayed
4.2x
more likely to engage with content featuring familiar logos
58%
of B2B buyers consider client references before purchase

Key Trust Benefits

  • Borrowed credibility: Association with established brands transfers trust to your company
  • Risk reduction: Prospects see that others have successfully worked with you
  • Industry validation: Logos from your target industry show relevant experience
  • Size signaling: Enterprise logos suggest capability to handle complex projects
  • Visual shorthand: Logos communicate more in seconds than paragraphs of text

The ScaleUp Ventures Approach

We display client logos to demonstrate our track record of delivering certainty to ambitious companies. Each logo represents a relationship built on results and trust. Our approach:

  • Only feature clients who have given explicit permission
  • Prioritize quality of relationship over quantity of logos
  • Ensure visual consistency while respecting brand guidelines
  • Update regularly to reflect current client base

Logo File Format Requirements

Collecting logos in the right formats ensures consistent, professional display across all applications. Always request multiple formats when possible.

Format Priority Best For Notes
SVG Required Web, responsive design, any size Infinitely scalable, small file size, editable
EPS Required Print, large format, professional use Industry standard for vector, requires software to open
AI Optional Adobe workflows, editing Native Illustrator format, maintains all features
PNG (transparent) Required Web, presentations, documents Request 2x and 3x sizes for retina displays
PNG (with background) Optional Specific placement needs For use on colored or textured backgrounds
PDF Optional Print, sharing Vector if exported properly, universally viewable

File Organization Structure

/client-logos/ /[client-name]/ /vector/ [client-name]-logo-full-color.svg [client-name]-logo-full-color.eps [client-name]-logo-mono-dark.svg [client-name]-logo-mono-light.svg /png/ [client-name]-logo-full-color-400px.png [client-name]-logo-full-color-800px.png [client-name]-logo-grayscale-400px.png /guidelines/ [client-name]-brand-guidelines.pdf authorization.pdf notes.txt

When Clients Can Only Provide Limited Formats

If a client can only provide a JPG or low-resolution PNG:

  • Ask if their marketing team has vector files
  • Check if their brand kit is publicly available (press page)
  • Request the highest resolution version possible
  • Consider recreating the logo (with permission) if necessary
  • Document limitations in your notes file

Size and Resolution Requirements

Consistent sizing ensures a professional, balanced appearance across your logo displays. These specifications apply to different contexts.

Usage Context Recommended Width Resolution Notes
Website Logo Grid 160-200px display width SVG preferred; 2x PNG for raster Consistent visual weight across logos
Website Hero Section 200-300px display width SVG or 3x PNG Higher prominence positioning
Carousel/Slider 120-160px display width SVG preferred Multiple logos in scrolling view
Case Study Headers 80-120px display width SVG or 2x PNG Supporting element, not focal
Print Materials Minimum 1 inch width 300 DPI minimum Vector preferred for any print
Presentations 200-400px display width High-res PNG or SVG Consider projector resolution

Visual Sizing Consistency

Logos vary significantly in proportions. To create visual balance:

✓
Do
  • Normalize based on visual weight, not pixel dimensions
  • Test logos at actual display size before finalizing
  • Create bounding boxes with consistent proportions
  • Balance wide logos with tall logos in placement
  • Use CSS object-fit or similar for container consistency
✕
Don't
  • Force all logos to exact same pixel dimensions
  • Stretch or distort logos to fit containers
  • Display logos smaller than brand guidelines allow
  • Mix wildly different sizes in the same row
  • Ignore minimum clear space requirements

Retina/HiDPI Considerations

  • Standard displays: 1x resolution (72-96 PPI)
  • Retina displays: 2x resolution (144-192 PPI)
  • Super Retina: 3x resolution (216+ PPI)

Always provide 2x PNG files for raster logos, or use SVG which scales infinitely.

ScaleUp Ventures Recommended Treatment

Primary Recommendation: Grayscale with Hover Effect

Display all client logos in grayscale (#888888 base) with a smooth transition to full color on hover. This approach:

  • Maintains visual consistency with our Navy and Gold brand palette
  • Allows instant brand recognition upon interaction
  • Creates an elegant, sophisticated presentation
  • Encourages user engagement through discovery

CSS Implementation: filter: grayscale(100%); transition: filter 0.3s ease;

Treatment Restrictions

  • Never apply effects that distort the logo (blur, warp, 3D)
  • Maintain aspect ratios exactly as provided
  • Respect minimum size requirements even in grayscale
  • Check that grayscale versions maintain sufficient contrast
  • Get approval if significantly altering the visual presentation

"As Seen In" vs "Our Clients" Distinction

These are fundamentally different claims. Misusing them damages credibility. Understand and respect the distinction.

C
"Our Clients" / "Trusted By"

What it means: These companies have paid for and received your services/products.

Appropriate Labels:

  • Our Clients
  • Trusted By
  • Companies We've Worked With
  • Our Partners
  • Clients We've Helped

Requirements:

  • Direct business relationship
  • Services/products delivered
  • Permission to use their logo
  • Current or past engagement
P
"As Seen In" / "Featured In"

What it means: Your company has been mentioned, quoted, or featured by these media outlets.

Appropriate Labels:

  • As Seen In
  • Featured In
  • Press Coverage
  • Media Mentions
  • In The News

Requirements:

  • Actual published coverage
  • Verifiable article/segment
  • Media outlet logos only
  • Links to actual coverage
!
Critical Warning

Never mix client logos with media logos under the same heading. Never imply a company is a client when they're actually a media outlet that covered you, or vice versa. This is misleading and can damage trust.

Additional Categories to Consider

Category Meaning Example Labels
Partners Formal partnership or integration agreement Partners, Integration Partners, Technology Partners
Investors Companies/funds that have invested in you Backed By, Our Investors, Supported By
Certifications Official certifications or memberships Certified By, Member Of, Accredited By
Awards Recognition from award bodies Award Winner, Recognized By, Honored By

How to Handle Clients Who Decline

Some clients will decline logo usage requests. Handle this professionally and maintain the relationship. Remember: a declined logo request is not a rejection of your work.

Common Reasons for Declining

  • Policy restrictions: Company-wide rules against logo usage on vendor sites
  • Competitive concerns: Don't want competitors to know they use you
  • Brand control: Strict guidelines limiting external logo placement
  • Confidential relationship: The engagement itself is confidential
  • Legal/regulatory: Industry regulations prevent endorsements
  • Internal approval process: Too complex to be worth pursuing

Response Protocol

1
Acknowledge
Thank them for considering and accept gracefully
2
Confirm continued confidentiality
Reassure
3
Explore Alternatives
Ask about case study, quote, or reference
4
Document
Record decline and reason in your database

Response Email Template

Email Template
Subject: Re: Logo Permission Request Hi [Contact Name], Thank you for getting back to me and for considering our request. I completely understand - many companies have policies around logo usage, and we respect [Client Company]'s decision. Please be assured that we will not use your logo or mention our relationship publicly. If there are ever alternative ways to collaborate - such as anonymous case studies, industry benchmarks, or private references - we'd be happy to explore those options when appropriate. We remain grateful for the opportunity to work with your team and look forward to our continued partnership. Best regards, [Your Name]

Alternative Options to Explore

  • Anonymous case study (industry and company size, no name)
  • Aggregate statistics ("50+ financial services clients")
  • Private reference calls for qualified prospects
  • Quote without logo ("A Fortune 500 client")
  • Future request after specific milestone
i
Never Burn Bridges

Clients who decline now may approve later. Company policies change, contacts change, and relationships deepen. Set a reminder to revisit the request in 12-18 months if appropriate.

Update Process When Clients Rebrand

Client rebrands require proactive management. Outdated logos damage your credibility and disrespect client brands.

Monitoring for Rebrands

  • Set Google Alerts for client company names + "rebrand," "new logo," "brand refresh"
  • Follow client companies on LinkedIn and social media
  • Schedule quarterly reviews of displayed logos
  • Subscribe to industry news in client sectors
  • Maintain relationships with client contacts who can inform you

Rebrand Response Workflow

1
Detect
Identify rebrand through monitoring
2
Verify
Confirm rebrand is complete and public
3
Request
Contact client for new logo files
4
Update
Replace all instances of old logo
5
Archive
Keep old files for historical record

Update Checklist

  • Website logo grid/carousel
  • Case study pages
  • Proposal templates
  • Presentation decks
  • Marketing collateral
  • Social media posts (if applicable)
  • Press releases
  • Email signatures (if featured)
!
Urgent Priority

When a major client rebrands, treat the logo update as urgent. Displaying an outdated logo is embarrassing and suggests you're not paying attention to your clients. Aim to update within 1-2 weeks of detecting the rebrand.
