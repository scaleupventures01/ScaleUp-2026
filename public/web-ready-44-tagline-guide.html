Tagline Usage Guide

The definitive reference for ScaleUp Ventures messaging

1

Tagline Hierarchy

ScaleUp Ventures uses three taglines, each serving a distinct purpose. Understanding when to deploy each ensures consistent, impactful communication.

Primary Tagline
"Certainty First."

The master tagline. Encapsulates our entire brand promise in two words. Use this in all flagship communications and whenever you have limited space but need maximum impact. This is the tagline that should appear most frequently and become synonymous with ScaleUp Ventures.

Logo lockups Business cards Website header Email signatures Social profiles Signage Branded materials
Secondary Tagline
"Know Before You Spend."

The explanatory tagline. Clarifies what "Certainty First" means in practical terms. Use when you have space to elaborate or when addressing audiences unfamiliar with the brand. This tagline works as a supporting message or standalone in educational contexts.

Proposals Landing pages Sales decks Print ads Service pages Case studies
Heron-Specific
"Watch. Then Strike."

The metaphorical tagline. Connects to our heron brand imagery and communicates patient, strategic action. Use selectively in premium contexts where the heron visual is prominent. This tagline adds depth for audiences already familiar with the brand.

Premium collateral Executive materials Brand storytelling Event materials

Size Relationship Rules

  • Logo at 100px height: Tagline at 50px height (50% ratio)

  • Maximum tagline size: Never exceed 60% of logo height

Do's and Don'ts

Consistency requires discipline. Follow these rules to maintain tagline integrity across all applications.

Do

  • Use taglines exactly as written, including punctuation "Certainty First." (with period)
  • Match tagline to audience familiarity level New audiences: "Know Before You Spend."
    Existing contacts: "Certainty First."
  • Place taglines in a consistent position relative to the logo Always centered below, with 0.5x cap height spacing
  • Use italics for taglines in lockup configurations Certainty First.
  • Size taglines at 50% of logo height (40-60% range acceptable)
  • Use "Watch. Then Strike." only when heron imagery is present Executive materials with heron watermark or logo
  • Pair taglines with supporting copy that expands the message "Certainty First. Every project starts with a detailed ROI model."
  • Use gold (#D4A853) for taglines on navy backgrounds
  • Use gray or navy for taglines on light backgrounds
  • Omit taglines when space is too small (under 12px)

Don't

  • Alter the wording of any tagline "Certainty Always." "See Then Spend." "Watch and Strike."
  • Remove punctuation from taglines "Certainty First" (missing period)
  • Use multiple taglines in the same layout "Certainty First. Know Before You Spend."
  • Place taglines above or to the side of the logo Tagline should always appear below
  • Use "Watch. Then Strike." without heron visual context The metaphor requires the imagery to make sense
  • Make taglines the same size as the logo Tagline should be visually subordinate
  • Use taglines in body copy mid-sentence "At ScaleUp Ventures, we believe in Certainty First approach."
  • Create new taglines or variations Only the three approved taglines may be used
  • Use different colors than brand palette for taglines No bright blue, red, or other off-brand colors
  • Stack taglines vertically (one word per line) Taglines should always read horizontally
