Brand Guidelines Section

"Certainty First" - Proving ROI Before You Invest

Why a Proof Library Matters

At ScaleUp Ventures, our brand promise of "Certainty First" demands that every claim we make is backed by verifiable evidence. The Proof Library is the centralized repository where we collect, organize, and maintain all proof points that demonstrate our value to clients.

This section of the brand guidelines establishes the standards, processes, and organizational structure for managing proof assets that support our credibility and convert prospects into clients.

1

Purpose of the Proof Library

Strategic Intent

The Proof Library serves as the single source of truth for all evidence that supports ScaleUp Ventures' claims, positioning, and value propositions. It ensures that every team member can access verified, up-to-date proof points when communicating with prospects, partners, and stakeholders.

Core Objectives

  • Credibility Reinforcement: Provide concrete evidence that backs our "Certainty First" promise
  • Sales Enablement: Arm sales teams with relevant, verified proof for every stage of the buyer journey
  • Consistency: Ensure all teams use the same approved statistics, quotes, and case studies
  • Compliance: Maintain proper documentation and releases for all proof assets
  • Competitive Advantage: Differentiate through demonstrable results rather than empty claims

Brand Alignment: Every proof point in this library directly supports our positioning as the firm that proves ROI before clients invest. If a piece of evidence doesn't demonstrate measurable outcomes, it doesn't belong here.

Categories of Proof

Four Pillars of Proof

ScaleUp Ventures organizes all proof assets into four primary categories, each serving distinct purposes in the sales and marketing process.

T Testimonials

Direct quotes from clients and partners that speak to their experience working with ScaleUp Ventures.

In-depth narratives that document the challenge, solution, and measurable outcomes of client engagements.

S Statistics

Quantifiable data points that demonstrate impact, scale, and performance metrics.

  • Client ROI figures

C Credentials

Third-party validations, certifications, and affiliations that establish authority and trust.

  • Industry certifications
  • Awards and recognition
  • Partner badges
  • Team credentials
  • Media mentions

Quality Standards for Proof

The Three R's of Proof Quality

All proof in the library must meet our "3R" quality standard to maintain the credibility that underpins our "Certainty First" promise.

Specific

Quantified Results

Proof must include concrete numbers, percentages, or timeframes - not vague claims. "Increased revenue by 34%" not "significantly improved revenue."

Recent

Current Relevance

Statistics and results should be from the past 18 months. Older proof must be contextualized or retired. Markets change; our proof must reflect current reality.

Relevant

Audience Alignment

Proof must resonate with the specific audience and context. A CFO needs ROI figures; a CTO needs technical metrics. Match proof to persona.

Quality Gate: Before any proof enters the library, it must pass review by both Marketing and the original engagement lead to verify accuracy and appropriate framing.

Expiration/Refresh Policy for Proof Points

Proof Freshness Standards

To maintain the integrity of our "Certainty First" positioning, all proof must reflect current market conditions and recent outcomes. Stale proof undermines credibility.

6 MO
Quarterly Statistics Review

All numeric statistics and performance metrics reviewed for continued accuracy. Update figures if client data has changed or add context if markets have shifted.

12 MO
Annual Case Study Refresh

Full case studies reviewed annually. Reach out to clients for updated results, refresh language and design, consider adding follow-up outcomes.

18 MO
Testimonial Re-confirmation

Testimonials older than 18 months require re-confirmation from source. Update titles/roles, verify continued endorsement, refresh quote if needed.

24 MO
Archive Consideration

Proof older than 24 months moved to archive unless still highly relevant. May be used with date context but not as primary proof.

Exception Process: Credentials (certifications, awards) may have different lifecycles based on issuer policies. Media mentions become historical reference and should be dated but not archived if from reputable sources.

Legal Requirements

Compliance and Protection Standards

All proof in the library must meet legal standards to protect both ScaleUp Ventures and our clients. Non-compliance can result in legal liability and reputational damage.

Required Documentation Checklist

  • Client Release Form: Signed permission to use company name, logo, and results in marketing materials. Must specify allowed channels (web, print, video, social).
  • Testimonial Authorization: Written approval from the quoted individual with their current title. Re-authorization required if they change roles.
  • Data Accuracy Verification: Source documentation for all statistics. Keep calculation methodology and raw data on file.
  • NDA Review: Confirm no NDA restrictions prevent disclosure. Some engagements require anonymization.
  • Trademark Compliance: Proper usage of client logos per their brand guidelines. Written permission for logo usage.
  • Video/Photo Releases: Model releases for any individuals appearing in video testimonials or photography.

Accuracy Standards

  • All statistics must be verifiable with source documentation
  • Results must be representative, not cherry-picked outliers
  • Context must be provided where outcomes depend on specific circumstances
  • Aggregated data must include sample size and date range
  • Projections must be clearly labeled as estimates, not guaranteed outcomes

Anonymization Protocol

When client permission is restricted, use anonymization:

  • Replace company name with descriptor: "A Series B SaaS company"
  • Remove identifying details while preserving outcome specifics
  • Do not use anonymized case studies as primary proof in final sales stages

Team Access and Update Process

Role-Based Access Control

Different team members have different permissions to ensure proof integrity while enabling appropriate access.

Role View Proof Use in Materials Submit New Proof Approve Proof Archive/Delete
Executive Team Yes Yes Yes Yes Yes
Marketing Lead Yes Yes Yes Yes Limited
Sales Team Yes Yes Yes No No
Consulting Team Yes With approval Yes No No
External Partners Selected only With approval No No No

Update Process Workflow

  1. Submission: Team member completes proof submission template (Section 5)
  2. Initial Review: Marketing reviews for quality standards (within 3 business days)
  3. Verification: Engagement lead confirms accuracy and approves representation
  4. Legal Check: For new clients, verify release forms are in place
  5. Approval: Marketing Lead or Executive approves for library inclusion
  6. Publication: Proof added to library with all tags and metadata
  7. Notification: Team notified of new proof availability via Slack

Quarterly Review: Marketing leads a quarterly proof library review to identify gaps, retire outdated proof, and prioritize new proof collection based on sales needs.
