1Primary Proposal Footer CTA

The primary footer CTA is the final action prompt in every proposal. It must validate the reader's caution while providing a low-commitment path forward.

Review Your ROI Model Before Deciding

We've built a custom projection based on your specific numbers. No commitment required to examine the data together.

Schedule Your ROI Review

Why This CTA Works for Skeptical Owners

  • "Review" implies examination, not commitment - appeals to analytical mindset
  • "Your ROI Model" personalizes the data - it's already theirs to examine
  • "Before Deciding" respects their authority over the decision timeline
  • "No commitment required" explicitly removes pressure they expect
  • "Examine the data together" frames next step as collaborative analysis

Skeptic-Specific Insight: Business owners aged 30-55 have seen countless "urgent" offers. Only use high-urgency language when there's a genuine, verifiable deadline they can confirm.

5Follow-up Action Prompts

Structured follow-up sequences that maintain professionalism without appearing desperate. Each prompt provides an excuse for re-engagement.

Day 3 After Proposal
The Check-In
"I wanted to confirm the proposal arrived and offer to clarify any questions before your review. No pressure - just here if you need context on any section."
Day 7 After Proposal
The Value-Add
"Found this industry report that relates to the [specific challenge] we discussed. Thought it might be useful for your planning regardless of direction."
Day 14 After Proposal
The Direct Question
"It's been two weeks since we sent the proposal. Are you still evaluating, or has the timing changed? Either way, I'd rather know than guess."
Day 21 After Proposal
The Alternative Offer
"If the full engagement feels like too much right now, we can start with just the [smaller piece]. Often that's a better way to test the relationship."
Day 30 After Proposal
The Clean Break
"I'm archiving open proposals this week. Before I close yours, wanted to check if there's any chance we'll move forward this quarter - or if I should follow up next year instead."
Day 60+ After Proposal
The Reason to Reconnect
"We've just completed a project for [similar company type] that achieved [specific result]. Reminded me of our conversation. Worth revisiting if your situation has changed."
Follow-up Philosophy: Skeptical business owners respect directness. Each follow-up should provide genuine value or ask a clear question. Never use guilt ("Just following up again...") or artificial urgency ("Last chance...").

6Risk-Reduction Language for Closing

Explicitly address the risks skeptical owners calculate in their heads. Name them before they do.

Core Risk-Reduction Statements

Our Guarantee to You

"If we don't deliver the measurable outcomes specified in this proposal within the agreed timeline, we continue working at no additional cost until we do. If after good-faith effort the goals remain unmet, we'll refund the difference between value delivered and fees paid."

Written Into Every Agreement

Risk Statements by Concern Type

Their Fear Our Risk-Reduction Statement
"This will cost more than quoted" "This is a fixed-fee engagement. The number in this proposal is the number you pay - no hourly overages, no surprise charges, no scope creep billing."
"It won't work for my business" "We'll complete a 30-day pilot before full commitment. If the approach doesn't translate to your operation, you owe nothing beyond the pilot."
"My team will hate it" "Implementation includes staff onboarding and change management support. If adoption fails, we adjust the approach - included in the original scope."
"I'll be locked in" "You can pause or exit with 30 days notice. No termination penalties, no guilt trips, no bridges burned."
"The results will be theoretical" "Every projection is based on your actual data. We'll show our math, and you can verify every assumption against your own records."
"I'll lose control" "You approve every major decision before action. We recommend - you decide. Final authority never leaves your desk."

That's precisely why we've structured this engagement with protections you won't find in typical proposals: fixed pricing, written performance guarantees, and exit flexibility. The only risk is delaying improvements while your competitors act."

9Post-Signature P.S. Messages

The P.S. is the second-most-read element (after headlines). Use it strategically for final persuasion or urgency.

P.S. by Strategic Purpose

Urgency
P.S. If you're evaluating multiple options, consider this: every week of delay costs approximately $[X] in unrealized savings. The math is in section 3 if you want to verify.
Social Proof
P.S. Since sending you this proposal, we've received three referrals from [similar company type]. Happy to share what they're achieving if it helps your decision.
Guarantee Reminder
P.S. Remember: if we don't deliver the results outlined above, we keep working until we do - at no additional cost. That's not marketing language; it's written into the agreement.
Personal Touch
P.S. I know you've built this business through careful decisions, not impulsive ones. Take the time you need. When you're ready to move forward, we'll be here.
Soft Alternative
P.S. If the full engagement feels like too much right now, we can start with just the diagnostic phase. Often that's the best way to test whether we're the right fit.
Reference Offer
P.S. Still unsure? I'll connect you with three owners who've done exactly this engagement. Hear it from them, not from us.
Risk Reversal
P.S. The only thing you risk by saying yes is discovering exactly what's possible. The only thing you risk by saying no is never finding out.
P.S. Guidelines: Use maximum one P.S. per proposal. More than one dilutes impact. Choose the message that addresses the specific prospect's most likely hesitation.

10How Footer CTAs Differ from Body CTAs

Footer CTAs serve a different purpose than body CTAs. Understanding the distinction improves conversion.

Aspect Body CTAs Footer CTAs
Purpose Move to next section or specific action Close the deal or define next step
Reader State Still evaluating, gathering information Has all information, making decision
Commitment Level Low - "Learn more," "See details" High - "Approve," "Schedule," "Sign"
Tone Educational, inviting Decisive, summarizing
Length Short, action-focused Can be longer with supporting context
Alternatives Usually none - single path forward Often include fallback options
Urgency Rarely urgent May include deadline or timing context

Key Distinctions for Skeptical Audiences

  • Body CTAs should never ask for commitment. Skeptics need to complete their evaluation before being asked to decide.
  • Footer CTAs can be more direct because the reader has seen all the evidence - they're ready for a clear ask.
  • Body CTAs invite exploration; Footer CTAs invite action. Confusing these creates friction.
  • Footer CTAs should include an alternative for those not ready, preventing complete abandonment.

12Print vs Digital Footer Considerations

Footer design must adapt to the medium. What works on screen fails on paper, and vice versa.

Print Proposals

  • Include phone number prominently - print readers often call rather than email
  • Use QR codes for calendar links and reference request forms
  • Design signature block for actual physical signature (adequate space, clear lines)
  • Avoid colored backgrounds that won't reproduce well on copiers
  • Footer CTA should work without hyperlinks - include full URLs if necessary
  • Consider perforated reply card for easy response
  • P.S. works exceptionally well in print - handwritten-style fonts can enhance impact

Digital Proposals

  • Use clickable buttons - visual prominence matters for conversion
  • Include hover states and visual feedback on interactive elements
  • Link directly to scheduling tools (Calendly, etc.) - reduce friction
  • E-signature integration should be seamless (DocuSign, HelloSign)
  • Mobile optimization is critical - 40%+ may read on phones
  • Track engagement: which sections are read, where they stop
  • Consider video message option alongside text CTA
