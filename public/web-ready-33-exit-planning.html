The Challenge

Most Owners Wait Too Long to Prepare for Exit

After decades building your business, the way you exit defines your legacy. Yet most owners make critical mistakes that cost them millions.

The Timing Trap

Most owners start thinking about exit 6-12 months before they want to sell. By then, it's too late to implement the changes that dramatically increase valuation. Buyers can see the rush.

Key
Preparation takes longer than expected

The Valuation Reality

Business value isn't based on past performance alone - it's based on demonstrable future growth potential. Without proof of scalable systems, buyers discount heavily or walk away entirely.

Key
Documentation drives buyer confidence

The Proof Problem

Sophisticated buyers want evidence: documented processes, repeatable customer acquisition, and clear ROI projections. We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you. Most owner-operators have this knowledge in their heads - not on paper.

Key
Written systems enable ownership transfer

Exit Readiness

The Exit-Ready Checklist

Ten factors that sophisticated buyers evaluate - and that directly impact your valuation multiple.

1

Documented Revenue Systems

Written sales processes, scripts, and playbooks that any competent team could execute.

2

Diversified Customer Base

No single customer represents more than 10% of revenue. Concentration is a major red flag.

3

Recurring Revenue Streams

Predictable monthly or annual contracts command 2-3x higher multiples than project-based work.

4

Management Independence

A leadership team that can run operations without daily owner involvement.

5

Proven Growth Trajectory

3+ years of consistent revenue growth with documented strategies that drove results.

6

Clean Financial Records

GAAP-compliant financials, separated personal expenses, clear add-back documentation.

7

Scalable Operations

Systems that can handle 2-3x current volume without proportional cost increases.

8

Protected Intellectual Property

Trademarks, patents, proprietary processes, and documented trade secrets.

9

Strong Digital Presence

Consistent lead flow from owned channels - website, email list, search rankings.

10

Customer Retention Metrics

Documented retention rates, NPS scores, and proof of customer satisfaction.

Your Exit Timeline

The 3-Year Exit Preparation Plan

Start early. The businesses that command premium valuations begin preparing years before going to market.

3 Years Out

Build the Foundation

  • Document all core business processes and systems
  • Begin transitioning key relationships to team members
  • Implement Two-Track ROI measurement (guaranteed leads for ads, three-scenario projections for development)
  • Clean up financials and separate personal expenses
2 Years Out

Accelerate Growth

  • Launch documented lead generation campaigns
  • Diversify customer base and reduce concentration risk
  • Develop recurring revenue streams where possible
  • Strengthen management team capabilities
1 Year Out

Prepare for Market

  • Compile proof of all growth initiatives with documented ROI (cash returns and asset value increases)
  • Test business operations without daily owner involvement
  • Prepare Confidential Information Memorandum
  • Engage M&A advisor and begin buyer outreach
