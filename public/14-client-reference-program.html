Program Overview and Purpose

Why we built a formal client reference program and what it means for our business development

The ScaleUp Ventures Difference

While competitors offer carefully curated testimonials and case study quotes, we provide something far more valuable: direct access to our current clients. Prospects can ask anything. Clients can answer honestly. No scripts. No filtering. Just truth.

Program Philosophy

The Client Reference Program is built on a simple premise: confident clients create confident prospects. When a potential client speaks directly with someone who has worked with us, they gain insights no marketing material can provide. They hear about our real working style, how we handle challenges, and whether we deliver on our promises.

Strategic Objectives

  • Accelerate sales cycles by providing immediate proof of capability and character
  • Differentiate from competitors who rely on curated, controlled messaging
  • Demonstrate confidence in our work by inviting scrutiny
  • Deepen client relationships by recognizing them as advocates and partners
  • Create accountability by knowing every client could be a reference

The "Any Client, Any Question" Promise

We do not pre-select which clients prospects can speak with. We offer access to our entire active client roster. This radical transparency is possible only when you are genuinely confident in every client relationship you have.

Program Scope

This program covers:

  • Client enrollment and consent processes
  • Reference request handling and coordination
  • Client briefing and preparation
  • Recognition and appreciation for participating clients
  • Tracking and analytics
  • Legal protections for all parties

Client Enrollment Process

How we invite clients to participate and formalize their involvement

Enrollment Timeline

Clients become eligible for the reference program after reaching specific milestones in our engagement:

1

Engagement Milestone (Month 3)

After three months of active work together, assess relationship health and initial results. Client should have experienced our working style, communication patterns, and problem-solving approach.

2

Informal Conversation

During a regular check-in, introduce the concept: "We have a reference program where satisfied clients speak with prospective clients. Would you be open to that?" Gauge interest before formal invitation.

3

Formal Invitation

Send the official enrollment invitation (template below) with clear explanation of what participation involves, how often they might be contacted, and what appreciation they will receive.

4

Consent Documentation

Collect signed Reference Participation Agreement (see Legal section) and any specific preferences or restrictions the client has.

5

Onboarding to Program

Add client to reference database with preferences, availability, and any talking points they are particularly qualified to speak about. Send welcome package.

Enrollment Criteria

Not every client is appropriate for the reference program. Consider these factors:

Ideal Reference Clients

  • Achieved measurable results they can speak to specifically
  • Experienced multiple phases of our engagement (not just initial work)
  • Comfortable speaking candidly about challenges as well as successes
  • Have appropriate authority to represent their organization
  • Not under NDA restrictions that would limit what they can discuss
  • Represent industries or use cases relevant to typical prospects

Enrollment Conversation Script

"I have a slightly different request for you today. We do something unusual at ScaleUp Ventures, we offer direct references to prospective clients. Not curated testimonials, but actual conversations. It is a big part of how we differentiate ourselves. Based on our work together, I would love to have you as part of that program. Would you be open to occasionally taking a 20-minute call with someone considering working with us? We typically ask our reference clients to take no more than one or two calls per quarter."

Briefing Guide for Reference Clients

How to prepare clients for successful reference conversations

The Briefing Philosophy

We do not script our reference clients. We do not tell them what to say or avoid. But we do prepare them to have a productive, comfortable conversation. Good briefing is about context, not control.

Pre-Call Briefing Checklist

  • Prospect Background Share who they will be speaking with, their role, their company, and their industry
  • Engagement Context Explain what the prospect is considering hiring us for so the reference can draw relevant parallels
  • Specific Concerns If the prospect has expressed particular worries or questions, share them so the reference can address directly
  • Time Expectation Confirm the expected duration (typically 15-20 minutes)
  • Candor Permission Explicitly remind them they are encouraged to share challenges and areas for improvement

Briefing Document Template

Reference Call Brief

Prospect: [Name], [Title] at [Company]

Industry: [Industry/Sector]

Company Size: [Employees/Revenue range]

Considering: [Type of engagement they are exploring]

Their Main Questions:

  • [Question 1]
  • [Question 2]
  • [Question 3]

Your Relevant Experience: [Why you are a good match for this call]

Call Duration: 15-20 minutes

What to Tell Reference Clients

Encourage Them To:

  • Share their honest experience, including early challenges
  • Describe specific examples and outcomes when possible
  • Explain what surprised them (positively or negatively)
  • Mention what they wish they had known before starting
  • Answer questions they feel comfortable with and redirect others

Remind Them They Can:

  • Decline to answer specific questions that feel inappropriate
  • Say "I do not know" or "That was not part of my experience"
  • Keep the conversation focused on their direct experience
  • End the call if it runs long or feels uncomfortable

Confidentiality Reminder

Brief clients not to share confidential business metrics, trade secrets, or information that could identify other clients they may have heard about through their work with us.

Questions Prospects Typically Ask References

Prepare reference clients for the most common inquiries

Understanding typical questions helps reference clients prepare thoughtful responses. Share this list during onboarding so they can reflect on their answers in advance.

Working Relationship
"What is it actually like to work with them day-to-day?"
Prospects want to understand communication style, responsiveness, and collaboration dynamics.
Results and Outcomes
"What specific results have you seen?"
Looking for concrete evidence of value delivered, ideally with metrics.
Challenges
"Were there any challenges or things that did not go as expected?"
Testing for honesty and wanting to understand realistic expectations.
Decision Validation
"Knowing what you know now, would you hire them again?"
The ultimate question, seeking a direct endorsement or honest hesitation.
Problem Resolution
"How do they handle it when something goes wrong?"
Character and professionalism under pressure are key concerns.
Differentiation
"What makes them different from other firms you have worked with?"
Seeking authentic differentiation from someone with comparative experience.
Onboarding
"How was the onboarding process? How quickly did they get up to speed?"
Understanding the ramp-up investment required.
Communication
"How often do you communicate? Do you feel informed?"
Frequency and quality of updates matter for client peace of mind.
Value and Investment
"Do you feel like you are getting good value for what you pay?"
ROI validation from someone who writes the checks.
Improvement Areas
"What is one thing you wish they did differently?"
A constructive way to surface honest feedback.
Scope and Flexibility
"Have they been flexible when your needs changed?"
Adaptability and responsiveness to evolving requirements.
Strategic Value
"Do they just execute, or do they contribute strategic ideas?"
Understanding whether they add value beyond deliverables.

Preparing for Hard Questions

The most valuable reference calls include honest discussion of challenges. Coach clients that sharing a difficulty we worked through together often builds more trust than presenting a perfect picture. "We had a rocky start because of X, but they addressed it by Y, and now Z" is more credible than "Everything was perfect."

How to Prepare Clients to Be References

Setting reference clients up for comfortable, productive conversations

Initial Preparation Session

When a client joins the reference program, schedule a 30-minute preparation session covering:

1

Reflect on the Journey

Walk through the engagement together. What were the initial challenges? What surprised them? What results did they see? This helps them organize their thoughts and remember specifics they might otherwise forget.

2

Identify Signature Stories

Help them identify 2-3 specific anecdotes that illustrate your working relationship. Stories are more memorable and credible than general impressions.

3

Practice Common Questions

Review the typical questions list and discuss how they might answer each. Not to script, but to help them feel prepared and confident.

4

Discuss Boundaries

Clarify what they are comfortable discussing and what is off-limits. Ensure they know it is acceptable to redirect or decline questions.

5

Set Expectations

Explain the typical call format, duration, and how often they might be contacted. Emphasize that we will always check availability first.

Reference Client Preparation One-Pager

Your Role as a Reference

You are not a salesperson. You are a peer sharing your authentic experience. Here is what makes reference calls valuable:

  • Be specific: "They delivered the project two weeks early" beats "They are really good"
  • Be honest: Sharing a challenge we overcame together builds trust
  • Be yourself: Prospects can tell when someone is reading from a script
  • Be comfortable: Only answer what you feel appropriate answering
  • Be helpful: You are helping a peer make a good decision

Talking Points Framework

Help reference clients organize their thoughts around these themes:

Theme What to Think About Example Response
The Beginning Why did you hire us? What was the initial experience? "We were struggling with X. They came in and immediately..."
The Working Relationship Day-to-day collaboration, communication, responsiveness "I never have to chase them. They are proactive about..."
The Results Specific outcomes, metrics, achievements "In six months, we saw a 40% improvement in..."
The Challenges Any difficulties and how they were handled "Early on, there was a miscommunication about X. They owned it and..."
The Verdict Would you hire again? What would you tell a peer? "Absolutely. If you value X and need Y, they are the right choice."

Incentive and Appreciation Structure

Recognizing and thanking clients who advocate on our behalf

Philosophy on Incentives

We do not pay for references. Paid testimonials undermine the authenticity that makes our program valuable. Instead, we show appreciation through recognition, access, and partnership benefits that reinforce the relationship without creating a transactional dynamic.

Appreciation Tiers

Foundation
Reference Partner
1-2 reference calls per year
  • Personal thank-you note after each call
  • Recognition in quarterly partner newsletter
  • Annual appreciation gift (high-quality branded item)
  • Priority response on support requests
Distinguished
Reference Advocate
3-5 reference calls per year
  • All Foundation tier benefits
  • Exclusive preview access to new services
  • Invitation to annual client advisory dinner
  • Feature opportunity in case study (with approval)
  • Complimentary strategy session annually
Elite
Reference Ambassador
6+ reference calls per year
  • All Advocate tier benefits
  • Speaking opportunity at annual conference
  • Executive access and direct line to leadership
  • Custom appreciation experience (dinner, event, etc.)
  • First access to strategic partnership opportunities
  • Co-branded thought leadership content option

After Each Reference Call

  • Same day: Personal thank-you email from the person who requested the reference
  • Within one week: Handwritten note mailed to their office
  • If deal closes: Special acknowledgment and update on the outcome

Annual Appreciation

  • Year-end summary of their contribution to our growth
  • Invitation to exclusive appreciation event or experience
  • Recognition certificate or plaque for significant contributors
  • LinkedIn recommendation or endorsement offer (reciprocal relationship building)

What We Never Do

We never offer cash payments, discounts contingent on reference calls, or any incentive that could make the reference feel obligated or compromise their candor. The value of our program depends on unquestionable authenticity.

Reference Call Scheduling Process

Coordinating reference conversations efficiently while respecting everyone's time

Complete Scheduling Workflow

1

Prospect Requests Reference

Prospect indicates interest in speaking with a current client. Understand their specific questions and concerns to match with the right reference.

2

Select Appropriate Reference

Match prospect needs with a reference client who has relevant experience. Consider industry, engagement type, challenges faced, and recency of reference calls to avoid over-burdening any single client.

3

Request Availability

Contact reference client with context about the prospect and request their availability. Always ask, never assume. Include the expected time commitment (15-20 minutes).

4

Confirm and Brief

Once reference confirms, send briefing document with prospect details. Simultaneously, brief the prospect on who they will be speaking with.

5

Make Introduction

Send introduction email connecting both parties. Let them schedule directly, or offer to coordinate if either party prefers. Provide calendar scheduling link if available.

6

Confirm Completion

Follow up with both parties after the scheduled time to confirm the call happened and gather initial feedback.

Scheduling Best Practices

Timing Guidelines

  • Request availability at least 3-5 business days in advance
  • Suggest calls during business hours unless both parties prefer otherwise
  • Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons when possible
  • If urgent, offer to compensate reference's time more generously

Reference Rotation Protocol

To prevent reference fatigue and maintain enthusiasm, follow these rotation guidelines:

Reference Volume Minimum Rest Period Maximum per Quarter
After each call 2 weeks before next request 3 calls
After 3 calls in one quarter 4 weeks before next request 5 calls
After 6 calls in one year Requires explicit re-confirmation of willingness 8 calls

Handling Scheduling Conflicts

  • Reference unavailable: Thank them, note in system, move to backup reference
  • No response in 48 hours: Follow up once, then move to backup
  • Last-minute cancellation: Apologize to prospect, reschedule with same or alternative reference
  • Prospect no-show: Apologize to reference, note in prospect record, still send thank-you

Post-Reference Follow-Up

What happens after the reference call concludes

Immediate Follow-Up (Same Day)

A

Thank the Reference Client

Send personalized thank-you email within hours of the call. Acknowledge their time investment and contribution to our business development.

B

Check In with Prospect

Send brief email asking how the conversation went. What resonated? Any remaining questions? Any concerns that surfaced?

Prospect Follow-Up Questions

Post-Reference Check-In

Subject: How did your call with [Client Name] go? Hi [Prospect Name], Hope your conversation with [Client Name] was valuable. I am curious to hear your takeaways. A few questions, if you have a moment: - What stood out most from the conversation? - Did any new questions come up that I can help address? - How are you feeling about moving forward? Happy to discuss any of this on a quick call if helpful. Best, [Your Name]

Reference Client Feedback Collection

Periodically (after every 2-3 calls), gather feedback from reference clients:

  • How did the conversation go?
  • Were there any questions that were difficult to answer?
  • Did the prospect seem well-prepared?
  • Any suggestions for how we can improve the process?
  • Are you still comfortable participating in the program?

Closing the Loop

When the Deal Closes

Always inform reference clients of the outcome:

  • If won: Share the good news. Thank them for their contribution. Let them know their call was part of why the prospect chose us.
  • If lost: Thank them anyway. Confirm that their call was valuable regardless of outcome. Share any relevant feedback (without compromising confidentiality).

Document Everything

After each reference call, update the tracking system with:

  • Date and duration of call
  • Prospect feedback and key takeaways
  • Reference client feedback (if provided)
  • Any issues or notable moments
  • Follow-up actions required
  • Final deal outcome when known

Legal Considerations and Client Consent

Protecting all parties through proper documentation and clear agreements

Important Legal Notice

The templates and guidance in this section are for informational purposes only and do not constitute legal advice. Consult with qualified legal counsel before implementing any consent agreements or processes.

Key Legal Considerations

1. Informed Consent

Reference clients must provide explicit, documented consent before participating. This includes understanding:

  • What information they may be asked to share
  • How often they may be contacted
  • Their right to decline any specific request
  • Their right to withdraw from the program at any time
  • How their participation may be referenced in marketing

2. Confidentiality Boundaries

Reference clients must understand they should NOT share:

  • Proprietary business information or trade secrets
  • Specific financial terms of their engagement with us
  • Information about other clients they may have learned through us
  • Details protected by their own company's confidentiality policies

3. Liability Considerations

Address potential liability issues:

  • Reference clients speak from personal experience and opinion, not as our agents
  • We do not guarantee any specific outcome based on reference conversations
  • Prospects should conduct their own due diligence beyond references
  • We are not liable for opinions expressed by reference clients

4. Data Protection Compliance

Ensure compliance with applicable data protection regulations:

  • Store reference client contact information securely
  • Use information only for stated reference program purposes
  • Honor requests to update or delete personal information
  • Maintain records of consent and any preference changes
