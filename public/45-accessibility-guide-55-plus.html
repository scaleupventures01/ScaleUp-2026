Why Accessibility Matters for This Audience

Business owners in the 30-55 age range are at the peak of their professional power but are also experiencing natural age-related changes that affect how they interact with digital products. Understanding these changes is the foundation of good design.

Common Age-Related Changes

Change Impact on Users Design Response
Presbyopia Difficulty focusing on near objects; need for reading glasses Larger text, adjustable sizing
Reduced Contrast Sensitivity Harder to distinguish similar colors and low-contrast text High contrast ratios, clear boundaries
Slower Processing Speed Need more time to process information and make decisions Generous timeouts, clear confirmations
Reduced Fine Motor Control Less precise tapping and clicking, especially on mobile Larger touch targets, spacing between clickables
Working Memory Changes Harder to hold multiple pieces of information at once Progressive disclosure, clear progress indicators
Business Reality

Our audience controls significant assets and makes major business decisions. Poor accessibility does not just frustrate them; it signals that we do not understand or respect their needs. This directly impacts trust and conversion.

Minimum Font Sizes

Typography size is the most critical accessibility factor for our audience. These are non-negotiable minimums for all ScaleUp Ventures digital properties.

Body Text

18px minimum

All paragraph text, descriptions, and general content. 20px preferred for longer reading.

Headlines (H1)

36-48px

Page titles and primary headlines. Must be clearly distinguishable from body text.

Subheadings (H2-H3)

24-32px

Section headers and content divisions. Clear hierarchy is essential.

Navigation Links

16px minimum

Menu items and navigation elements. Combined with adequate spacing.

Button Text

16px minimum

All button labels and calls to action. Font weight of 500 or higher.

Form Labels

16px minimum

Input labels and field descriptions. Bold weight for labels.

Element Minimum Size Preferred Size Font Family
Page Title (H1) 36px 48px Crimson Pro
Section Header (H2) 28px 32px Crimson Pro
Subsection (H3) 22px 26px Crimson Pro
Body Text 18px 20px DM Sans
Small Text / Captions 14px 16px DM Sans
Never Go Below 14px

Any text smaller than 14px becomes essentially unreadable for users with presbyopia, even with reading glasses. If something feels like it needs to be smaller than 14px, reconsider whether it needs to be shown at all.

Line Height and Spacing Requirements

Generous spacing dramatically improves readability for all users, but especially for those with age-related vision changes. Cramped text causes eye strain and increases cognitive load.

Line Height Specifications

Text Type Minimum Line Height Calculation
Body Text 1.6 - 1.8 18px text = 29-32px line height
Headlines 1.2 - 1.3 48px text = 58-62px line height
Lists 1.5 - 1.6 With additional spacing between items

Paragraph and Element Spacing

Between Paragraphs

20-28px

Clear visual separation between text blocks aids scanning and comprehension.

Section Spacing

48-64px

Major content sections need clear breathing room to establish hierarchy.

List Item Spacing

12-16px

Each list item should be clearly distinct from neighbors.

Line Length

60-80 characters

Optimal line length for comfortable reading without losing place.

The 50% Rule

When in doubt, add 50% more spacing than you think you need. What looks "clean and tight" to a 25-year-old designer often looks cramped and overwhelming to a 55-year-old reader.

Touch Target Sizes for Mobile

Reduced fine motor control and slightly less precise touch make larger tap targets essential. Small buttons are the number one frustration for 55+ mobile users.

Touch Target Requirements

Element Minimum Size Minimum Spacing
Primary Buttons (CTA) 48x48px 16px between buttons
Secondary Buttons 44x44px 12px between buttons
Navigation Links 44x44px 8px minimum between items
Form Checkboxes/Radios 24x24px (visual) + padding Clickable area 44x44px

The Spacing Is As Important As the Size

A 48px button next to another 48px button with only 4px gap will still cause mis-taps. Adequate spacing between touch targets is essential to prevent accidental activation of the wrong element.

Link Styling for Visibility

Links must be identifiable through more than just color. Users with reduced color perception need additional visual cues to distinguish links from regular text.

Avoid: Color-Only Links

Learn more about our services and investment approach for business owners looking to scale.

Correct: Underlined + Color

Learn more about our services and investment approach for business owners looking to scale.

Link Styling Requirements

State Visual Treatment Purpose
Default Blue (#0066CC) + underline (2px) Clearly distinguishable from body text
Hover Darker blue (#004499) + thicker underline (3px) Confirms interactivity on desktop
Focus Visible focus ring (3px solid, offset 2px) Keyboard navigation indicator
Visited Purple (#551A8B) + underline Indicates previously clicked links

✓ Do

  • Always underline links in body text
  • Use descriptive link text ("Read the full report")
  • Maintain consistent link styling across the site
  • Ensure links have visible focus states
  • Make link text meaningful out of context

✗ Don't

  • Rely only on color to indicate links
  • Use "click here" or "learn more" alone
  • Remove underlines for "cleaner" design
  • Use colors too similar to body text
  • Create links that look like buttons (or vice versa)

Form Field Design Guidelines

Forms are high-stakes interactions. Poor form design leads to errors, frustration, and abandonment. Our audience expects forms that respect their time and cognitive load.

We will use this to send your account information
Format: (555) 555-5555
!
Please enter a complete phone number with area code, for example: (555) 555-5555

Form Design Requirements

Element Requirement
Labels Always visible (no placeholder-only labels). 16px minimum, bold weight.
Input Fields 48px minimum height. 16px+ text. 2px+ border. High contrast border.
Placeholder Text Show format examples, not labels. Use gray that passes contrast.
Helper Text Position below label, above input. Explain format requirements upfront.
Focus States Visible focus ring (3px+). Color change on border. No subtle-only indicators.
Field Spacing 24px minimum between form groups. Clear visual grouping.
One Column, Always

Multi-column form layouts cause cognitive overhead and increase error rates for all users. For our 55+ audience, always use single-column forms with clear top-to-bottom progression.

8

Error Message Clarity

Error messages must be immediate, specific, and helpful. Vague errors waste time and increase frustration. Our audience expects professional, respectful communication even when something goes wrong.

Error Message Requirements

✓ Good Error Messages

  • "Please enter your email address in this format: name@company.com"
  • "Your password must include at least 8 characters. Add 2 more characters."
  • "We could not find an account with that email. Check the spelling or create a new account."
  • "The phone number needs an area code. Example: (555) 555-5555"

✗ Bad Error Messages

  • "Invalid input"
  • "Error: Please try again"
  • "Field required"
  • "Format incorrect"
  • "Something went wrong"

Error Display Guidelines

Aspect Requirement
Timing Show errors immediately after field loses focus or on submit. Never on keystroke.
Position Directly below the problematic field. Never in a distant summary only.
Visual Red border on field + red text + error icon. Never color alone.
Content State what is wrong + how to fix it. Include example of correct format.
Tone Helpful, not blaming. "Please enter..." not "You forgot..."
Prevent Errors, Do Not Just Report Them

The best error handling is error prevention. Use input masks, real-time validation, clear format examples, and smart defaults to help users enter correct information the first time.

9

Navigation Simplicity

Complex navigation taxes working memory. Our audience values efficiency and directness. Navigation should make the site structure immediately clear with minimal cognitive overhead.

Navigation Principles

Maximum Items

5-7 items

Primary navigation should have no more than 7 top-level items. Use grouping for more.

Depth Limit

2 levels

Avoid deeply nested menus. Two levels maximum for dropdown navigation.

Current Location

Always visible

Users must always know where they are. Use breadcrumbs and active states.

Consistent Position

Fixed location

Navigation stays in the same place across all pages. No surprises.

Navigation Checklist

Requirement Implementation
Clear Labels Use plain language. "Our Services" not "Solutions Hub"
Visual Hierarchy Primary nav clearly distinguished from secondary and utility nav
Touch-Friendly Dropdowns Dropdowns work on tap (not just hover). Clear open/close indicators.
Breadcrumbs Show path on all pages deeper than home. Clickable links to each level.
Search Prominent search for sites with significant content. Large input field.
Skip Links "Skip to main content" link for keyboard users. Visible on focus.
Mobile Navigation

Hamburger menus are less intuitive for 55+ users who may not recognize the icon. Consider showing primary nav items directly on mobile when possible, or label the hamburger icon with "Menu" text.

Reading Level Recommendations

Clear, direct writing respects our audience's time and reduces cognitive load. This is not about "dumbing down" content; it is about communicating efficiently with busy professionals.

Reading Level Targets

Content Type Target Grade Level Notes
Marketing Copy 8th Grade Clear, compelling, jargon-free
Product/Service Descriptions 9th-10th Grade Accessible while maintaining professionalism
Technical/Legal Content 10th-12th Grade Necessary complexity with plain language summaries
UI Text/Microcopy 6th-8th Grade Action-oriented, extremely clear

Writing Examples

Before: Complex

Our comprehensive suite of strategic advisory services leverages decades of accumulated expertise to facilitate optimal value realization for stakeholders seeking to effectuate transformational liquidity events.

After: Clear

We help business owners get the best possible price when selling their company. Our advisors have helped hundreds of owners through successful sales.

Writing Guidelines

✓ Do

  • Use short sentences (15-20 words average)
  • Choose simple words over complex ones
  • Write in active voice
  • Break up long paragraphs
  • Use bullet points for lists
  • Define technical terms when first used

✗ Avoid

  • Jargon and buzzwords
  • Unnecessarily long sentences
  • Passive voice when active works
  • Dense walls of text
  • Acronyms without explanation
  • Unnecessarily formal language
Testing Readability

Use the Hemingway Editor (free online) to check reading level and highlight complex sentences. Aim for "Good" or better rating on most content.

Testing Checklist for 55+ Accessibility

Use this checklist before launching any page, feature, or campaign. Every item should pass. Click checkboxes to track your audit progress.

Complete Accessibility Audit Checklist

A Typography

  • Body text is 18px or larger
  • No text is smaller than 14px anywhere on the page
  • Line height is 1.6 or greater for body text
  • Line length does not exceed 80 characters
  • Clear visual hierarchy between heading levels
  • Sufficient spacing between paragraphs (20px+)

B Color and Contrast

  • Body text has 7:1 contrast ratio or better
  • Large text (24px+) has 4.5:1 contrast ratio or better
  • UI components have 4.5:1 contrast against background
  • Color is not the only means of conveying information
  • Focus indicators are clearly visible (3:1+ contrast)
  • Tested with color blindness simulator

C Touch and Interaction

  • All touch targets are 44x44px or larger
  • Primary CTAs are 48x48px or larger
  • Adequate spacing between clickable elements (8px+)
  • Hover effects also work on tap/focus
  • No functionality requires precise gestures (pinch, swipe)

D Links and Navigation

  • Links are underlined (not color-only)
  • Link text is descriptive (not "click here")
  • Current location is clearly indicated
  • Navigation has 7 or fewer primary items
  • Breadcrumbs present on deeper pages
  • Skip link available for keyboard users

E Forms and Errors

  • All form fields have visible labels (not placeholder-only)
  • Form inputs are 48px tall or larger
  • Error messages appear next to problematic field
  • Error messages explain how to fix the problem
  • Required fields are clearly marked
  • Form uses single-column layout

F Content and Language

  • Reading level is 10th grade or lower
  • Sentences average 15-20 words
  • Jargon and acronyms are explained
  • Key information is at the beginning (not buried)
  • Content uses bullet points and headers to break up text

G Testing Methods

  • Tested with screen zoomed to 200%
  • Tested with keyboard-only navigation
  • Tested on actual mobile device (not just emulator)
  • Tested with someone in target age range (30-55)
  • Run automated accessibility audit (WAVE, Lighthouse)

Common Accessibility Mistakes to Avoid

These are the most frequent accessibility failures we see in digital products targeting our demographic. Every one of these issues directly impacts user experience and business metrics.

1. Placeholder-Only Labels

Using placeholder text as the only label for form fields. When users start typing, they lose context about what the field is for.

2. Light Gray Text

Using gray text for "secondary" content that fails contrast requirements. If it is worth showing, it is worth making readable.

3. Tiny Touch Targets

Designing for pixel precision instead of finger-friendly interactions. Common in footer links, close buttons, and pagination.

4. Color-Only Indicators

Using red/green color alone to indicate success/error states. Users with color vision deficiency cannot distinguish these.

5. Removing Underlines from Links

Eliminating underlines for "cleaner" design makes links invisible to users who do not perceive subtle color differences.

6. Complex Navigation

Mega menus with dozens of options, multiple levels of nesting, and hover-triggered dropdowns that do not work on touch.

7. Vague Error Messages

"An error occurred" or "Invalid input" without explanation of what went wrong or how to fix it.

8. Auto-Playing Media

Videos or audio that start automatically, potentially startling users or causing confusion about the source of sound.

9. Session Timeouts Without Warning

Logging users out or losing form data without warning. Our audience may take more time to complete tasks.

10. PDF-Only Documents

Critical information locked in PDFs that may not be accessible or easy to read on mobile devices.

11. Missing Focus States

Removing outline styles for "cleaner" design makes keyboard navigation impossible to use.

12. Infinite Scroll

Continuous scrolling without pagination makes it impossible to return to a specific item or reach the footer.

The Cost of These Mistakes

Each accessibility failure has a measurable business impact: increased bounce rates, lower conversion rates, more support requests, and damaged brand perception. Fixing these issues is not just ethical; it is profitable.
