SECTION 01

Introduction to Brand Voice

The ScaleUp Ventures voice is the verbal expression of our brand identity. Like the Heron that watches before it strikes, our communication is characterized by patient observation, measured analysis, and decisive action. Every piece of content we create should embody these principles.

"Our voice is the bridge between our expertise and our clients' success. It must be as precise as our investment decisions."
ScaleUp Ventures Brand Philosophy

Why Voice Matters

In venture capital, trust is earned through consistency. Our voice creates that consistency across every touchpoint: from initial founder conversations to board presentations, from market analysis reports to public communications.

When founders hear our voice, they should immediately recognize the patient, analytical, and decisive partner they chose. When LPs read our reports, they should sense the same measured confidence that led them to invest with us.

The Foundational Principle

Our tagline "Certainty First" is not merely a marketing phrase—it is the philosophical foundation of everything we communicate. Every sentence should convey that we prioritize certainty: in our research, in our decisions, and in our commitments.

SECTION 02

The Three Core Attributes

Our voice is built on three interconnected attributes that mirror the Heron's hunting strategy: watch, measure, strike. These attributes work in harmony—each strengthening the others.

Watchful
Observing what others overlook

Watchful communication demonstrates that we see the full landscape. We reference context, acknowledge complexity, and show awareness of factors that others might miss. Our voice reveals the depth of our observation without being exhaustive or overwhelming.

This sounds watchful
"While the market celebrates this quarter's growth, we've observed three underlying patterns in customer acquisition costs that warrant closer attention." Shows awareness of what others miss
This does not sound watchful
"Great quarter! The numbers look amazing and we're excited about the momentum." Surface-level, misses deeper patterns
Measured
Precision over presumption

Measured communication is precise, proportionate, and grounded. We quantify when possible, qualify appropriately, and never overstate. Our voice reflects the careful analysis that precedes every conclusion. We earn trust through accuracy, not exaggeration.

This sounds measured
"Based on cohort analysis across 847 B2B SaaS companies, we anticipate enterprise revenue reaching $2.3M-$2.8M by Q4, contingent on current sales velocity." Specific, qualified, grounded in data
This does not sound measured
"We're confident this will be huge. The upside potential is absolutely massive and we expect explosive growth." Vague, hyperbolic, unqualified
Decisive
Conviction earned through rigor

Decisive communication conveys clear conviction without arrogance. When we've done the work—watched carefully and measured precisely—we state our conclusions directly. We don't hedge unnecessarily or dilute our perspective with excessive qualifiers. Our decisiveness is earned, not assumed.

This sounds decisive
"We're committing $8M to Series B. The market position is defensible, unit economics are proven, and the team has demonstrated execution capability in adjacent markets." Clear conviction with supporting rationale
This does not sound decisive
"We're perhaps somewhat inclined to maybe consider a potential investment, though there are some things we might want to think about further." Over-hedged, unclear position

SECTION 04

Grammar and Style Preferences

Consistent grammar and style choices reinforce our brand voice. The following preferences apply to all written content unless context requires deviation.

Oxford Comma

Always use the Oxford comma for clarity and precision. research, analysis, and investment

Contractions

Avoid in formal documents (memos, LP reports). Permitted in blog posts and social media for warmth.

Numbers

Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above. Always use numerals for money, percentages, and metrics.

Percentages

Use the % symbol with numerals: 47% not "forty-seven percent" or "47 percent."

Currency

Use $ with M/B abbreviations for large amounts: $2.4M, $150B. Spell out for emphasis in headlines.

Capitalization

Use sentence case for headlines and subheads. Reserve title case for proper nouns and formal document titles.

Ampersands

Avoid in body copy. Acceptable in headers, titles, and branded phrases only.

Abbreviations

Spell out on first use with abbreviation in parentheses. Use abbreviation thereafter: total addressable market (TAM)

SECTION 05

Sentence Structure Guidelines

Our sentence structure should reflect our voice attributes: watchful in its awareness, measured in its construction, and decisive in its delivery.

Guideline Example Use
Lead with the conclusion "We're investing. Here's why..." rather than building to the decision ✓
Vary sentence length deliberately Follow complex sentences with short, decisive statements. Brevity after depth creates impact. ✓
Use active voice predominantly "We identified three risks" not "Three risks were identified by the team" ✓
Front-load paragraphs with key points Topic sentence first, supporting details follow. Never bury the lead. ✓
Limit qualifiers per sentence One qualifier maximum. "Results suggest" not "Results might possibly suggest" ✓
Avoid stacking prepositional phrases Restructure: "the report on the analysis of the market" becomes "the market analysis report" ✗
Minimize passive constructions Avoid: "It was determined that..." Use: "We determined..." ✗
Eliminate filler phrases Remove: "In order to," "It is important to note that," "At this point in time" ✗
The Rhythm Principle

Our writing has a distinctive rhythm: observation, analysis, conclusion. Longer sentences explore context and nuance. Shorter sentences deliver verdicts. This pattern—expand, then contract—mirrors how we think and how we invest.

SECTION 06

Punctuation Preferences

Element Preference Notes
Em dashes Use for emphasis and parenthetical thoughts—no spaces around them. Conveys measured precision
Colons Use to introduce lists, explanations, or elaborations. Capitalize after if complete sentence follows. Decisive, direct
Semicolons Use sparingly to connect closely related independent clauses; never more than one per paragraph. Shows sophistication
Exclamation points Avoid in formal content. Maximum one per piece in casual content. Undermines measured voice
Ellipses Avoid. They suggest uncertainty, which conflicts with decisiveness. Use em dash instead
Quotation marks Double quotes for direct quotations. Single quotes for quotes within quotes. Punctuation inside. American English standard
Bullet points Use for lists of 3+ items. Maintain parallel structure. Period after complete sentences only. Enhances scannability
SECTION 07

Formatting Guidelines

Document Hierarchy

Headings

Use clear hierarchical structure. H1 for document title, H2 for major sections, H3 for subsections. Never skip levels.

Paragraph Length

3-5 sentences for body paragraphs. Single sentence paragraphs for emphasis. One idea per paragraph.

White Space

Generous margins and line spacing. White space signals confidence and clarity, not emptiness.

Bold and Italics

Bold for key terms and emphasis. Italics for publication names, introducing new terms, and subtle stress.

Data Presentation

Tables

Use for comparative data. Always include clear headers. Align numbers right, text left.

Charts

Prefer simplicity. One message per chart. Always title and label clearly. Use brand colors.

Key Metrics

Highlight with larger type or call-out boxes. Always provide context: "$4.2M ARR (up 127% YoY)"

Sources

Cite data sources. Footnotes for detailed sourcing, inline for critical context.

SECTION 08

Industry Terminology Usage

Our use of industry terminology should demonstrate expertise without creating barriers. We speak the language of venture capital fluently, but we never use jargon to obscure meaning.

Use Freely
  • ARR / MRR
  • Series A / B / C
  • TAM / SAM / SOM
  • Unit economics
  • CAC / LTV
  • Burn rate
  • Runway
  • Term sheet
Define on First Use
  • Net Dollar Retention
  • Cohort analysis
  • Gross margin profile
  • Capital efficiency
  • Payback period
  • Rule of 40
  • Magic number
  • Quick ratio
Avoid or Translate
  • "Crushing it"
  • "Killing it"
  • "Disruptive"
  • "Game-changing"
  • "Unicorn potential"
  • "Move fast and break things"
  • "Synergies"
  • "Pivot" (overused)
The Translation Principle

When communicating with non-VC audiences (founders early in their journey, LPs from non-tech backgrounds, media), translate terminology into plain language. Our watchfulness includes awareness of our audience's fluency level.

Before publishing or sending any content, verify alignment with our voice by completing this checklist. All items should be affirmative before content is approved.

Content Voice Verification
Complete before publishing any external content
Does the content demonstrate observation beyond surface level?
Are claims supported with specific data or evidence?
Is the conclusion or recommendation stated clearly and directly?
Have hyperbolic words and phrases been removed?
Is the voice intensity appropriate for this context?
Does sentence structure vary between observation and conclusion?
Are industry terms used appropriately for the audience?
Does the content avoid excessive hedging or qualifiers?
Is punctuation consistent with guidelines (no exclamation points in formal content)?
Does the content feel like it could only come from ScaleUp Ventures?

SECTION 10

Approval Process for Brand Content

All external communications representing ScaleUp Ventures must follow the appropriate approval pathway. This ensures consistency and protects brand integrity while enabling efficient content production.

1
Draft
Author creates content following these guidelines
2
Self-Review
Complete voice checklist verification
3
Peer Review
Second team member validates voice alignment
4
Final Approval
Designated approver signs off based on content tier

Content Approval Tiers

Content Type Required Approver Turnaround
LP Letters and Reports Managing Partner 5 business days
Investment Announcements Partner + Communications Lead 3 business days
Blog Posts and Thought Leadership Communications Lead 2 business days
Social Media Posts Author self-approval (trained staff) Same day
Press Releases Managing Partner + Legal 5 business days
Crisis Communications Managing Partner only Immediate review
Emergency Protocol

For time-sensitive communications requiring immediate response, contact the Managing Partner directly. If unavailable, the most senior available partner may approve with mandatory post-publication review within 24 hours.
