Our Approach & Methodology

Our assessment is a structured analysis designed to answer a specific question - usually some version of "Should we do this?" It's not a generic template or a sales pitch dressed up as analysis.

Depending on your situation, we examine:

  • Market validation: Is there real demand? Who else is serving this need? What would it take to compete?
  • Technical feasibility: Can this actually be built? What are the hard problems? What's the realistic timeline?
  • Business model analysis: Do the economics work? What assumptions need to be true for this to succeed?
  • Risk assessment: What could go wrong? What are the key dependencies? What's the downside?
  • Recommendation: Clear go/no-go guidance with reasoning you can evaluate.

The assessment is yours to keep, whether you continue with us or not. We want you making informed decisions.

We'll be direct: no projection is 100% accurate. Anyone who tells you otherwise is lying. That's why we use a Two-Track ROI model that's honest about what can and can't be guaranteed.

Track 1 - Ad Campaigns: We quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads BEFORE starting any campaign to ensure the expected results will meet your needs. If we miss our guaranteed lead numbers, we refund our fee (not your ad spend - that's media cost, not our work). This is measurable, trackable, and accountable.

Track 2 - App & Development Projects: We provide three-scenario ROI projections showing high, mid, and low outcomes. Returns are modeled in both cash terms AND company/asset value - because sometimes the win is equity appreciation, not immediate revenue.

For development projects, we build projections designed to be more reliable than typical estimates:

  • We research actual market data and comparable benchmarks
  • We account for competitive dynamics specific to your market
  • We build in conservative assumptions (we'd rather over-deliver than under-promise)
  • We identify the key variables that could change outcomes across all three scenarios

Our philosophy: We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you. We'd rather lose a fee than take on work where we can't demonstrate likely value.

Then we tell you that, and we explain why. That's exactly what you're paying for.

This isn't hypothetical - we've done it. A client approached us with a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment for a major build. Instead, we proposed a $5,000 discovery engagement (4 weeks). Our analysis revealed market saturation, hidden costs, and that 80% of what they needed was achievable with existing solutions. We saved them $150,000+ and 12+ months of wasted effort.

When we recommend "no," we provide:

  • A clear explanation of what doesn't work and why
  • What would need to change for this to become viable
  • Alternative approaches worth considering

We don't take clients when we can't help them succeed. That's not noble - it's practical. Unhappy clients are bad for everyone.

Our philosophy: "Certainty First" means we would rather earn $5,000 telling you the truth than $150,000 building something that will not serve you.

A typical assessment takes 2-4 weeks from kickoff to final delivery, depending on complexity.

Here's the general process:

  • Week 1: Kickoff conversation, context gathering, and preliminary research
  • Weeks 2-3: Deep analysis, validation work, and recommendation development
  • Week 3-4: Findings presentation and discussion

The timeline can extend if we're waiting on information from your end, or compress if your situation is straightforward.

We don't rush this process. A thorough analysis now prevents expensive mistakes later.

The specifics depend on what we're assessing, but generally we need context about your situation, your goals, and your constraints.

For a typical assessment:

  • Clear articulation of what you're trying to decide
  • Background on your business/situation
  • Any existing research or analysis you've done
  • Constraints (budget, timeline, technical, regulatory)
  • What success looks like for you

For technical assessments:

  • Access to relevant systems or documentation
  • Time with key stakeholders
  • Existing architecture/design materials

We'll guide you through exactly what we need during our kickoff conversation. We try to minimize the burden on your team.
