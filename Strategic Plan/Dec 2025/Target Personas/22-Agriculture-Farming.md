# Target Persona Analysis: Agriculture / Farming / Agribusiness

**Analysis Date**: December 30, 2025
**Analyst**: ScaleUp Ventures Research
**Objective**: Evaluate agriculture/farming industry as potential target for dev, growth marketing, and AI/automation services

---

## Executive Summary

The agriculture/farming industry presents a **MIXED opportunity** for ScaleUp Ventures. While the sector has ideal demographics (older owners, technology laggards) and massive market size, several structural challenges limit acquisition ease and fit with ScaleUp's services. The industry is better suited for **B2B SaaS and AgTech products** than for marketing/dev services targeting individual farm operators.

**Verdict**: MODERATE FIT - Consider targeting **agribusiness suppliers and agricultural service providers** rather than farms directly.

## Detailed Research Findings

### 1. Market Size & Structure

**Industry Revenue (2025)**:
- Gross Cash Farm Income (GCFI): $623.9 billion forecast
- Net Farm Income: $179.8 billion forecast (up 40.7% from 2024)
- Total Cash Receipts: $535.2 billion
- Agriculture, forestry, fishing & hunting industry: $586.5 billion

**Farm Count & Distribution**:
- Total U.S. farms (2024): 1,880,000 (down 14,950 from 2023)
- Small family farms (<$350K GCFI): 86% of farms, 17% of production value
- Midsize family farms ($350K-$999K): 6% of farms, 18% of value
- Large-scale family farms ($1M+): 4% of farms, 48% of value
- Average farm size: 466 acres

**Key Insight**: The market is highly bifurcated. Small farms (the majority) have limited budgets. Large farms (the money) are already served by enterprise solutions.

**Sources**: [USDA ERS - Farming and Farm Income](https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/ag-and-food-statistics-charting-the-essentials/farming-and-farm-income), [Statista - Agriculture Market](https://www.statista.com/outlook/io/agriculture/united-states)

---

### 2. Owner Demographics

**Age Profile**:
- Average age of all U.S. farm producers: **58.1 years** (up from 57.5 in 2017)
- Farmers 65 and older: **40%+ of all farmers** (increased 12% since 2017)
- Average age of beginning producers: 47.1 years
- Average farming experience: 23.4 years

**Age Distribution Trends**:
- 65+ farmers: Increased from 1.15M (2017) to 1.29M (2022) - 12% growth
- 35-64 farmers: Decreased by 9%
- Under 44 farmers: Increased by 7% (small positive trend)

**Regional Variation**: Farmers are generally younger in Midwest states and older in Southern states.

**Technology Comfort**:
- 75% of farms have internet access
- 73% have desktop/laptop computers
- 52% use smartphones/tablets for farm business (up 8pp since 2017)
- Computer usage for farm business: 49% (up from 20% in 1997)

**Key Insight**: Perfect demographic fit for "older, technology-laggard" target. However, this also means longer education cycles and slower adoption.

**Sources**: [USDA NASS - Farm Producers](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Publications/Highlights/2024/Census22_HL_FarmProducers_FINAL.pdf), [Fortune - Average Age of Farmers](https://fortune.com/2023/10/12/how-old-american-farmer-average-age-boomer-gen-x/)

---

### 3. Technology Adoption Rates

**Current Adoption Levels**:
- Guidance/autosteering: 70% of large farms, 52% of midsize farms
- Yield monitors/maps: 68% of large-scale farms
- Farm management software: 39% global adoption rate
- Variable rate technology (VRT): 5-25% of acreage (varies by crop)

**Adoption Barriers**:
- High cost/unaffordability: **61.62%** cite as barrier
- Lack of awareness: **71.59%**
- Lack of knowledge/training: **67.53%**
- High upfront costs: **52%** (North American farmers)
- Unclear ROI: **40%** (North American farmers)
- Telecom infrastructure issues: **52.4%**

**Key Trends**:
- Adoption increases sharply with farm size
- Younger farmers are 50% more likely to adopt new technology
- Peer influence is the strongest driver of adoption
- North America leads global adoption; Latin America fastest growing

**Digital Agriculture Market**:
- 2023 value: $24.1 billion
- 2032 projection: $71.96 billion
- CAGR: 13.2%

**Sources**: [USDA ERS - Precision Agriculture](https://www.ers.usda.gov/publications/pub-details?pubid=105893), [McKinsey - AgTech Adoption](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/agriculture/our-insights/agtech-breaking-down-the-farmer-adoption-dilemma)

---

### 4. Pain Points & Needs

**Top Challenges**:

| Pain Point | % Citing | Business Impact |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| High technology costs | 52-62% | Limits modernization |
| Lack of awareness/knowledge | 67-72% | Slows adoption |
| Labor shortages | High | Rising wages, productivity loss |
| Input cost inflation | High | Third-highest costs ever in 2024 |
| Unclear ROI on tech | 40% | Hesitancy to invest |
| Telecom/infrastructure | 52% | Rural connectivity gaps |
| Data ownership concerns | Moderate | Privacy/control fears |

**Disruption Fears**:
- Being outcompeted by tech-savvy operations
- Generational succession challenges
- Climate/weather volatility requiring better data
- Commodity price pressure requiring efficiency gains

**Unmet Needs**:
- Simple, affordable technology solutions
- Clear ROI demonstration before purchase
- Peer validation and case studies
- Training and ongoing support
- Integration between disparate systems

**Sources**: [BCG - 1000 Farmers on Tech Adoption](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2024/what-1000-farmers-told-us-about-tech-adoption), [GAO - Precision Agriculture](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-105962)

### 5. Marketing Spend & Budget

**Current State**:
- 60%+ of farms plan to boost digital marketing budgets (from low base)
- Shifting from traditional (print, radio, trade shows) to digital
- Top spending increases: Video advertising, data analytics, social media

**Digital Advertising Benchmarks (Agriculture)**:
- Average CPC: $2.46 (search), $0.44 (display), $0.82 (social)
- Average CPM: $2.77 (display), $5.39 (social), $24.45 (direct publisher)
- Lower competition = lower costs than other industries

**Budget Reality**:
- 48.1% of farms have <$10K annual sales
- 78.9% have <$100K annual sales
- Marketing is NOT a priority for most small farms
- Larger commercial operations have marketing budgets but use specialized ag agencies

**Key Insight**: Farms themselves are not big marketing spenders. The opportunity is with agricultural suppliers, equipment dealers, and agribusiness companies marketing TO farmers.

**Sources**: [Think Shift - Digital Advertising Benchmarks](https://thinkshiftinc.com/blog/2024-digital-advertising-benchmarks-for-agriculture), [Farm Progress - Marketing Trends](https://marketing.farmprogress.com/ag-marketing-trends-insights-survey-report/)

### 6. Competitive Landscape

**Specialized Agriculture Marketing Agencies**:
- **AdFarm** - Deep ag expertise, proprietary farm panel research
- **Rhea+Kaiser** - Full-service ag advertising, farmer/rural focus
- **Meyocks** - Agricultural advertising with agribusiness clients
- **Elevation Marketing** - Strategy-led B2B agricultural marketing
- **Digital Strike** - Agriculture-focused digital campaigns

**Farm Management Software Players**:
- **Deere & Company** - Dominant equipment + software ecosystem
- **Trimble Inc.** - Precision ag hardware and software
- **Climate LLC (Bayer)** - Data-driven farm insights
- **Granular (Corteva)** - Farm management platform
- **AGCO Corporation** - Equipment + Fuse connected services

**Emerging SaaS Players**:
- Agrivi, AgriWebb, FarmBrite, CropX, farmOS (open source)

**Key Insight**: Market is well-served at both ends. Large players dominate enterprise. Specialized boutique agencies serve mid-market. ScaleUp would face entrenched, knowledgeable competition.

**Sources**: [Capterra - Farm Management Software](https://www.capterra.com/farm-management-software/), [Mordor Intelligence - Farm Software Market](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/farm-management-software-market)

### 7. Acquisition Channels

**Where Farmers Congregate Online**:
- **Facebook**: Dominant platform; active groups like "Modern Farmers Network"
- **YouTube**: Most influential for instructional content
- **TikTok/Instagram Reels**: Younger farmers, visual content
- **Specialized platforms**: AgFuse, FarmerNation, Farmonaut Community
- **WhatsApp groups**: Regional peer networks

**Offline Channels**:
- Trade shows (Farm Progress Show, World Ag Expo)
- Cooperative meetings
- Extension service events
- Agricultural dealer networks
- Farm Bureau meetings

**Trust Building**:
- Peer recommendations are #1 influence on adoption
- Farmers heavily influenced by neighbors' experiences
- Long relationship development cycles
- Skepticism toward outsiders/non-ag people

**Key Insight**: Acquisition requires deep industry presence and credibility. Cold outreach is difficult. Partnerships with existing ag organizations may be necessary.

**Sources**: [Rhea+Kaiser - Farmers Use of Social Media](https://www.rkconnect.com/blog/farmers-use-of-social-media), [AgChat - Farmer Social Networks](https://agchat.org/marketing/what-social-networks-do-farmers-use/)

---

### 8. Sales Cycle Considerations

**B2B Sales Cycle Characteristics**:
- Average B2B sales cycle: 30-180+ days depending on complexity
- 6-10 decision-makers involved in significant purchases
- Only 17% of deliberation time spent with potential suppliers
- Seasonal purchasing patterns (pre-planting, post-harvest)

**Agricultural-Specific Factors**:
- Tied to crop cycles and cash flow timing
- Heavy reliance on government payment schedules
- Equipment/service decisions made during off-season
- Multi-generational family decision-making common

**Key Insight**: Sales cycles are long, seasonal, and relationship-dependent. Not suited for quick wins or high-velocity sales.

**Sources**: [Gartner B2B Buying Research](https://www.gartner.com), [Penn State Extension - Farm Business Strategies](https://extension.psu.edu/growth-strategy-year-round-sales-for-farm-and-food-businesses)

## Recommended Pivot: Target Agribusiness, Not Farms

Given the analysis, ScaleUp Ventures should consider targeting the **B2B companies that sell TO farmers** rather than farms directly:

### Better-Fit Subsegments

| Segment | Why It Fits | ScaleUp Service Fit |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Ag Equipment Dealers** | Local businesses, older owners, need digital presence | Web dev, local SEO, lead gen |
| **Seed/Fertilizer Distributors** | Regional B2B, outdated marketing | Growth marketing, CRM automation |
| **Agricultural Lenders/Insurers** | Financial services, need modern UX | Web dev, AI automation |
| **AgTech Startups** | Need dev/growth resources | Full suite fit |
| **Cooperative Marketing** | Large orgs with budgets, slow to modernize | Marketing automation, AI |
| **Agricultural SaaS Companies** | Growth-stage companies needing scale | Dev, growth, AI |

---

## Final Assessment

### Strengths for ScaleUp
- Massive market size with real modernization needs
- Ideal owner demographics (older, tech-averse)
- Lower digital advertising costs than other industries
- Genuine pain points around technology adoption

### Weaknesses for ScaleUp
- Individual farms have minimal marketing/dev budgets
- Long, seasonal, relationship-based sales cycles
- Entrenched specialized competition
- High trust barriers for outsiders
- Service needs (AgTech) don't match ScaleUp's core offerings

### Strategic Recommendation

**Score: 47/80 - MODERATE FIT**

**Do Not Pursue**: Individual farm operators as primary targets
**Consider Pursuing**: Agribusiness suppliers, AgTech companies, and agricultural service providers who market to farmers

If pursuing this vertical, ScaleUp should:
1. Partner with existing ag organizations for credibility
2. Focus on agribusiness B2B companies, not farms
3. Develop ag-specific case studies before outreach
4. Attend 1-2 major trade shows to build presence
5. Consider hiring staff with agricultural background
