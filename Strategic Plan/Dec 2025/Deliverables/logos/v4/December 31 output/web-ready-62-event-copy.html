Ready-to-Use Webinar Topics

The No-Build Decision: When Saying "Don't" Is the Best Advice

For founders considering expensive builds without validation

Full Description

Sometimes the best consulting engagement ends with a recommendation NOT to build. In this session, I'll share a real case study where strategic analysis saved a client $150K+ by identifying that their proposed solution already existed in the market.

You'll learn the validation framework that separates "should we build this?" from "should we buy, partner, or skip it entirely?" No more assuming that custom development is always the answer.

You'll learn:

  • The 5-question validation process before any significant technology investment
  • How to conduct rapid market analysis to find existing solutions
  • When custom builds make sense vs. when they're expensive ego projects
  • Real case study: How a $5K engagement saved $150K+ in unnecessary development
  • The conversation framework for telling clients what they need to hear

The best build is sometimes no build at all.

Building in Regulated Industries: Lessons from Financial Services

For founders navigating compliance-heavy markets

Full Description

You want to build something in a regulated industry. Maybe it's fintech, healthcare, or another compliance-heavy space. The opportunity is real‚Äîbut so are the landmines.

Based on real experience building a financial trading app POC, this session covers what founders need to know before diving into regulated markets‚Äîthe decisions that matter, the mistakes that are expensive, and the validation approach that works.

You'll discover:

  • The compliance considerations that should inform your architecture from day one
  • How to validate product-market fit in markets with heavy regulatory oversight
  • Build vs. partner decisions specific to regulated industries
  • The documentation and audit trail requirements you'll need to plan for
  • Real lessons from financial services POC development

Build smart in regulated markets. Know what you're getting into.

Relevant industries: Fintech, Healthcare/MedTech, InsurTech, RegTech, Compliance Software, Data Privacy

Early-Stage Architecture: Building Right the First Time

For startups about to make foundational technical decisions

Full Description

Your early architecture decisions will either accelerate or constrain everything that comes after. Most startups don't realize the cost of poor foundation choices until they're paying 10x to fix them later.

In this session, I'll share the architecture consulting framework we use with early-stage companies‚Äîfocused on making decisions that scale without over-engineering for problems you don't have yet.

In this session, you'll learn:

  • The "right-sized architecture" approach: enough structure to scale, not so much you're paralyzed
  • How to evaluate build vs. buy decisions for core infrastructure
  • Common early-stage architecture mistakes and their long-term costs
  • When to bring in external architecture review (and what to look for)
  • Real examples from startup advisory work across different industries

Build your foundation right. Everything else follows from there.

Founder to Investor: Preparing Your Startup for Funding

For founders preparing to raise their first round

Full Description

You've built something. Now you need capital to grow. But the gap between "working product" and "investable company" is wider than most founders realize.

Investors see hundreds of pitches. The ones that stand out have done the work to articulate their business model, demonstrate traction, and show they understand their market.

In this session, I'll share the preparation framework we use with startups getting ready for investor conversations‚Äîfrom business model clarity to the materials that actually matter.

You'll learn:

  • The 5 questions every investor will ask (and how to answer them convincingly)
  • Business model validation: what investors actually want to see
  • How to structure your story for different investor types
  • The materials that matter vs. the ones that waste your time
  • Warm intro strategies that actually work

Get investor-ready before you start the conversation.

AI-Enabled PM: Practical Infrastructure for Growing Teams

For founders scaling their project management capabilities

Full Description

You've heard the hype about AI transforming everything. But when you look at your project management chaos‚Äîscattered tools, inconsistent processes, visibility gaps‚Äîyou're not sure where to start.

This session covers the AI-enabled PM infrastructure we're building and implementing for clients: practical applications that improve project visibility, automate status updates, and give founders real-time insight without the overhead of traditional PM roles.

We'll focus on what's working now, not what's theoretical or still in beta.

You'll discover:

  • How AI can augment (not replace) your PM processes today
  • The automation stack that reduces status meeting overhead by consolidating updates
  • When to build custom PM tooling vs. use off-the-shelf solutions
  • Integration patterns that connect your existing tools without ripping everything out
  • Realistic expectations: what AI-enabled PM can and can't do right now

Practical AI applications. Real PM improvements. No hype.
