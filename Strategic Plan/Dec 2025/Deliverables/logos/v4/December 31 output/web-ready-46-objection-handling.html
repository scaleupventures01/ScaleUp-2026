The LAER Framework

Every objection is an opportunity. Use this four-step framework to turn resistance into revenue.

L
Listen
Hear the full objection without interrupting
A
Acknowledge
Validate their concern is legitimate
E
Explore
Ask questions to understand the real issue
R
Respond
Address with proof and next steps

Trust Issues

Objections rooted in past experiences or skepticism about your credibility

1
"We've been seeking certainty before"
Trust Issue
+
!
What They Really Mean
They've wasted money before and are protecting themselves from repeating that mistake. There's likely a specific bad experience - overpromising, poor communication, or no results. They want proof you're different.
>
Your Response Script
"I appreciate you sharing that - honestly, that frustration is one of the reasons we started ScaleUp Ventures. After our exit from a $4M venture-backed startup (backed by Google and Northwestern Mutual), our team came together specifically to help business owners avoid those bad experiences. Can I ask what specifically went wrong? Because we've built our model around preventing exactly that. We've delivered a full financial trading platform - from idea to SEC/NFA-compliant app - the highest regulatory bar in financial software. For ad campaigns, we guarantee specific lead numbers ($25-$33 CPL) - if we miss, we refund our fee. And we've told clients NOT to build when that was the right answer. One client came to us with a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment in mind. We proposed a $5,000 discovery (4 weeks). We found market saturation, hidden costs, and that 80% was achievable with existing solutions - saving them $150,000+ and 12+ months. Our philosophy: 'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you. We're consultants, not order-takers."
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results No-Build Decision example Discovery-first approach
?
Follow-Up Question
"What would need to be true for you to feel confident trying again?"
Purpose: Lets them define their own success criteria, which you can then commit to.

Quick Tips

  • Don't bash the previous agency - it comes across as unprofessional
  • Ask specific questions about what went wrong
  • Mirror their concern back to show you heard them

Let me walk you through a specific example relevant to your situation."

Proof Points to Reference

Budget Concerns

Objections about price, cost, and financial investment

3
"We don't have the budget"
Budget Concern
+
!
What They Really Mean
One of three things: (1) They genuinely can't afford it, (2) They don't see enough value to prioritize the spend, or (3) They're using it as a polite excuse to end the conversation. Your job is to figure out which.
>
Your Response Script
"I hear you - budget is a real consideration. Here's how we think about it: We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you. For ad campaigns, we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead - and we guarantee those lead numbers (if we miss, we refund our fee). For development projects, we've built SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - proving we can deliver complex products. Our AI agents save teams 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly. Our discovery engagements start at $5K. One client came to us with a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment. Our $5,000 discovery (4 weeks) found market saturation, hidden costs, and 80% achievable with existing solutions - saving them $150,000+ and 12+ months. 'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you. Would a focused discovery engagement make sense as a starting point?"
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) $150K+ saved (No-Build)
?
Follow-Up Question
"Help me understand - is this a timing issue or a value question?"
Purpose: Separates genuine budget constraints from value perception issues.

Quick Tips

  • Never immediately offer a discount - it devalues your service
  • Reframe as investment vs. cost
  • Calculate the cost of NOT doing this
12
"This seems expensive"
Budget Concern
+
!
What They Really Mean
They don't yet see enough value to justify the price. This is a value problem, not a price problem. They're comparing your price to something - likely the wrong thing (like doing it themselves or a cheaper alternative).
>
Your Response Script
"Compared to what? That's a fair question. Here's how I think about it: We prove it before you buy. We've built SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - proving we can deliver complex products. For ad campaigns, we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead - and we guarantee those lead numbers (fee refunded if we miss). Our AI agents save teams 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly. Compare that to: hiring a full-time technical lead ($150K+/year), or paying a big consultancy ($300+/hour), or making an expensive mistake because no one modeled the ROI first. One client came with a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment. Our $5,000 discovery (4 weeks) found market saturation, hidden costs, and 80% achievable with existing solutions - saving them $150,000+ and 12+ months. 'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you."
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) $150K+ saved (No-Build)
?
Follow-Up Question
"What would you expect to pay for the outcomes we're discussing?"
Purpose: Reveals their price anchor so you can address the gap directly.

Quick Tips

  • Always ask "compared to what?"
  • Shift from price to value and outcomes
  • Break it down to cost per lead/customer/day

Timing Issues

Objections about when to start, readiness, and bandwidth

6
"We're too busy right now"
Timing Issue
+
!
What They Really Mean
They're overwhelmed and adding anything new feels like a burden. They may also be concerned that onboarding will require too much of their time and attention.
>
Your Response Script
"I get it - you're in the weeds. Here's what makes us different: We're not an agency that needs constant feeding. We do focused engagements - architecture reviews, full product development, strategic decisions. Better yet, our AI automation agents handle delegated work - our clients save 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly as additional processes flow in. That's time BACK on your plate, not more demands. We helped one startup founder with market validation, business model validation, app development, fundraising plans, AND investor/advisor introductions - all while they were running their company. We work around your schedule, not the other way around."
*
Proof Points to Reference
AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) Focused engagements Startup founder experience End-to-end support
?
Follow-Up Question
"What would need to come off your plate for this to make sense?"
Purpose: Shows you can potentially help remove that burden, not add to it.

Quick Tips

  • Emphasize the time they'll SAVE, not spend
  • Quantify your time requirements precisely
  • Position yourself as a burden-reducer
7
"We need to think about it"
Timing Issue
+
!
What They Really Mean
There's an unspoken objection you haven't uncovered yet. "Think about it" almost always means there's something specific holding them back that they haven't shared. Your job is to find it.
>
Your Response Script
"Of course, take your time. But I've learned that 'think about it' usually means there's something specific I haven't addressed. Can I ask - if you had to decide right now, which way would you lean? And what's the one thing that would make it a definite yes? I'd rather address that now than have you walk away wondering."
*
Proof Points to Reference
Decision support materials FAQ document Risk-free trial offer Other decision-makers to involve
?
Follow-Up Question
"What specifically do you need to think about? Maybe I can help clarify right now."
Purpose: Surfaces the hidden objection so you can address it directly.

Quick Tips

  • Never accept "think about it" at face value
  • Be respectful but probe for the real concern
  • Set a specific follow-up time before ending the call

We've helped startups figure out architecture before they had a technical team. We've built SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - from idea to market. For ad campaigns, we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead with guaranteed results (fee refunded if we miss). Our AI agents help even lean teams save 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly. One client came to us with just an idea - we helped them with market validation, business model validation, app development, fundraising plans, and investor/advisor introductions. 'Not ready' might actually be the perfect time."

Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) Investor intro support
?
Follow-Up Question
"What would need to be true for you to feel ready?"

  • Challenge their definition of "ready"

13
"How long until we see results?"
Timing Issue
+
!
What They Really Mean
They're worried about sinking money into something with delayed or uncertain returns. They want predictability and a clear timeline to plan around. This is often about cash flow concerns.
>
Your Response Script
"Fair question - you need to plan. Here's our honest answer: It depends on the engagement. A discovery engagement takes 2-4 weeks and gives you a clear decision framework. For ad campaigns, you see results as leads come in - we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead with guaranteed results (fee refunded if we miss). For AI automation agents, team members save 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly as additional processes flow in - you see value incrementally as agents take over routine tasks. For full product builds, we've delivered SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - from idea to market. What's the outcome you're trying to achieve?"
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) Discovery: 2-4 weeks
?
Follow-Up Question
"What's driving the timeline question? Are you working against a specific deadline?"
Purpose: Uncovers if there's urgency you can address or a business constraint to work around.

Quick Tips

  • Give specific, honest timelines - never overpromise
  • Explain what happens at each milestone
  • Offer leading indicators they can track before full ROI

Value Questions

Objections about effectiveness, differentiation, and past experiences

4
"We tried marketing, it didn't work"
Value Question
+
!
What They Really Mean
They invested time or money in marketing (probably random tactics without strategy) and didn't see the return. They've concluded marketing doesn't work for their business, when really it was bad execution.
>
Your Response Script
"I believe you - most marketing doesn't work. But marketing isn't one thing. It's like saying 'we tried medicine, it didn't work.' The question is: which medicine, what dose, and was it the right diagnosis? What exactly did you try, and what happened? When we run ad campaigns, we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead - and we guarantee those lead numbers (fee refunded if we miss). We did this for an HR Automation Tool client, enabling them to validate their market, customer, and profitable customer acquisition. Nine times out of ten, marketing fails due to wrong channel, wrong message, or not enough time - not because marketing doesn't work for your business."
*
Proof Points to Reference
Lead Guarantee Results HR Automation Tool results Diagnostic assessment Strategy vs. tactics distinction
?
Follow-Up Question
"What specifically did you try, and how did you measure whether it worked?"
Purpose: Often reveals they didn't measure properly or gave up too early.

Quick Tips

  • Don't argue - validate their experience first
  • Diagnose what they actually tried
  • Separate strategy from tactics in your explanation

Competition Concerns

Objections about existing solutions and alternatives

10
"We already have someone doing this"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
They might be satisfied with their current solution (unlikely if they're talking to you), they might be locked into a contract, or they're using this to end the conversation. The fact they took the meeting suggests something isn't working.
>
Your Response Script
"That's great that you're already investing in this area. Quick question - if they were crushing it, would you have taken this call? I'm not trying to put anyone down, but usually when someone talks to us, there's something they wish was different. What would that be? Even if we never work together, I might be able to give you some ideas to get more from what you're already doing."
*
Proof Points to Reference
Audit/assessment offer Complementary services Transition support Competitive comparison
?
Follow-Up Question
"On a scale of 1-10, how happy are you with the results you're getting?"
Purpose: Anything below 8 opens the door for discussion about what's missing.

Quick Tips

  • Don't bash the competitor directly
  • The meeting itself is a signal of dissatisfaction
  • Offer value even if they don't switch
16
"We can do this ourselves"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
They're comparing your cost to hiring internally or DIY. They may underestimate the complexity, time, or expertise required. Or they're protecting their ego - admitting they need help feels like a failure.
>
Your Response Script
"You probably could - you're clearly smart and capable. The question is: should you? Here's the honest calculation: hiring a senior technical person is $150K+/year. The learning curve for specialized work is steep. We've built SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - from idea to market. For ad campaigns, we've achieved $25-$33 cost per lead with guaranteed results (fee refunded if we miss). Our AI agents save teams 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly. One client came to us with a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment in mind. We proposed a $5,000 discovery engagement (4 weeks). We found market saturation, hidden costs, and that 80% was achievable with existing solutions - saving them $150,000+ and 12+ months. 'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you. Sometimes the right answer is 'don't do this.' That's experienced outside perspective."
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) $150K+ saved (No-Build)
?
Follow-Up Question
"What's stopped you from doing it so far?"
Purpose: Usually reveals time or expertise constraints that justify your value.

Quick Tips

  • Calculate true cost of DIY (time + tools + mistakes)
  • Position as acceleration, not replacement
  • Respect their capability while showing your value
17
"We're talking to other vendors"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
They're doing due diligence, which is smart. They might be trying to create leverage for pricing. Or they genuinely don't know how to evaluate options. This is actually a buying signal - they're actively in market.
>

(We've built SEC/NFA-compliant financial software - the highest regulatory bar - from idea to market.) Two, guaranteed results - will they stand behind their work? (For ad campaigns, we guarantee specific lead numbers at $25-$33 CPL - fee refunded if we miss.) Three, tangible value - can they show measurable outcomes? (Our AI agents save teams 20-25 hours per month, growing 10-20% monthly.) Four, honesty - will they tell you NOT to do something if that's the right answer? (We've saved clients $150K+ by recommending they don't build.) Ask every vendor these questions."
*
Proof Points to Reference
SEC/NFA Financial Platform Lead Guarantee Results AI Agents (20-25 hrs/month saved) $150K+ saved (No-Build)
?
Follow-Up Question
"What's your timeline for making this decision?"
Purpose: Helps you understand urgency and plan your follow-up.

Quick Tips

  • Be the helpful advisor, not the pushy salesperson
  • Provide a framework that makes you look good
  • Ask who else they're considering (you may learn a lot)
18
"We're locked into a contract"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
They're stuck with a current vendor but interested in changing. This is actually a good sign - they're unhappy enough to look elsewhere even while under contract. They need a reason to break the contract or a timeline to wait.
>
Your Response Script
"I understand - contracts are contracts. When does it end? Let's put something on the calendar for then. In the meantime, two thoughts: First, many contracts have performance clauses - if they're not meeting agreed-upon metrics, you may have an out. Worth reviewing the fine print. Second, some of our clients run us in parallel during their transition period - we start building pipeline so there's no gap when you're free. Would either of those options work for your situation?"
*
Proof Points to Reference
Parallel engagement option Contract review tips Transition planning Early buyout analysis
?
Follow-Up Question
"What would need to be true for you to not renew that contract?"
Purpose: Helps you position your future pitch around their pain points.

Quick Tips

  • Get the exact contract end date
  • Set a calendar reminder to follow up
  • Offer value now to stay top of mind
19
"My partner/spouse needs to approve this"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
Could be legitimate (another decision-maker exists) or a stall tactic. Either way, you need to get in front of the other person or arm your contact with what they need to sell internally.
>
Your Response Script
"Absolutely - major decisions should be made together. Would it help if I joined a call with both of you? That way your partner can ask questions directly, and you won't have to play telephone. I've found that works better than trying to recreate the conversation. Alternatively, I can put together a summary document you can share - what questions do you think they'll have?"
*
Proof Points to Reference
Executive summary document Two-Track ROI one-pager Common questions answered Case study PDF
?
Follow-Up Question
"What's your partner most likely to be concerned about?"
Purpose: Lets you preemptively address their partner's objections.

Quick Tips

  • Always try to get in front of all decision-makers
  • Provide leave-behind materials they can share
  • Ask what the partner's role and concerns might be
20
"Send me some information"
Competition
+
!
What They Really Mean
Usually a polite brush-off. They want to end the conversation without saying no directly. Occasionally they genuinely want to review materials before committing time. Either way, information alone rarely closes deals.
>
Your Response Script
"Happy to send information - but honestly, PDFs and brochures rarely do justice to what we actually do. They end up in inbox purgatory. Here's what I'd suggest instead: let's do a 15-minute call where I can show you exactly what we'd do for your specific situation. If after that you want materials to share internally, I'll customize them for you. Fair enough? What does your Thursday look like?"
*
Proof Points to Reference
Custom proposal offer 15-minute demo Case study video Interactive assessment
?
Follow-Up Question
"What specifically would you want that information to cover?"
Purpose: Forces them to articulate what they actually need, which often reveals the real objection.

Quick Tips

  • Pivot to scheduling a short call instead
  • If you must send, follow up immediately
  • Make the next step specific and calendared
