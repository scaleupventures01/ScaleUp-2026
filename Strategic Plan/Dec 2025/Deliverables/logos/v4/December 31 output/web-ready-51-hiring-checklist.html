20 Questions to Ask
Before Hiring Any Consultant

Protect your investment. Use this checklist to evaluate consultants, agencies, and advisors before you sign.

ScaleUp Ventures
Print or save this checklist for your next discovery call

Why This Matters

Hiring the wrong consultant costs more than money. It costs time, momentum, and often your team's trust in outside help. The right questions separate genuine experts from smooth talkers.

How to use this checklist:

  • Bring these questions to every discovery call
  • Check off questions as you get satisfactory answers
  • Use the notes section to capture their responses
  • Compare answers across multiple candidates
0/20 Questions Asked
⚙

About Their Process

Question 1
How do you prove ROI before I fully commit?
Why it matters: Good consultants de-risk the engagement. Look for pilots, proof-of-concept phases, or performance guarantees.
Question 2
What's your methodology? Walk me through it step by step.
Why it matters: Vague answers signal they're making it up as they go. Proven frameworks indicate repeatable success.
Question 3
How do you measure success? What specific metrics will we track?
Why it matters: Without clear metrics, you can't evaluate performance or hold anyone accountable.
Question 4
What does the first 30/60/90 days look like?
Why it matters: This reveals if they have a real plan or just promise results "eventually."
Question 5
Who on your team will actually work on my account?
Why it matters: Sometimes you meet the A-team in sales, then get handed to juniors. Know who does the work.
★

About Their Experience

Question 6
Have you worked with companies like mine? (Size, industry, stage)
Why it matters: What works for enterprise doesn't work for SMB. Industry context matters.
Question 7
Can I speak with 2-3 references? (Current and past clients)
Why it matters: Anyone can cherry-pick testimonials. Real references reveal the truth.
Question 8
Show me a case study with specific numbers.
Why it matters: "Increased revenue" is meaningless. "Grew revenue 47% in 6 months" is verifiable.
Question 9
What's your biggest failure? What did you learn?
Why it matters: Everyone fails. Those who learn from it are worth hiring. Those who deny it are dangerous.
Question 10
How many clients do you currently serve?
Why it matters: Too few might mean they're new. Too many means you won't get attention.
$

About Pricing & Terms

Question 11
What's included in the price? What costs extra?
Why it matters: Hidden fees add up. Know the full cost before you commit.
Question 12
What's the minimum contract length? Why?
Why it matters: Long contracts without proof of concept = red flag. 90-day pilots show confidence.
Question 13
What if it doesn't work? What's your guarantee or exit clause?
Why it matters: Skin in the game matters. Those who guarantee results believe in their work.
Question 14
Do I own the work product? (Code, content, strategies, data)
Why it matters: Some agencies hold your assets hostage. Get ownership in writing.
Question 15
How quickly can we start? What do you need from us?
Why it matters: Reveals their current capacity and how organized their onboarding is.
✉

About Communication & Support

Question 16
How often will we meet? What reporting will I receive?
Why it matters: No visibility = no accountability. Regular updates prevent surprises.
Question 17
What's your response time for urgent issues?
Why it matters: When things break, speed matters. Set expectations upfront.
Question 18
Who is my main point of contact?
Why it matters: One person who owns the relationship is better than a rotating cast.
Question 19
What happens when you disagree with my direction?
Why it matters: You want a partner who pushes back thoughtfully, not a yes-person.
Question 20
What does the handoff look like when we're done?
Why it matters: Good consultants make themselves unnecessary. They should train your team.
⚠

Red Flags to Watch For

If you encounter any of these, proceed with extreme caution:

✕
Guaranteed results with no qualifications "We guarantee #1 rankings" or "Double your revenue guaranteed." Real experts know results depend on many factors.
✕
Long contracts required upfront (12+ months) If they need to lock you in before proving value, that's a sign they can't retain clients on results alone.
✕
Vague or proprietary "secret sauce" Refusing to explain methodology often means there isn't one. Transparency builds trust.
✕
No references or only anonymous testimonials If no client will vouch for them by name, ask yourself why.
✕
Pressure tactics or artificial urgency "This price is only good today" is a sales trick, not a partnership approach.
✕
They talk more than they listen A good consultant asks more questions than they answer in the first call.
✕
No clear point of contact or account manager "The team will handle it" often means no one owns your success.
