2 Track 1: Ad Campaign Lead Guarantee

For paid advertising and lead generation campaigns, we project exactly how many leads you'll receive - and we guarantee that number.

How It Works

  • Before campaign launch: We analyze your market, industry benchmarks, and competitive landscape to project lead volume
  • We commit to a number: "This campaign will generate X qualified leads at your proposed budget"
  • You control ad spend: The ad budget goes directly to the platform (Google, Meta, etc.) - you see exactly where every dollar goes
  • If we miss: We refund our management fee. You only paid for ad spend, which went to the platforms regardless.
  • Why this works for us: We only take campaigns where we're confident in the projections. If the numbers don't support success, we'll tell you before you spend anything.

Why We Decide on the Conservative Case

Most agencies sell you on the best-case scenario. Then when reality hits, they blame "market conditions" or "your team's execution."

We flip this: if the conservative scenario doesn't show positive ROI, we recommend against proceeding - even if the mid and high scenarios look fantastic. This protects you from optimism bias, which kills more software projects than technical debt.

4 Two Types of Return

ROI isn't always cash in your pocket this quarter. For business owners planning an exit, increased company value is often more valuable than immediate cash return.

Cash Return
Direct financial impact you can see in your bank account. Revenue increase, cost savings, efficiency gains that translate to real dollars.
Examples: Lead generation campaigns, sales automation, process efficiency tools, website conversion optimization
Asset / Company Value
Improvements that increase what your company is worth when you sell. Digital capabilities directly impact business valuation.
Examples: Modern technology stack, automated operations, digital customer experience, documented processes, proprietary systems

Why This Matters for Exit-Focused Owners

65% of our target clients are planning to sell their business within 5-10 years. For them, the question isn't just "will this make money this year?" - it's "will this make my company worth more when I sell?"

A custom CRM might not generate direct revenue, but it could increase your valuation by 0.5-1x EBITDA by demonstrating operational sophistication to buyers. We model both types of return so you can make the right decision for your situation.

7 When the Answer is "Don't Build"

Any vendor who makes money on builds has an incentive to recommend building. Our discovery model separates the analysis from the build, so you get honest recommendations.

Real Example: The No-Build Decision

A client approached ScaleUp Ventures with a vision for a custom web application, ready to budget $150,000+ and commit 12-18 months to development. They expected us to build it.

Rather than immediately proceeding, we proposed a $5,000 discovery and feasibility analysis lasting four weeks. This examination covered market conditions and saturation levels, client assumptions and validity, competitive landscape assessment, and technical requirements evaluation.

The analysis revealed three critical issues:

  • Market Reality: The target market was more saturated than the client realized
  • Hidden Costs: The envisioned technical approach required ongoing maintenance expenses not accounted for in the budget
  • Existing Solutions: Current alternatives could accomplish 80% of project goals at significantly lower cost

Outcome

Client saved $150,000+ in misdirected spending and 12+ months of effort. They used the existing SaaS solution instead.

Why This Matters

"'Certainty First' is not a marketing slogan. It means we would rather earn $5,000 telling you the truth than $150,000 building something that will not serve you."
