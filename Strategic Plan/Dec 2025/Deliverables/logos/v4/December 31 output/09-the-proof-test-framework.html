The 5-Step Process

A rigorous, systematic approach to validating ROI before engagement

Days 1-2 Discovery Immersion

We dive deep into your business model, current operations, market position, and strategic objectives. This isn't a surface-level intake -- it's a comprehensive understanding of every lever that affects your outcomes.

Client Receives
  • Business Model Canvas documentation
  • Current state operational assessment
  • Initial opportunity identification matrix
1
Days 3-5 Data Validation

We collect, verify, and analyze the quantitative data that will drive our ROI model. Every assumption is tested. Every number is validated. We identify data gaps and work with you to fill them.

Client Receives
  • Data quality audit report
  • Validated metrics baseline
  • Gap analysis with mitigation strategies
2
Days 6-8 Scenario Modeling

Using our proprietary modeling framework, we build multiple scenarios: conservative, moderate, and optimistic. Each scenario is stress-tested against market conditions and operational realities.

Client Receives

  • Sensitivity analysis on key variables
  • Risk-adjusted return calculations
3
Days 9-10 Proof Synthesis

We synthesize all findings into a clear, actionable recommendation. This includes the definitive ROI projection, implementation roadmap, and the specific conditions required to achieve projected returns.

Client Receives
  • Executive summary with go/no-go recommendation
  • Detailed ROI projection with confidence intervals
  • Critical success factors documentation
4
Day 11 Decision Briefing

A comprehensive presentation to your leadership team walking through every aspect of The Proof Test findings. You leave with complete clarity on whether to proceed -- and exactly what to expect if you do.

Client Receives
  • Executive presentation deck
  • Complete Proof Test documentation package
  • Implementation blueprint (if proceeding)

Discovery Immersion (Days 1-2)

TechScale was considering a complete ERP overhaul to address operational inefficiencies. During discovery, we mapped their current processes across 6 departments, interviewed 12 key stakeholders, and identified 23 discrete pain points costing them an estimated $1.8M annually in lost productivity.

Key Finding: 60% of pain points originated from 3 core processes
2

Data Validation (Days 3-5)

We pulled 18 months of operational data from their existing systems. Our analysis revealed that the vendor's initial ROI projection was based on industry averages that didn't reflect TechScale's actual cost structure. Their real baseline was 34% worse than assumed -- meaning higher potential gains but also higher implementation risk.

Data Insight: True baseline $2.4M in annual losses, not $1.8M
3

Scenario Modeling (Days 6-8)

We built three scenarios: Conservative (addressing only the 3 core processes), Moderate (full ERP with phased rollout), and Optimistic (full ERP with aggressive timeline). Each scenario included sensitivity analysis on 8 key variables including adoption rate, training time, and productivity ramp.

Model Output: Conservative ROI: 127% | Moderate: 234% | Optimistic: 312%
4

Proof Synthesis (Days 9-10)

Our recommendation: proceed with the Moderate scenario but with a critical modification -- address the 3 core processes first (Phase 1) before expanding to full ERP (Phase 2). This approach reduced upfront risk while validating returns before the larger investment.

Recommendation: Phased approach with $1.2M Phase 1 investment

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about The Proof Test methodology

What does The Proof Test cost?

+
The Proof Test is a fixed-fee engagement priced based on the complexity of your situation. For most organizations, this ranges from $25,000 to $75,000. The fee is independent of any subsequent engagement -- if The Proof Test reveals that proceeding isn't advisable, you've avoided a much larger mistake. If you proceed with a full engagement, The Proof Test fee is credited toward your project investment.

What if The Proof Test shows we shouldn't proceed?

+
That's exactly the point. About 20% of our Proof Test engagements result in a "no-go" recommendation -- and those are some of our most valuable outcomes. Clients avoid investments that wouldn't have delivered the expected returns. We provide detailed documentation of why the initiative isn't advisable at this time, and what would need to change for it to become viable.

What data do you need from us?

+
We provide a detailed data request list tailored to your specific initiative. Typically this includes: historical operational metrics (12-24 months), financial data related to the areas of focus, current process documentation, and previous relevant analyses. We work with whatever format your data exists in and help identify gaps early in the process.

How accurate are your ROI projections?

+
Our track record shows that actual results fall within our projected ranges 87% of the time. For engagements that proceed following a "go" recommendation, the median outcome is 94% of our moderate scenario projection. We're conservative in our estimates because we'd rather under-promise and over-deliver than the reverse.

Can we do The Proof Test before selecting a vendor?

+
Absolutely -- this is actually an ideal use case. The Proof Test can help you develop requirements, understand what ROI you should expect, and create evaluation criteria before you engage with vendors. This gives you significant leverage in negotiations because you'll know exactly what outcomes you should demand.

What happens after The Proof Test?

+
If our recommendation is to proceed, you'll have a complete implementation blueprint ready to execute. You can choose to engage ScaleUp Ventures for implementation support, use another firm, or handle it internally. The Proof Test deliverables are yours regardless. About 70% of clients who receive a "go" recommendation choose to continue with us for implementation.

How do you handle sensitive data?

+
All Proof Test engagements are covered by comprehensive NDAs. We use secure, encrypted file sharing systems, and all client data is destroyed 90 days after project completion unless you request otherwise. Our team members are bound by confidentiality agreements, and we never use client data for any purpose other than your specific engagement.
