Our Two-Track ROI Model

"We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you"

Track 1: Ad Campaigns

We quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads BEFORE starting to ensure expected results meet client needs. Guaranteed lead numbers with our fee refunded (not ad spend) if we miss the target. You know exactly what you're getting before you commit.

Track 2: App & Development Projects

Three-scenario ROI projections (high/mid/low) showing return in cash OR company/asset value. Transparent expectations from day one.

Phone/Video Call Script: The "What's Next" Conversation

Opening (30 seconds):

"[First Name], thanks for making time today. Before we dive into the regular update, I wanted to step back and have a bigger-picture conversation. You've accomplished [summary of wins]. I'm curious—when you look at the next 6-12 months, what's keeping you up at night? What would make you say 'this was a breakthrough year' for [Company Name]?"

Listen and probe (2-3 minutes):

Let them talk. Take notes. Ask: "Tell me more about that." and "What would success look like specifically?"

Bridge to expanded engagement (1 minute):

"That's interesting—[paraphrase their goal]. We're actually helping [similar client or 'several clients'] with exactly that right now. The work we're doing together on [current project] positions you well to tackle [their stated goal]. Would you be open to me putting together a brief proposal on how we could support that initiative? No pressure—just want to show you what's possible."

Close (30 seconds):

"I'll send something over by [specific date]. We can review it together next time and you can tell me if it's even worth discussing further. Sound fair?"

Best Time to Ask
After delivering a win, during quarterly reviews, or when client mentions future challenges
Red Flags to Avoid
Don't pitch expansion when current deliverables are late, quality is questioned, or client is stressed

Phone Script: Asking for a Referral Live

After a positive meeting/call:

"[First Name], I'm really glad to hear that [positive thing they mentioned]. Quick question before we wrap up—we're always looking to work with more great companies like [Company Name]. Is there anyone in your network who's dealing with [similar challenge] that might benefit from a conversation with us? No pressure, just curious if anyone comes to mind."

If they say yes:

"That's great. What's the best way to connect—would you be comfortable making an introduction, or would you prefer I reach out directly and mention you?"

If they hesitate:

"No worries at all. If anyone comes to mind later, just shoot me an email. I'll follow up with them personally and make sure it's not awkward."

Low effort, quick win

Re-engagement & Win-back Messaging

Wait at least 30 days after pause/end before first re-engagement attempt
Pause Re-engagement: The Check-in
Send: 30-45 days after pause begins
Subject: Checking in—how are things going?

Hi [First Name],

I hope this finds you well. I know it's been [timeframe] since we paused our engagement, and I wanted to check in.

First—no agenda here. I'm genuinely curious how you and the team are doing, and whether [issue that caused the pause] has resolved.

Second—if things have shifted and you're thinking about picking back up, I'd love to chat about how we can make that seamless. We've kept your files and context intact.

Either way, I'm around if you ever want to grab a virtual coffee and catch up.

Hope to hear from you,

[Your Name]

Win-back: The Direct Ask
Last attempt—use when others haven't worked
Subject: Ready when you are

Hi [First Name],

I'm reaching out one more time.

I know our engagement ended [timeframe] ago, and I've respected your space since then. But I wanted you to know: if you're ever ready to pick things back up, we'd welcome you back.

Your account is still set up. We still have context on [Company Name]. And we've genuinely missed working with you.

If the timing isn't right, I completely understand. This is the last you'll hear from me unless you reach out. But if there's even a small part of you considering it, reply to this email and let's talk.

Either way, I wish you and the team all the best,

[Your Name]

Win-back Best Practices
✓ Lead with value, not sales
✓ Acknowledge time passed without guilt
✓ Reference specific context from relationship
✓ Make it easy to say yes OR no
✓ Space attempts 30-60 days apart
✓ Know when to stop (3-4 attempts max)
