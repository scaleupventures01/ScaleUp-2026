1 Competitor Categories

Understanding the competitive landscape is essential for positioning. ScaleUp Ventures competes across four primary categories, each with distinct strengths, weaknesses, and buyer motivations.

Traditional Agencies

Full-service marketing and development firms

  • High overhead costs
  • Established processes
  • Team of specialists
  • Long timelines
  • Premium pricing

Freelancers

Independent consultants and contractors

  • Lower costs
  • Flexible availability
  • Single point of failure
  • Limited scope
  • Variable quality

Offshore Teams

International development and support teams

  • Lowest costs
  • Time zone challenges
  • Communication barriers
  • Quality inconsistency
  • IP concerns

DIY / Internal

In-house teams and self-service tools

  • Full control
  • Learning curve
  • Opportunity cost
  • Tool fatigue
  • Limited expertise

8 What NOT to Say About Competitors

Competitive positioning should elevate ScaleUp Ventures, not tear down alternatives. Here is what to avoid and better alternatives.

X Never Say This

"Agencies are a waste of money."
"Freelancers are unreliable and flaky."
"Offshore work is always garbage."
"You will fail if you try to do it yourself."

Why: Negative attacks make you look unprofessional and defensive. They also insult prospects who may have used these options successfully.

+ Say This Instead

"Agencies can be great for enterprises with big budgets and long timelines."
"Freelancers work well for defined, scoped projects with clear deliverables."
"Offshore teams can work when you have strong internal management capacity."
"DIY is a great way to start and learn what you really need."

Why: Acknowledging when alternatives work shows confidence and builds trust. Then explain when ScaleUp is the better fit.

Additional Guidelines

  • ! Never name specific competitors unless the prospect mentions them first
  • ! Never claim capabilities you do not have just to win against a competitor
  • ! Never disparage a prospect's past choices - they were doing their best
  • ! Never make promises about competitors' pricing or capabilities
  • ! Never use fear tactics or worst-case scenarios

9 When to Use Competitor Comparison

Appropriate Times to Compare

+

When the prospect explicitly asks

"How are you different from [agency/freelancer/etc]?" is an invitation to compare. Take it.

+

When the prospect mentions evaluating alternatives

If they say "We're also talking to..." or "We're considering...", provide helpful comparison context.

+

When past experience shapes their concerns

"We tried a freelancer before and it didn't work out" - address the category concern with how you are different.

+

When explaining your positioning

In discovery, naturally reference where you fit: "Unlike traditional agencies, we focus on..."

Times to Avoid Comparison

-

As an unsolicited attack

Never bring up competitors just to criticize them. It looks defensive and desperate.

-

When you do not know the specific competitor

If they mention a specific company you do not know, ask questions rather than making assumptions.

-

When the prospect has already decided on you

If they are ready to move forward, stop selling against alternatives - you have already won.

~

When the alternative is a strong fit for them

If a prospect genuinely would be better served elsewhere, consider whether pursuing them is right for both parties.

12 Ethical Guidelines for Competitive Messaging

Our competitive positioning reflects our values. Winning the right way builds lasting relationships and a reputation that attracts the right clients.

+ Always Do

  • Lead with your own strengths, not competitor weaknesses
  • Acknowledge when alternatives genuinely work well for some situations
  • Use factual, verifiable comparisons
  • Respect the prospect's intelligence and autonomy to decide
  • Be willing to refer out when you are not the right fit
  • Focus on value delivered, not just price comparisons
  • Maintain confidentiality about competitor information
  • Admit when you do not know something about a competitor
  • Treat competitor employees with respect
  • Win on merit, lose with grace

X Never Do

  • Spread rumors or unverified information about competitors
  • Make false claims about competitor capabilities or pricing
  • Disparage competitor employees personally
  • Use confidential information obtained improperly
  • Prey on prospects' fears with worst-case scenarios
  • Promise capabilities you do not have to win against a competitor
  • Imply illegal or unethical behavior without evidence
  • Contact competitor clients specifically to poach
  • Celebrate competitor failures publicly
  • Engage in price wars that devalue the industry

The ScaleUp Standard

"We compete with confidence because we deliver with certainty. Our goal is not to tear down alternatives but to illuminate why ScaleUp Ventures is the right choice for clients who value speed, quality, and partnership. When we win, it is because we earned it. When we lose, it is an opportunity to learn and improve."
