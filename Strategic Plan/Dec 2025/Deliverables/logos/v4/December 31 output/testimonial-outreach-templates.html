The No-Build Client

Discovery Engagement - Saved $150K+ by NOT Building

Project Context

Paid $5K for discovery engagement. After thorough analysis, ScaleUp recommended NOT building the planned solution - saving the client $150K+ in development costs and protecting them from a potentially failed investment.

Initial Outreach Email

Primary
Subject Line
Quick question about our discovery project together
Email Body
Hi [First Name], I hope this finds you well. I've been thinking about our discovery engagement from [timeframe] and wanted to reach out. You may remember that after digging into your business numbers and market analysis, we recommended you hold off on the build - that the project as scoped wouldn't deliver the return you needed. That's not an easy recommendation for us to make (it means we don't get the development work), but it was the right one for your business. I'm reaching out because we're rebuilding our website with a focus on real client experiences. Our target clients are typically established business owners who've been seeking certainty before - people who need to see proof before they trust anyone with their investment. Your story - that we told you NOT to build something - is actually one of our most powerful examples of what makes us different. Most agencies would have happily taken your money and built something that wouldn't have worked. Would you be willing to share a few words about your experience? I'm not looking for polished marketing language - just honest feedback about what it was like working with us.
A few questions that might help:
  • What was your initial concern or skepticism when you came to us?
  • How did our discovery process compare to what you expected?
  • What was your reaction when we recommended NOT building?
  • Looking back, how do you feel about that decision now?
  • Would you recommend our discovery process to a peer? Why or why not?
If it's easier, I'm happy to draft something based on our conversations and have you review/edit it. Whatever works best for you. I completely understand if you'd prefer not to - your privacy matters. Either way, I hope the money we helped you not spend is working hard somewhere else. Thanks for considering this, [Your Name] P.S. - We can keep your testimonial anonymous (first name and industry only) if you prefer.

Follow-Up Email (5 Days)

If No Response
Subject Line
Re: Quick question about our discovery project together
Email Body
Hi [First Name], Just floating this back up in case it got buried. I know you're busy running a business. If a testimonial isn't something you're comfortable with, no worries at all - I completely understand. If it's more about finding the time, I'm happy to: 1. Draft something based on our project notes for you to review 2. Set up a 10-minute call where I ask a few questions 3. Accept even just 2-3 sentences via text or email Whichever is easiest for you. Or if you'd prefer I not follow up again, just let me know - no hard feelings. Thanks, [Your Name]

Tips for Sending These Messages

Personalize Before Sending

Replace all [bracketed items] with specific details. Add a personal touch - mention something specific from your work together or their recent news.

Timing Matters

Send Tuesday-Thursday, mid-morning. Avoid Mondays (inbox overwhelm) and Fridays (weekend mode). If they're in a different timezone, adjust accordingly.

Follow Through on Offers

If you offer to draft something for them, be prepared to do it well. Have your project notes ready. Make the draft specific and honest - not marketing fluff.

Respect the "No"

If they decline or don't respond after the follow-up, let it go. Pushing harder damages the relationship. Some clients have valid reasons for staying private.

Capture Raw Feedback

If they respond informally (text, call, etc.), write down their exact words. Authentic language is more valuable than polished marketing copy.

Get Permission for Usage

Before publishing, send them the final version and get explicit approval. Include how it will be displayed (website, proposals, etc.).
