We've Heard These Stories

The Scars Don't Fade Easily

  • The Promises That Weren't Kept

    They painted a beautiful picture in the sales meeting. Guaranteed results. Explosive growth. A partnership that would transform your business. Six months later, you had nothing but excuses and invoices.

  • The Money That Was Wasted

    It wasn't just the retainer. It was the opportunity cost. The other initiatives you didn't fund. The hires you didn't make. The time you'll never get back. That money represented your hopes for what your business could become.

  • The Trust That Was Broken

    Maybe the hardest part. You took a risk. You let someone in. You believed them. And now a part of you wonders if you can ever trust an outside partner again. That skepticism? It's not a flaw. It's wisdom earned the hard way.

We've sat across the table from founders who told us these exact stories. Some were angry. Some were resigned. All of them were careful. And they had every right to be.

The Uncomfortable Truth

It's Not Just Bad Actors. It's a Broken Industry.

The agency model itself creates problems that even well-intentioned firms struggle to overcome.

Misaligned Incentives

Most agencies get paid whether you succeed or not. Their revenue comes from retainers and billable hours, not from your results. When your incentives don't match, your outcomes won't either.

The Expertise Gap

The senior people who sold you? They hand you off to junior staff the moment the contract is signed. You bought decades of experience and got months of it instead.

No Real Accountability

Vague deliverables. Murky metrics. Goals that shift when they're not met. It's designed this way, whether consciously or not, because accountability is uncomfortable when you're not confident in your ability to deliver.
