Problem Slide (Slide 3-4)

Transition phrase after presenting pain points. Creates bridge to solution.
Case Study Slide
"Ask us about [Client Name]'s full journey"
Small text in corner. Creates conversation starter without hard sell.

"Let's find your starting point"
Non-committal but forward-moving. Works for any budget conversation.
Social Proof Slide

Reinforces credibility while suggesting action. Reference specific client outcomes.

Internal Training

How to Use CTAs Effectively

Do This

  • Match CTA intensity to prospect readiness
  • Use action verbs: Start, Get, Schedule, Discover
  • Include specific outcomes or benefits
  • Create urgency without pressure
  • Test different CTAs and track response rates
  • Place CTAs where the eye naturally lands
  • Use one primary CTA per page/slide
  • Make CTAs visually distinct with gold/navy contrast

Avoid This

  • Generic "Learn More" or "Click Here"
  • Multiple competing CTAs on same page
  • CTAs that don't match the content
  • Aggressive or salesy language
  • CTAs without clear next steps
  • Burying CTAs at the bottom
  • Using CTAs that promise too much
  • Forgetting to include contact info

CTA Intensity Ladder

Intensity Best For Example CTAs
Low Cold prospects, awareness stage "Stay connected" / "Get insights"
Medium Warm leads, consideration stage "See how it works" / "Explore your options"
High Hot leads, decision stage "Schedule now" / "Start your engagement"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1
Using the same CTA everywhere
Fix: Match CTA to context. A trade show banner needs different language than a follow-up email. Customize based on where the prospect is in their journey.
Mistake #2
Making promises your CTA can't keep
Fix: "Get instant results" sets wrong expectations. Use "Begin your growth journey" instead. Honest CTAs build trust.
Mistake #3
Forgetting the value proposition
Fix: Don't just say "Contact us." Say "Get your free growth roadmap." Every CTA should answer "What's in it for me?"
Mistake #4
Hiding contact information
Fix: Always include at least two ways to reach us near every CTA: email, phone, or QR code to scheduling page.
Mistake #5
Poor visual hierarchy
Fix: Primary CTA should be the most visually prominent element. Use gold button on navy background. Ensure 44px minimum touch target for digital.
