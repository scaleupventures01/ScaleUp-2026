Featured Engagement

The No-Build Decision

When "Certainty First" means telling you not to build.

The Situation

A client came to us with a vision for a custom web application. They had budgeted $150,000 or more for development and were ready to commit 12-18 months to the project. They wanted us to build it.

Instead, we proposed a $5,000 discovery and feasibility analysis. We spent four weeks examining their market, their assumptions, their competitive landscape, and the technical requirements they had outlined.

What We Found

Our analysis revealed that the market they wanted to enter was more saturated than they realized. The technical approach they envisioned would require ongoing maintenance costs they had not budgeted for. And critically, there were existing solutions that could accomplish 80% of their goals at a fraction of the cost.

Our Recommendation

We told them not to build. We provided a detailed report explaining why, alternative approaches they could take with significantly less investment, and a clear-eyed assessment of what it would actually take to succeed if they still wanted to proceed.

They thanked us. They did not build. And they saved at least $150,000 and 12 months of effort that would have been misdirected.
