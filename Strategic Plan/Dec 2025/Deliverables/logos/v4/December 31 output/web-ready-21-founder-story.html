The $150,000 We Told a Client NOT to Spend

Certainty First.

It started with a $5,000 engagement where I told a client NOT to build.

They came to me with a vision—a software product they were convinced would change their industry. They'd already gotten quotes from development shops. The cheapest estimate was $150,000. They were ready to sign.

I spent two weeks digging in. Analyzing the market. Talking to potential customers. Running the numbers. And at the end of it, I delivered the hardest advice a consultant can give: "Don't build this."

The market wasn't there. The unit economics didn't work. Building would have drained their runway on a bet that the data said wouldn't pay off. They paid me $5,000 and saved themselves $150,000 or more. That engagement changed how I think about my work.

"The best advice isn't always the advice that gets me paid more. Sometimes the right call is telling someone to walk away."

Why I Started ScaleUp Ventures

I founded ScaleUp Ventures because I kept seeing the same pattern: founders with genuine ambition being served poorly by the people they hired to help them. Agencies that churned out deliverables without caring about outcomes. Consultants who told clients what they wanted to hear instead of what they needed to know.

I wanted to build something different. A consultancy that earns trust through honesty, not just activity. One that measures success by whether clients actually succeed—not by how many hours we bill.

We're still early-stage. I'm not going to pretend we're a massive firm with decades of case studies. But what we lack in scale, we make up for in focus and integrity.

Calvin Williams Jr.

Founder & CEO

The Work We Do

Our engagements range across the early-stage spectrum. We've helped startups with architecture, prototypes, business model refinement, and investor introductions. We've built proof-of-concept applications in regulated industries like financial trading. We've designed AI-enabled project management infrastructure for solutions firms.

And sometimes, like that first engagement, we tell people not to build at all.

We practice what we preach. We're building our own product—OfferPath—because we believe consultants should understand firsthand what it means to ship, to find customers, to iterate based on feedback. It keeps us honest. It keeps us sharp.

"We don't just advise founders. We are founders. That changes how we show up."

Why the Heron

People ask about our symbol—the heron. It wasn't chosen by a branding agency running focus groups. It emerged from thinking about patience and precision.

Watch a heron hunt. It stands motionless at the water's edge, sometimes for hours. It doesn't chase. It doesn't flail. It waits, observes, calculates. And when it strikes, it doesn't miss.

That's the philosophy we aim to embody. Patience before precision. Observation before action. Certainty before commitment. In a world of consultants promising instant results and hockey-stick growth, we chose the bird that knows the value of waiting for the right moment.

The heron doesn't guarantee a catch. But when it moves, it moves with data—the angle of the light, the movement of the water, the position of the fish. Certainty, not hope.

Where We're Going

ScaleUp Ventures is early in its own journey. We're building our reputation one engagement at a time, one referral at a time. Every client we help—whether that means building with them or advising them to hold off—adds to the body of work that defines who we are.

We're not the right fit for everyone. If you want a consultant who will tell you what you want to hear, we're not it. If you want flashy proposals and impressive-sounding jargon, look elsewhere. But if you want a partner who will be straight with you—even when the honest answer is "don't do this"—we should talk.

That no-build decision still defines how I think about this work. It would have been easy to take the bigger engagement, to help them build something and collect fees along the way. But that's not why I started this company.

Certainty first. It's not just a tagline. It's a commitment to honesty over revenue, to outcomes over activity. It's the standard we hold ourselves to.

— Calvin Williams Jr.
Founder & CEO, ScaleUp Ventures
