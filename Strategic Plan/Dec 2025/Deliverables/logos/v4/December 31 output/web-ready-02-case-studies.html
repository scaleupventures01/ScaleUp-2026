The Situation

A Client Ready to Invest Six Figures in the Wrong Direction

A client approached us with a vision for a custom web application. They had budgeted $150,000 or more for development and were ready to commit 12-18 months to the project. They wanted us to build it.

Instead, we proposed a $5,000 discovery and feasibility analysis. Most consultancies would have happily taken the larger project. The client was eager, funded, and asking for exactly what we offer. But our Two-Track ROI model demands we prove it before you buy - and if we can't show a high-probability positive outcome, we recommend not proceeding.

The Discovery

When the Three-Scenario Analysis Shows "Don't Build"

For Track 2 (App & Development Projects), we create three-scenario ROI projections showing return in cash OR company/asset value across high, mid, and low probability outcomes. In this case, even our high scenario couldn't justify the investment.

We spent four weeks examining their market, their assumptions, their competitive landscape, and the technical requirements they had outlined. Our analysis revealed that the market they wanted to enter was more saturated than they realized. The technical approach they envisioned would require ongoing maintenance costs they had not budgeted for. And critically, there were existing solutions that could accomplish 80% of their goals at a fraction of the cost.

We presented our findings honestly: building this application would be a mistake. The client would likely spend $150,000 or more and 12+ months only to discover what we found in four weeks of focused analysis.

  • 1Three-Scenario ROI Analysis: Modeled high/mid/low return scenarios showing value in cash and asset terms
  • 2Market Analysis: Evaluated competitive landscape and demand signals
  • 3Technical Assessment: Mapped architecture requirements and ongoing costs
  • 4Alternative Identification: Found existing solutions that addressed core needs
  • 5Honest Recommendation: No high-probability positive outcome - advised against the build

Financial services is one of the most demanding domains in software development. Compliance isn't optional. Security must be bulletproof. Every feature must work within strict regulatory frameworks. Most development teams can't operate at this level - and most can't prove ROI before you commit.

The Approach

Two-Track ROI: App Development + Ad Campaigns
