3How to Present the Model to Prospects

The ROI model presentation is a pivotal moment in our sales process. Follow this structured approach to maximize impact and build trust.

Presentation Structure (45-60 minutes)

  1. Context Setting (5 min): Recap their challenges and goals from discovery. Confirm nothing has changed since data collection.
  2. Methodology Overview (10 min): Explain how we built the model, what data we used, and our calculation approach. Build credibility through transparency.
  3. Key Findings (15 min): Present the headline numbers: ROI, payback period, net benefit. Use visuals and walk through the logic.
  4. Sensitivity Analysis (10 min): Show best-case, base-case, and conservative scenarios. Demonstrate that even conservative projections show positive returns.
  5. Risk Discussion (10 min): Proactively address risks and assumptions. Show mitigation strategies. This builds trust through honesty.
  6. Q&A and Next Steps (10 min): Answer questions, confirm understanding, and propose clear next steps.

Presentation Best Practices

Do:

  • Start with their goals, not our solution
  • Use their actual numbers in examples
  • Acknowledge uncertainty transparently
  • Let them adjust inputs live to see impact
  • Connect every benefit to their stated pain points
  • Leave time for questions throughout

Don't:

  • Oversell or use aggressive projections
  • Rush through the methodology section
  • Dismiss concerns or skip risk discussion
  • Use jargon without explanation
  • Present without decision-makers present
  • Leave without clear next steps

6Payback Period Calculation Methodology

Payback period is one of the most intuitive ROI metrics for decision-makers. Here is how we calculate and present it.

Standard Payback Period Formula
Payback Period = Total Investment / Monthly Net Benefit
Where Monthly Net Benefit = (Gross Monthly Benefit - Ongoing Monthly Costs) x Adoption Rate

7Risk Factors and Assumptions Section

Every ROI model includes explicit assumptions and identified risks. This transparency builds trust and sets realistic expectations.

Standard Risk Assessment

Risk Factor Impact Level Mitigation Strategy Owner
Adoption resistance from staff Medium Change management program, executive sponsorship Client + SVU
Integration complexity exceeds estimate Medium Technical discovery phase, buffer in timeline SVU
Key stakeholder departure High Multi-stakeholder buy-in, documentation Client
Market conditions change Low Quarterly review and model updates Joint
Data quality issues discovered Medium Data audit phase, remediation budget Joint
Scope creep during implementation Low Clear SOW, change control process Joint

Key Assumptions

Business Environment Assumptions

  • No major organizational restructuring during implementation
  • Key decision-makers remain in place for project duration
  • Budget remains allocated as approved
  • Market conditions remain within normal variance
  • Regulatory environment does not fundamentally change

Technical Assumptions

  • Existing systems can integrate via documented APIs
  • Data quality is sufficient for migration
  • Infrastructure can support new workloads
  • Security requirements are as documented
  • No legacy system lock-in prevents changes

Benefit Realization Assumptions

  • Staff will be redeployed to value-add work (not headcount reduction)
  • Training will be completed within planned timeline
  • Adoption curve follows industry benchmarks (conservative)
  • Benefits are measured and tracked as defined
  • Only quantifiable benefits are included (qualitative benefits are bonus)

Sensitivity Range: Our base-case projections assume all assumptions hold. Conservative scenarios assume 2-3 risks materialize. Even in conservative scenarios, we target minimum 150% ROI for project approval.

10FAQ About the ROI Model

Common questions prospects ask about our ROI modeling approach, with recommended responses.

Why should I trust these projections? Aren't all consultants' projections optimistic?

It's a fair question, and honestly, many firms do over-promise. Here's why we're different: First, we build ROI models before you pay us anything - we're investing our time to prove value. If we weren't confident in our projections, that would be a terrible business model. Second, every number is documented with its source and methodology - there's nothing hidden. Third, we include a conservative scenario that assumes some things go wrong, and even that shows positive returns. Finally, we tie our fees to outcomes - if we don't deliver, we don't get paid in full.

How accurate are these projections compared to actual results?

Across our client base, 85% of engagements meet or exceed the base-case projections within the first 18 months. About 10% land between the conservative and base case - still positive, but lower than projected. Only 5% underperform the conservative scenario, and in most of those cases, there were major business disruptions (acquisitions, leadership changes) that we couldn't have anticipated. We publish anonymized case studies with actual results, and we're happy to share references from clients in your industry.

What data do you need from us to build this model?

We need three categories of data: Financial (last 12 months P&L, ideally by department or function), Operational (headcount, process metrics, technology spending), and Strategic (growth targets, key initiatives, pain points you've identified). We can work with whatever level of detail you're comfortable sharing - we sign NDAs, and we've built models with as little as a summary P&L and a 30-minute interview. More data means more precision, but we can start with less.

What if our situation changes after we start?

The model is a living document. We update it quarterly during engagements as we learn more and as your situation evolves. If there's a major change (acquisition, new leadership, market shift), we'll rerun the model with new assumptions and discuss whether to adjust scope, timeline, or approach. The goal is always positive ROI - we're partners in achieving that, not just contractors delivering a spec.

How long does it take to build our custom ROI model?

Typically 1-2 weeks from receiving your data to presenting the model. The timeline depends on data availability and stakeholder access for interviews. We've done it in as little as 3 business days for urgent situations, but we prefer 2 weeks to ensure thoroughness. This is our gift to you - there's no charge for the ROI model, so there's no reason to rush it at the expense of quality.

What if the model shows negative ROI?

Then we tell you that, and we don't proceed. This has happened - about 1 in 10 prospects we model aren't a fit for an engagement with us. Maybe the timing is wrong, maybe the problem is different than assumed, maybe the investment doesn't make sense for your situation. When that happens, we share the model anyway, explain why we think it's not a fit, and often suggest alternative approaches that might work better. Our reputation depends on honest assessment, not on closing every deal.

Can we share this model with our board/investors?

Absolutely - in fact, we encourage it. The model is designed to be presentation-ready for any stakeholder audience. We can also join calls with your board or investors to walk through the methodology and answer questions directly. We've found that having a third-party ROI analysis actually accelerates internal approval processes.
