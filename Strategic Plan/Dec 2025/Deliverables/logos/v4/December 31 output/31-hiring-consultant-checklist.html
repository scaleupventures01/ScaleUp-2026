What to Ask Before Hiring Any Consultant

The Due Diligence Checklist for Business Owners

18 essential questions that separate trustworthy consultants from those who will waste your time and money. Based on insights from business owners who learned the hard way.

The 18 Questions

Print this. Bring it to every meeting. Take notes on their answers. The patterns will become clear quickly.

1 Track Record and Evidence

1 Can you show me three businesses similar to mine that you've helped, and what specific results did you achieve?

Why This Matters

Generalists rarely understand the specific challenges of your industry. A consultant who has solved problems like yours before can hit the ground running instead of learning on your dime.

Red Flags
  • Vague references to "various industries"
  • Unable to provide specific names or metrics
  • References are all from 5+ years ago
  • "We work with companies of all sizes"
Green Flags
  • Specific company names with permission to contact
  • Concrete metrics: revenue, cost savings, timeline
  • References from the past 24 months
  • Clear explanation of their role vs. team effort

How ScaleUp Answers This

"We work exclusively with established businesses doing $2M-$20M in revenue. Here are three recent clients in your sector, with their permission to share specific growth metrics. We'll also connect you directly with their founders if you'd like an unfiltered conversation."

2 What was your biggest failure with a client, and what did you learn from it?

Why This Matters

Anyone can cherry-pick successes. How they handle failure reveals their character, self-awareness, and ability to learn. A consultant who claims no failures is either lying or hasn't done enough work to encounter reality.

Red Flags
  • "We haven't really had failures"
  • Blames the client entirely
  • Gets defensive or dismissive
  • Story is vague and lacks specifics
Green Flags
  • Honest about what went wrong
  • Takes appropriate responsibility
  • Articulates specific lessons learned
  • Explains process changes implemented since

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Our biggest learning came from a manufacturing client where we moved too fast before understanding their internal politics. The strategy was right, but we underestimated resistance. That's why we now run a 90-day pilot before any major initiative, letting results build internal champions."

3 May I speak with a client where things didn't go perfectly?

Why This Matters

Reference lists are curated. A consultant willing to let you speak with someone who experienced challenges demonstrates confidence and transparency. How they handled difficulties matters more than perfection.

Red Flags
  • "All our clients are happy"
  • Only offers glowing testimonials
  • Hesitates or makes excuses
  • References are clearly coached
Green Flags
  • Immediately offers a reference
  • Provides context about the challenge
  • Client confirms they'd work together again
  • Shows pattern of resolving difficulties well

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Absolutely. Let me connect you with [Client Name], where our initial approach needed recalibration. They'll tell you how we handled that pivot and why they extended our engagement. Their honest perspective is more valuable than any polished testimonial."

2 Methodology and Approach

4 Walk me through your actual process, step by step, from signing to results.

Why This Matters

A clear methodology means they've done this enough to systematize it. Vague descriptions suggest they're making it up as they go. You're paying for proven process, not improvisation.

Red Flags
  • "It depends on the situation"
  • Heavy on buzzwords, light on specifics
  • Can't articulate clear phases or milestones
  • Process seems to start with strategy decks
Green Flags
  • Named methodology with documented steps
  • Clear timeline with specific milestones
  • Defined deliverables at each stage
  • Client responsibilities clearly outlined

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Phase 1 is a 2-week diagnostic where we analyze your operations, financials, and market position. Phase 2 is a 90-day pilot implementing our top recommendation with measurable KPIs. Only if that pilot proves ROI do we discuss Phase 3 expansion. You have full data at every decision point."

5 How will you learn our business before prescribing solutions?

Why This Matters

Consultants who arrive with pre-packaged solutions don't actually consult; they sell. Your business has nuances that generic frameworks miss. The discovery process reveals whether they're genuinely curious or just going through motions.

Red Flags
  • Already suggesting solutions in first meeting
  • Brief questionnaire is their only discovery
  • Focus on selling their service, not understanding you
  • "We've seen this before" without asking questions
Green Flags
  • Deep discovery before any recommendations
  • Want to talk to your team, not just leadership
  • Ask about failed initiatives and why
  • Require access to real data and metrics

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Before we recommend anything, we spend two weeks inside your business. We interview your leadership, front-line staff, and key customers. We analyze your financials, operations, and competitive position. Only then do we share findings, and we won't recommend anything we can't prove will work."

6 What happens if your approach isn't working after 90 days?

Why This Matters

Every consultant sounds confident at the start. The real test is what happens when things don't go as planned. Their answer reveals whether you're protected or whether you'll be left holding the bag.

Red Flags
  • "That won't happen with our methodology"
  • No defined checkpoints or exit ramps
  • Long-term contracts with no performance clauses
  • Blame-shifting language in contract terms
Green Flags
  • Clear KPIs defined before starting
  • Regular check-ins with go/no-go decisions
  • Money-back or adjustment clauses
  • Process for recalibrating if needed

How ScaleUp Answers This

"We define success metrics before we start. At 90 days, we review together. If we're not hitting targets, we either adjust approach at no additional cost, or you can exit with a prorated refund. We've been doing this long enough to know confidence requires accountability."

3 Who Actually Does the Work

7 Who exactly will be working on my account, and what is their experience?

Why This Matters

The classic bait-and-switch: senior partners sell the work, junior staff deliver it. You need to know who will actually be solving your problems, not just who shows up for the pitch.

Red Flags
  • "We'll assign the right team"
  • Can't name specific people
  • Team is mostly recent graduates
  • Heavy reliance on subcontractors
Green Flags
  • Named individuals with bios
  • You meet the delivery team before signing
  • Contract specifies who works on account
  • Principal/partner involvement guaranteed

How ScaleUp Answers This

"You're meeting the team who does the work. There's no B-team that gets swapped in after you sign. I'll be your primary point of contact throughout, and you can specify in the contract which team members are assigned. Any changes require your approval."

8 How many other clients is my team working on simultaneously?

Why This Matters

Overloaded consultants can't give your business the attention it needs. If they're juggling eight clients, you're getting 12% of their focus. Capacity constraints explain many consulting failures.

Red Flags
  • Evasive or vague about workload
  • Consistently slow response times
  • "We scale as needed"
  • Account managers handle many clients
Green Flags
  • Transparent about current client load
  • Maximum client-per-person policy
  • Defined hours allocated to your account
  • Will pause new business if at capacity

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Each principal works with a maximum of four clients at any time. Your contract specifies minimum weekly hours. We've turned down business when at capacity because diluted attention serves no one. Quality requires focus."

9 What happens if my main contact leaves your firm?

Why This Matters

Consulting firms have turnover. If your relationship and institutional knowledge walks out the door, you're starting over with someone new while still paying full price.

Red Flags
  • No transition plan discussed
  • All knowledge in one person's head
  • No documentation or knowledge sharing
  • History of high turnover
Green Flags
  • Documented processes and learnings
  • Multiple team members know your account
  • Transition clause in contract
  • Long tenure record at the firm

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Every engagement has documented playbooks and shared knowledge across our team. If any team member departs, you have a 30-day transition period at no charge. But frankly, we're a small firm with minimal turnover because we only take work we believe in."

4 Money and Metrics

10 What is the total investment, including all potential additional costs?

Why This Matters

The initial proposal is often just the tip of the iceberg. Add-ons, scope creep charges, tool licenses, and "unexpected" fees can double your investment. You need the full picture upfront.

Red Flags
  • Quote only covers "base" engagement
  • Vague language about additional phases
  • Tool or software costs not included
  • Travel, expenses, or "out of scope" warnings
Green Flags
  • All-inclusive pricing clearly stated
  • Fixed-price options available
  • Any variable costs have caps
  • No surprise fees in past client history

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Our proposals are all-inclusive. The number you see includes all team time, tools, travel, and deliverables. We don't nickel-and-dime. If scope genuinely expands beyond what we agreed, we'll discuss before any additional charges. No surprises on your invoice."

11 How will we measure success, and when will we know if it's working?

Why This Matters

Without defined metrics, success becomes subjective. "We improved your brand awareness" is meaningless. You need specific, measurable outcomes with clear timelines that both parties agree to upfront.

Red Flags
  • Soft metrics only: "engagement," "awareness"
  • Results promised at vague future date
  • No baseline measurement established
  • "Success is a journey, not a destination"
Green Flags
  • Specific KPIs defined before starting
  • Baseline measurement established first
  • Regular reporting schedule specified
  • Clear timeline for expected results

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Before we start, we establish 3-5 specific KPIs together. We baseline your current state in week one. You get monthly dashboards showing progress. At 90 days, we have a formal review against targets. We only succeed if those numbers move, not if we deliver pretty reports."

12 What is your fee structure, and is there any performance-based component?

Why This Matters

If a consultant won't put any skin in the game, ask why. Purely time-based billing means they profit whether you succeed or not. Some performance linkage aligns incentives.

Red Flags
  • Hourly only with no ceiling
  • Refuses any performance-based option
  • Retainer with vague deliverables
  • Long-term lock-in with no exit clause
Green Flags
  • Offers performance-based components
  • Fixed-fee options for defined scope
  • Capped hours with clear billing
  • Flexible terms based on results

How ScaleUp Answers This

"We offer three structures: fixed project fee, monthly retainer with caps, or a hybrid with base fee plus performance bonus tied to your KPIs. We believe enough in our work to put our fee at risk. Let's design an arrangement where we only win when you win."

5 Process and Communication

13 How often will we communicate, and what does your reporting look like?

Why This Matters

Consultants who disappear between deliverables leave you in the dark. You need to know what's happening with your investment. Regular, substantive communication prevents surprises and builds trust.

Red Flags
  • Monthly calls only with slide decks
  • Reports heavy on activity, light on impact
  • Difficult to reach between meetings
  • Status updates feel generic
Green Flags
  • Weekly check-ins plus as-needed access
  • Dashboards with real-time metrics
  • Prompt responses to questions
  • Proactive problem identification

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Weekly 30-minute syncs as a baseline. You'll have our mobile numbers for urgent matters. Monthly written reports show metrics against targets, not just activity logs. We believe you should never wonder what we're doing or why."

14 What do you need from us to be successful?

Why This Matters

Good consultants know they can't succeed in a vacuum. They need access, decisions, and cooperation. A consultant who doesn't set expectations is either naive or setting up excuses for failure.

Red Flags
  • "We'll handle everything"
  • No discussion of client responsibilities
  • Vague about access or decision-making
  • Surprises you with requests mid-project
Green Flags
  • Clear list of what they need from you
  • Realistic time expectations for your team
  • Specific access requirements defined
  • Decision points mapped to timeline

How ScaleUp Answers This

"We'll need a decision-maker with authority available weekly. Access to your financial systems and key team members for interviews. Decisions on our recommendations within one week. We document all of this in the engagement letter so there's no confusion about roles."

15 What happens to the work when the engagement ends?

Why This Matters

Some consultants create dependency by design. If their frameworks, tools, and processes require their ongoing involvement, you're not building capability; you're renting it. You should own the outcomes.

Red Flags
  • Proprietary systems only they can run
  • No knowledge transfer component
  • Ongoing licensing fees required
  • Deliverables are "consultant-speak"
Green Flags
  • You own all work product
  • Training included for your team
  • Documentation for self-sufficiency
  • Gradual handover built into timeline

How ScaleUp Answers This

"You own everything we create. Period. The last month of any engagement includes training your team to run the playbooks without us. Our goal is to make ourselves unnecessary. If you need us again, it should be by choice, not dependency."

6 The Questions That Reveal Everything

16 Why should we hire you instead of your competitors?

Why This Matters

This question reveals self-awareness and honesty. Consultants who trash competitors lack class. Those who can't articulate differentiation don't know their own value. The best answer is specific, fair, and confident.

Red Flags
  • Attacks competitors by name
  • Generic claims: "better service," "more experienced"
  • Can't name actual competitors
  • Focuses on their size or age alone
Green Flags
  • Acknowledges competitors' strengths
  • Specific differentiation with evidence
  • Honest about when they're not the best fit
  • Focuses on unique value, not superiority

How ScaleUp Answers This

"There are excellent consultants out there. What makes us different is our proof-first model. We don't ask you to trust a strategy deck. We run a pilot, show you results, and only then discuss expansion. Most firms won't structure engagements this way. If that matters to you, we're a fit. If you want bold promises upfront, there are others who specialize in that."

17 When have you told a potential client they shouldn't hire you?

Why This Matters

A consultant who takes every client is either desperate or indiscriminate. The best consultants know their sweet spot and will decline work that doesn't fit. This protects both parties.

Red Flags
  • "We can help anyone"
  • No clear ideal client profile
  • Can't recall turning down business
  • Desperate closing behavior
Green Flags
  • Specific examples of declined work
  • Clear criteria for fit/no-fit
  • Would refer you elsewhere if not right
  • No pressure to close quickly

How ScaleUp Answers This

"Last month, actually. Great company, but they wanted us to validate a strategy their board already approved. We don't do rubber-stamping. We also decline startups, turnarounds, and businesses under $2M revenue. Not because we can't help, but because we've learned where we deliver the most value. If we take you on, it's because we're confident we can deliver."

18 Can we do a small pilot project before committing to a larger engagement?

Why This Matters

This is the ultimate test. A consultant confident in their value will welcome the chance to prove it. Those who only want large commitments upfront may be more focused on revenue than results.

Red Flags
  • "Our methodology requires full engagement"
  • Minimum engagement is 6-12 months
  • Reluctance to prove value first
  • Pressure tactics for larger commitment
Green Flags
  • Enthusiastic about pilot approach
  • Suggests specific pilot scope
  • Success metrics defined for pilot
  • No pressure to expand before ready

How ScaleUp Answers This

"That's exactly how we prefer to work. In fact, it's how we insist on working. Our standard engagement starts with a 90-day pilot on a single initiative. We define success metrics together, execute, and only then discuss broader scope. We believe in earning your confidence, not assuming it."

How to Score Your Consultant

For each question, give 1 point for each green flag answer, 0 for neutral, and subtract 1 for each red flag. Add up your total.

0-6 Points
Walk Away
Too many red flags. This consultant lacks transparency, accountability, or relevant experience. Keep looking.
7-12 Points
Proceed with Caution
Some green flags, but concerns remain. Address specific issues directly. Consider a smaller pilot with clear metrics.
13-18 Points
Strong Candidate
Demonstrates transparency, accountability, and relevant experience. A partnership worth exploring further.
