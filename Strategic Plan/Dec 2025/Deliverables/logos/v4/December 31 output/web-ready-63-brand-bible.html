Two-Track ROI Model

"We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you."

The Two-Track Framework

Our Two-Track ROI Model is the operational backbone of "Certainty First." Every engagement falls into one of two tracks, each with its own accountability structure designed to eliminate risk for established business owners.

Core Philosophy: If we cannot demonstrate clear ROI potential using one of these two frameworks, we will tell you directly and decline the engagement. We would rather lose a sale than damage our reputation for proof-first recommendations.

1

Track 1: Ad Campaigns

Guaranteed Lead Numbers

For marketing and advertising engagements, we quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads BEFORE starting to ensure expected results meet client needs. If we miss the target, our fee is refunded.

What's Guaranteed
  • + Projected ad spend quantified upfront
  • + Projected lead volume targets agreed before starting
  • + Expected results validated against client needs
  • + Lead quality criteria defined before launch
If We Miss

Our management fee is refunded in full. You only pay for the ad spend itself. We share the risk because we believe in our work.

Note: Ad spend is not refunded as it goes directly to platforms (Google, Meta, etc.). Our fee refund policy applies to our management/strategy fees only.

2

Track 2: App & Development Projects

Three-Scenario ROI Projections

For development work, we build comprehensive ROI models showing high, mid, and low scenarios for your investment return.

HIGH
Optimistic
MID
Expected
LOW
Conservative
Return Measured In
  • $ Cash ROI: Direct revenue generation, cost savings, efficiency gains
  • + Company/Asset Value: Increased valuation, IP creation, strategic positioning for exit
Before You Spend a Dollar

You'll see a detailed model using your actual business numbers showing exactly how the investment pays back under each scenario.

How to Present the Two-Track Model

Context Lead With Key Message
Initial Sales Call "We prove it before you buy" Introduce the concept of two tracks - they'll fit into one based on their needs
Ad Campaign Prospects Fee refund guarantee "If we don't hit the lead numbers we agree on, you get our fee back"
Development Prospects Three-scenario model "We'll show you the math - high, mid, and low - before you commit"
Skeptical Prospects "If we can't prove it, we'll tell you" We decline engagements where we can't demonstrate clear ROI potential

Approved Language

Use These Phrases
  • "We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you."
  • "For ad campaigns, we guarantee the lead numbers or refund our fee."
  • "For development projects, we model three scenarios so you see the range of outcomes."
  • "You'll see the ROI in cash savings OR increased company value - often both."
  • "If the numbers don't work, we'll tell you before you spend a dollar."
Avoid These Phrases
  • "Guaranteed ROI" (too broad - be specific about what's guaranteed)
  • "Risk-free" (nothing is entirely risk-free)
  • "Full refund" without specifying it's the fee only (for Track 1)
  • "We guarantee results" without specifying which track/terms
  • "10x ROI" or specific multipliers without modeling

Track Selection Criteria

Use this framework to quickly identify which track applies to a prospect's needs:

Track 1 (Ad Campaigns) When:
  • • Primary goal is lead generation
  • • Success is measurable in lead volume/cost
  • • Client has sales capacity to handle leads
  • • Timeframe is 3-6 months
Track 2 (Development) When:
  • • Building apps, tools, or automation
  • • ROI comes from efficiency or valuation
  • • Client considering exit in 2-5 years
  • • Strategic investment mindset

Business owner aged 45-65, built a successful company over 15-30 years. Revenue typically $2M-$20M. Has decision-making authority and budget for significant investments ($20K+). Respects competence over charisma.

Before You Create Content

Ask Yourself
  • Would a skeptical 60-year-old trust this?
  • Can I prove this statement?
  • Does this respect their intelligence?
  • Am I being direct or just pushy?
  • Would I want to receive this?
Remember
  • Evidence over enthusiasm
  • Precision over personality
  • Patience over pressure
  • Clarity over cleverness
  • Respect over rapport
The Test
  • Read it aloud
  • Cut 20% of the words
  • Remove any word you can't defend
  • Add specific numbers where possible
  • Delete all exclamation points
