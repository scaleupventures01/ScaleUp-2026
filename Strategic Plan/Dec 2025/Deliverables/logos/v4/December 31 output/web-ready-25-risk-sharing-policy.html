What Risk-Sharing Looks Like

Risk-sharing isn't one-size-fits-all. We tailor the structure to each engagement based on scope, timeline, and what success looks like for your specific situation.

Milestone-Based Payments

For larger engagements, we tie payment milestones to deliverable completion and agreed checkpoints. You don't pay for work that isn't done or doesn't meet the brief.

Success-Based Components

Where measurable outcomes can be defined upfront, we're open to structuring a portion of our fee around achieving them. The specifics depend on the engagement type.

Honest Recommendations

Sometimes the right advice is "don't build this" or "this isn't the right time." We've told clients not to proceed when the data pointed that direction, even when it meant less work for us.

Common Questions

How does this work in practice?

The specific risk-sharing terms are defined in each engagement's Statement of Work. We discuss what success looks like, how we'll measure it, and what's at stake for both of us. Every engagement is different, but the principle is consistent: aligned incentives.

Are you just a new firm trying to win business?

Yes, honestly. We're early-stage and we need to earn trust. But that's exactly why risk-sharing makes sense for both of us. You get reduced risk working with a newer firm, and we get the opportunity to prove ourselves. If we deliver, we build reputation. If we don't, we've earned nothing.

What kinds of projects are a good fit?

We work best on projects with clear deliverables: discovery assessments, prototype development, proof of concepts, architecture design, and strategic analysis. We're less suited for open-ended advisory relationships or ongoing staff augmentation.

What if my project doesn't fit your risk-sharing model?

Not every engagement needs performance-based fees. For some projects, straightforward milestone billing makes more sense. We're flexible on structure as long as there's clarity on deliverables and mutual agreement on what constitutes success.

Why should I trust a new consultancy?

You shouldn't trust us based on claims. That's the point. Start with a small engagement. See how we work. Judge us by what we deliver, not what we promise. Our risk-sharing approach means you have less to lose by giving us that chance.
