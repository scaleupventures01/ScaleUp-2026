Speaker Notes

Title Slide

Take a moment to personalize: client name and date should be filled in before presenting.

Key opening: "Thank you for taking time to explore what a partnership with ScaleUp could look like."

  • Make eye contact, gauge their energy
  • This sets the tone - be confident but not aggressive
  • If appropriate, reference something specific you know about their business

The Problem

This slide is about EMPATHY. You're describing their experience, not selling.

Pause after "shouldn't feel like gambling" - let it land.

  • Watch for head nods - which points resonate most?
  • If they interrupt with their own story, LET THEM. That's gold.
  • Don't rush through the bullets - each is a potential conversation starter

Transition: "The reason you feel this way isn't your fault..."

Cost of Uncertainty

This slide is about empathy and shared experience. No fake stats - just real talk.

The quote at the bottom is your moment of connection - deliver it personally.

  • Let them tell their story if they want to - this is where trust starts
  • Don't oversell the pain - they already know it. Just acknowledge it honestly.
  • The hesitation to trust is real and valid - respect it

Transition: "We built ScaleUp specifically to solve this problem..."

Who We Serve

Establish credibility by showing you know their world.

Highlight the industry that matches their business - show genuine understanding.

  • If they're not in these industries, acknowledge it: "We serve others too, but these are where we have the deepest expertise"
  • The revenue/employee range validates that you work with businesses like theirs
  • Ask: "Does this describe where you are today?"

Certainty First Philosophy

This is your CORE DIFFERENTIATOR. Take your time.

"Certainty First" - let this hang in the air. It's the answer to everything they've struggled with.

  • No Guesswork: "You'll know the numbers before you sign"
  • No Surprises: "Everything is documented, nothing is hidden"
  • No Excuses: "If we don't deliver, we own it"

Transition: "Let me show you exactly how we deliver on this promise..."

The Proof Test

This is WHERE you explain HOW you deliver certainty.

Emphasize: "You get this BEFORE you invest anything."

  • Stress that the ROI model uses THEIR actual numbers, not industry averages
  • Why we do this: "It protects both of us. If we can't show a path to ROI, we tell you upfront."
  • This builds trust - you're willing to walk away from bad-fit deals

How It Works

Keep this high-level - details come in individual service slides.

Three simple steps. Don't overcomplicate.

  • Discover: "We do the work to understand YOUR business"
  • Prove: "You see the math before you commit"
  • Deliver: "We execute and keep you informed every step"

Ask: "Which of these steps would be most valuable to walk through in detail?"

Business Development

Focus on OUTCOMES, not features.

The question to answer: "Will this make me money?"

  • Don't get lost in technical details unless they ask
  • Higher conversion = more customers from same traffic = more revenue
  • If they have an existing site, acknowledge it and focus on improvement potential

Growth Marketing

Position as predictable lead generation, not creative marketing.

Key phrase: "Predictable lead flow you can count on"

  • Many SMBs have been burned by agencies that focused on vanity metrics
  • Emphasize: qualified leads that close, not just clicks and impressions
  • Monthly reporting = no surprises, complete visibility

AI Automation

This is often the "wow" slide. Use it wisely.

Focus on TIME savings - that's what resonates with busy owners.

  • "Hours reclaimed" = time back with family, time for strategy, time for what matters
  • Acknowledge AI can feel intimidating - we handle the complexity
  • Common question: "Will this replace my people?" Answer: "No, it empowers them"

  • This story builds trust faster than any case study with metrics
  • The punchline: "We'd rather tell you the truth than take your money"

Building What We Believe In

Custom ROI Model

This is a PLACEHOLDER. Explain what will go here.

"This is where YOUR numbers go. Let me show you what we'll calculate together."

  • Walk through each metric placeholder briefly
  • Emphasize: "These will be based on YOUR actual data, not industry guesses"
  • Set up the discovery session: "This is what we'll build in step 2"

Investment

Be direct about money. Evasiveness kills trust.

The guarantee is the key: "If we can't show you a path to positive return, we tell you."

  • Project-based: Good for defined deliverables (new website, specific campaign)
  • Retainer: Good for ongoing optimization and growth
  • Don't apologize for pricing - these are starting points, not exact quotes

Why ScaleUp

Summarize your differentiators. This is your positioning statement.

"ROI Before Investment" - this is the #1 differentiator. Lead with it.

  • Don't trash competitors - focus on what makes you different
  • Founder-led: You're not handed off to juniors after the sale
  • Selective: We say no to projects we can't confidently deliver

Meet the Team

Make this personal. People buy from people.

The message: Senior expertise, not agency overhead.

  • Share something authentic about your background
  • Expert network: "We bring in specialists based on YOUR needs"
  • This is a good time to ask about their team structure

Next Steps

Clear call to action. Don't leave this ambiguous.

Ask directly: "Are you ready to schedule the discovery session?"

  • 30 min discovery is low commitment - emphasize that
  • 48 hours for ROI model shows you move fast
  • If they hesitate, ask what questions they still have
  • Have your calendar ready to book on the spot

Closing: "Thank you for your time. What questions do you have?"
