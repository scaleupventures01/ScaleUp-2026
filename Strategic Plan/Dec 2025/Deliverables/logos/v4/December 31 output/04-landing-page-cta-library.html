◆ CTA Best Practices for Skeptical B2B Audiences

Language That Works

  • "Explore" over "Buy" or "Get Started"
  • "Discuss" over "Schedule Sales Call"
  • "Assessment" or "Audit" over "Free Trial"
  • "Discover" over "Learn" or "Find Out"
  • Specific numbers over vague claims
  • "Owners like you" for peer positioning

Trust-Building Tactics

  • Set clear expectations (response time, call length)
  • Acknowledge they might not be a fit
  • Promise no aggressive follow-up
  • Show specific client numbers (47 owners, not "many")
  • Use industry-specific language when applicable
  • Offer value before asking for commitment

Button Design Principles

  • Primary: Gold (#D4A853) on Navy background
  • Secondary: Navy outline on light background
  • Ghost: Gold text with underline for inline
  • Minimum 44px height for touch targets
  • Clear hover states that increase contrast
  • Consistent padding: 12px 24px minimum

Placement Guidelines

  • Primary CTA: Above fold + page bottom
  • Secondary: After major content sections
  • Inline: Every 2-3 scroll heights
  • Exit-intent: Max 1 per session
  • Mobile: Consider sticky footer CTA
  • Never more than 2 CTA types visible at once

A/B Testing Priority

  • 1. Headline copy (benefit vs. problem framing)
  • 2. Button text (action verb choice)
  • 3. Supporting text (with vs. without)
  • 4. Button color/style
  • 5. Placement position
  • Run tests for min. 2 weeks or 100+ conversions

Audience-Specific Notes (30-55)

  • Larger button text (16px minimum)
  • High contrast color combinations
  • Clear, traditional button shapes
  • Explicit benefit statements
  • Avoid trendy/startup language
  • Emphasize experience and track record
