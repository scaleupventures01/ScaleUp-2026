A/B Test Framework

A systematic approach to testing CTAs. Start with high-traffic, high-impact elements. Use statistical rigor to validate results.

1 What to Test First

  • Homepage hero CTA Highest Priority
  • Pricing page CTA High Priority
  • Email subject lines High Priority
  • Landing page headlines Medium
  • Form submit buttons Medium
  • Blog CTAs Medium

2 Sample Sizes Needed

  • Detect 20% lift ~400 conversions/variant
  • Detect 30% lift ~200 conversions/variant
  • Detect 50% lift ~80 conversions/variant
  • Min traffic/variant 1,000 visitors
  • Email min sends 500 per variant

3 Test Duration Rules

  • Minimum runtime 7 days (full week)
  • Ideal runtime 14-21 days
  • Max runtime 30 days
  • Don't stop early if Winner looks clear
  • Account for Day-of-week variance

4 Statistical Significance

  • Target confidence 95% minimum
  • Enterprise decisions 99% preferred
  • Accept inconclusive If <10% difference
  • Retest threshold 85-94% confidence
  • Tool recommendation VWO or Optimizely

Documenting Your Results

When you complete a test, update the template with:

  • Duration: How many days the test ran
  • Sample Size: Total visitors/recipients per variant
  • CTR/Conversion Rate: Actual measured performance
  • Statistical Confidence: Use a calculator to verify significance
  • Key Insight: What you learned that applies to future tests

Build your own data library over time. Real results from your audience are more valuable than industry benchmarks.

Best Practices

Principles for effective CTA design in consultancy contexts. Apply these when creating and testing new CTAs for ScaleUp Ventures.

1

Honesty Builds Trust

As an early-stage consultancy, transparency is your differentiator. CTAs that signal honest assessment outperform pushy sales language with founders who are wary of vendors.

Do This
"Get an Honest Assessment"
"Check If We're a Fit"
"See a Real Example"
Avoid This
"Unlock Your Potential"
"Transform Your Business"
"Get Instant Results"
2

Specific Beats Generic

Tell them exactly what they'll receive. Vague CTAs force visitors to imagine outcomes, adding cognitive load that reduces clicks. Reference real deliverables from actual client work.

Do This
"See the Architecture Review"
"Download the Build-vs-Buy Framework"
"View the Trading App POC"
Avoid This
"Get Information"
"Download Resources"
"See What We Offer"
3

Low-Risk First Actions Convert

Early-funnel CTAs should feel safe. Founders are busy and skeptical. Save high-commitment asks for warm leads who have already seen your case studies or received a referral.

Do This (Cold Traffic)
"See How We Work"
"View Case Studies"
"Read the Approach"
Avoid This (Cold Traffic)
"Book a 60-Min Call"
"Request a Quote"
"Start Your Engagement"
4

Personal Beats Corporate

Early-stage consultancies succeed through founder relationships. "Talk to Calvin" is more authentic than "Contact Our Team." Personal visibility builds trust.

Do This
"Talk to Calvin"
"Discuss Your Challenge"
"Book Time with Me"
Less Effective
"Contact Our Team"
"Submit an Inquiry"
"Request a Callback"
5

Match CTA to Buyer Stage

Founders at different stages need different CTAs. Explorers need education. Evaluators need proof. Decision-makers need direct paths to conversation.

Exploring (Cold)
"See How We Work"
"Read the Case Study"
"Explore Our Approach"
Deciding (Warm)
"Book a Strategy Call"
"Check If We're a Fit"
"Discuss Your Project"

Quick Reference Checklist

Before Launching a Test

Define success metric. Calculate required sample size. Set end date in advance. Document hypothesis.

During the Test

Don't peek at results early. Don't end test before minimum duration. Don't change other page elements.

After the Test

Verify statistical significance. Document learnings. Implement winner. Plan next test based on insights.

Red Flags

Less than 95% confidence. Fewer than 100 conversions. Test ran less than 7 days. Major external events during test.
