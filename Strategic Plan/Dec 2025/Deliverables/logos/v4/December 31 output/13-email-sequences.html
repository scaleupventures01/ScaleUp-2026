Solution Introduction

Day 6
Framework
Subject Line
The 3 questions that prevent expensive mistakes
Preview Text
Ask these before you invest a dollar in any digital project...
Email Body

Hi {{First_Name}},

I promised to share the three questions that prevent expensive digital mistakes. Here they are:

Question 1: What specific business outcome am I trying to achieve?

Not "a new website" or "better marketing." What result? More leads? Higher-quality clients? Reduced time on manual tasks? If you can't articulate the outcome, you can't measure success.

Question 2: How will I know if it's working?

What numbers will you look at? How often? What's the target? Too many business owners invest in digital projects and never set up a way to measure results. That's how you get burned.

Question 3: What would make this investment worthwhile?

A $15,000 website that generates $50,000 in new business? That's worth it. A $15,000 website that generates nothing? That's an expensive mistake. You should know the math before you spend.

At ScaleUp Ventures, we actually require clients to answer these questions - with us - before we start any project. We build what we call an ROI model: a simple document that shows exactly how you'll make your investment back.

If the numbers don't work, we tell you. Even if it means we don't get the project.

That's what "Certainty First" means to us.

Best,
{{Your_Name}}
