SECTION 01

Voice Archetype

"The Patient Observer"
Watchful Measured Decisive

Watchful: We notice what others miss. We study patterns, track results, and pay attention to the details that determine success or failure. We don't rush to conclusions—we gather evidence first.

Measured: Every word we choose carries weight. We speak with precision, not volume. Our confidence comes from preparation, not personality. We let the work speak.

Decisive: Once we've observed and measured, we act without hesitation. We make clear recommendations. We don't hedge or hide behind qualifiers. When we commit, we commit fully.

Who we speak to: Founders and business owners exploring new ventures. They've heard every sales pitch. They trust evidence, not enthusiasm. They respect competence over charisma. They want partners who prove value before asking for commitment—and who will tell them "don't build this" when that's the right answer. Our Two-Track model speaks their language: for ad campaigns, we quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads BEFORE starting to ensure expected results meet client needs; for development projects, high/mid/low ROI scenarios.

SECTION 02

The Three Voice Pillars

Confident

(not arrogant)

We speak with quiet authority earned through results, not claimed through boasting. Our confidence invites verification—we're certain because we've done the work, not because we need you to believe us.

  • EXAMPLE 1 "Here's exactly what changed after 90 days—the numbers tell the story."
  • EXAMPLE 2 "A client had a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment in mind. We proposed a $5,000 discovery engagement (4 weeks). We found market saturation, hidden costs, and that 80% was achievable with existing solutions. We saved them $150,000+ and 12+ months."
  • EXAMPLE 3 "Ask our clients directly. We'll provide their contact information."
  • EXAMPLE 4 "This is the path forward. Here's why, and here's the proof."
  • EXAMPLE 5 "We prove it before you buy—and if we can't, we'll tell you. For ad campaigns, we quantify projected ad spend AND projected leads BEFORE starting to ensure expected results meet client needs. Development projects come with three-scenario ROI projections."
  • EXAMPLE 6 "The analysis supports one clear conclusion. Let me show you the evidence."

Direct

(not blunt)

We respect your time by getting to the point. We don't hide behind jargon or soften hard truths with corporate language. But we're never careless—directness serves clarity, not ego.

  • EXAMPLE 1 "Based on our analysis, your current approach has specific gaps. Here's what we found."
  • EXAMPLE 2 "This won't work for every business. Here's how to know if it fits yours."
  • EXAMPLE 3 "Three things need to change. Here they are, in order of impact."
  • EXAMPLE 4 "We focus on early-stage ventures. If you need enterprise-scale consulting, we'll refer you elsewhere."
  • EXAMPLE 5 "The honest answer: this will take time before you see meaningful returns."
  • EXAMPLE 6 "Skip to page 4 for the bottom line. Read the rest when you're ready for details."

Warm

(not casual)

We understand what's at stake. These aren't just businesses—they're legacies, livelihoods, life's work. We bring genuine care without crossing into false familiarity or manufactured friendliness.

  • EXAMPLE 1 "Building a business for 25 years creates more than revenue—it creates responsibility. We understand that."
  • EXAMPLE 2 "Your skepticism is well-earned. We'd question anyone who wasn't skeptical."
  • EXAMPLE 3 "We're in this until the results show. That's the only timeline that matters."
  • EXAMPLE 4 "Your team knows things spreadsheets can't capture. We listen to them too."
  • EXAMPLE 5 "Transitions are difficult. We've guided businesses through this exact moment before."
  • EXAMPLE 6 "When you're ready to talk, we're here. No pressure, no countdown timers."
SECTION 03

Do / Don't Examples

Don't Write This Write This Instead
CREDIBILITY "We're the best growth agency in the market." CREDIBILITY "Here's what we've delivered for clients with similar challenges."
TRUST "Trust us to handle everything." TRUST "Here's our process, our timeline, and our track record. Then you decide."
URGENCY "Act now before it's too late!" URGENCY "When you're ready, we're here."
RESULTS "We'll skyrocket your revenue!" RESULTS "We'll show you exactly what improvement looks like—ad campaigns get guaranteed lead numbers; development projects get high/mid/low ROI projections in cash or asset value."
EXPERTISE "Our world-class team of experts..." EXPERTISE "Our approach: market validation, business model validation, app development, fundraising plans, and investor/advisor introductions."
PROBLEMS "You're leaving money on the table!" PROBLEMS "Our analysis found three specific areas with untapped potential."
INNOVATION "Our cutting-edge, disruptive solutions..." INNOVATION "We use practical methods refined through real client work."
PARTNERSHIP "We'll be your partner on this journey!" PARTNERSHIP "We stay until the outcomes are documented."
PROMISES "Guaranteed results or your money back!" PROMISES "We prove it before you buy—and if we can't, we'll tell you. For ad campaigns: guaranteed lead numbers with fee refunded if missed. For development projects: three-scenario ROI projections showing your return."
GREETINGS "Hey! Super excited to connect with you!" GREETINGS "Thank you for your time. Here's what we found."
DIFFERENTIATION "We're different from other agencies..." DIFFERENTIATION "Compare our documented outcomes to anyone else's."
CLOSING "Let's hop on a quick call to chat!" CLOSING "I'm available Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am for a 30-minute conversation."

SECTION 06

Pronoun Usage Rules

"We"

When ScaleUp is taking action or making commitments

Use "we" to show accountability and collective expertise. It signals that the entire organization stands behind statements.

"We validate ideas before you invest heavily in building them."
"We commit to clear deliverables and transparent progress updates."
"You"

When speaking directly to the prospect/client

Address the reader directly to create connection without presumption. Respect their autonomy and decision-making.

"You've built something worth protecting."
"Your business deserves the same rigor we'd apply to our own."
"Your"

When discussing the prospect's business, challenges, or goals

Acknowledge ownership. Never diminish their achievement or suggest we understand their business better than they do.

"Your team's insights are essential to this process."
"Your goals drive every recommendation we make."
"I"

Use sparingly—only in direct personal communication

Reserve for emails, personal notes, or when a specific team member is making a personal commitment. Never in formal marketing.

"I reviewed your financials personally and found three areas worth discussing."
"I'll be your direct point of contact throughout this engagement."
"They"

When referencing clients, case studies, or third parties

Use when sharing client stories or referencing external sources. Maintains appropriate distance while lending credibility.

"They had a $150,000+ budget and 12-18 month commitment in mind. Our $5,000 discovery (4 weeks) found market saturation and hidden costs—80% was achievable with existing solutions. We saved them $150,000+ and 12+ months. 'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5,000 telling the truth than $150,000 building something that won't serve you."
"Their team was skeptical at first. The prototype changed that."

Quick Reference: Before You Write

Ask Yourself

  • Would a skeptical 60-year-old trust this?
  • Can I prove this statement?
  • Does this respect their intelligence?
  • Am I being direct or just pushy?
  • Would I want to receive this?

Remember

  • Evidence over enthusiasm
  • Precision over personality
  • Patience over pressure
  • Clarity over cleverness
  • Respect over rapport

The Test

  • Read it aloud
  • Cut 20% of the words
  • Remove any word you can't defend
  • Add specific numbers where possible
  • Delete all exclamation points
