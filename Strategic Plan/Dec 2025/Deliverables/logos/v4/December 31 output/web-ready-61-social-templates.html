LinkedIn Post Templates (10 Formats)

Personal Story Post

Build connection through vulnerability and authentic experiences.

I almost [made a big mistake / missed an opportunity / gave up on something]. [Year or timeframe]. [Set the scene in 1-2 sentences]. I was convinced [what you believed at the time]. Then [what happened - the turning point]. Everything changed. [What you learned in 2-3 sentences] Looking back, I'm grateful for that moment because [why it matters now]. If you're facing [similar situation] right now: [Your advice based on this experience] The path is rarely straight. But it's always forward. #LessonsLearned #ConsultingLife #ScaleUpVentures --- EXAMPLE (Building a Product): I almost gave up on a client project that seemed impossible. After years of consulting, I was convinced that building an HR automation tool with full product scope - from idea through user research, product development, persona research, targeting, ad management, and lead generation - alongside other client work was too ambitious. Then I realized: each client engagement was teaching me how to deliver better outcomes. Everything changed. Every client conversation revealed common pain points. Every architecture decision informed product thinking. The consulting work wasn't competing with full-product engagements - it was building the capability to deliver them. Looking back, I'm grateful for that realization because it showed me that client work can scale in scope and impact. If you're facing a project that feels beyond your current capacity right now: Consider that your past engagements have prepared you for exactly this. The skills compound.
Pro Tips:
  • Be specific with time and place (makes it real)
  • Show vulnerability - what you didn't know then
  • Connect the past lesson to present relevance
  • Full-scope client journeys make authentic content

My current thinking: [Your perspective in 2-3 sentences] But I'm genuinely curious what others are experiencing. [Restate the question or add a follow-up angle] Drop your thoughts below. No wrong answers. #OpenDiscussion #TechConsulting #ScaleUpVentures --- EXAMPLE (AI Automation Agents): Honest question for technical leaders and consultants: How are you delegating work to AI agents in your operations? I've been thinking about this because we built AI automation agents for clients using project management best practices, and I'm seeing a huge gap between the hype and what's actually delivering value. My current thinking: The real wins come from agents that handle delegated tasks - status updates, meeting summaries, requirement extraction, scheduling. Team members now save 20-25 hours per month—and that number grows 10-20% each month as additional processes flow into the automated workflow. But I'm genuinely curious what others are experiencing. What AI agents are actually giving your team time back? What approaches have been oversold? Drop your thoughts below. No wrong answers.
Pro Tips:
  • Ask questions you genuinely want answered
  • Share your perspective first (models the response)
  • Reply to EVERY comment within 2 hours
  • Our AI automation agent development work provides authentic question topics

Facebook & Instagram Adaptation

While LinkedIn is the primary platform for B2B tech consulting content, Facebook and Instagram can extend reach for brand awareness and culture building.

f Facebook

  • Best for: Long-form stories, event promotion, community building
  • Tone shift: More conversational, less formal than LinkedIn
  • Content that works: Behind-the-scenes, team culture, portfolio celebrations
  • What to avoid: Pure thought leadership (lower B2B engagement)
  • Posting frequency: 2-3x per week maximum
  • Key adaptation: Lead with emotion/story, then insight

IG Instagram

  • Best for: Visual branding, culture showcase, younger founder reach
  • Tone shift: More personal, behind-the-scenes, aspirational
  • Content that works: Office life, events, founder spotlights, infographics
  • What to avoid: Text-heavy posts, pure business content
  • Posting frequency: 3-5x per week (feed) + daily Stories
  • Key adaptation: Image-first, caption supports the visual

Formula

Hashtag Usage Formula

OPTIMAL HASHTAG COUNT: 3-5 per post (LinkedIn penalizes more) FORMULA: 1 Brand hashtag + 2 Industry hashtags + 1-2 Topic hashtags EXAMPLE: #ScaleUpVentures #TechConsulting #Founders #BuildVsBuy PLACEMENT: - At the end of the post - After a line break (---) - Never in the middle of content TRENDING HASHTAG USAGE: - Only if genuinely relevant - Don't force-fit to trends - Monitor: #TechTwitter, #IndieHackers for emerging hashtags
Hashtag Don'ts:
  • Never use more than 5 hashtags on LinkedIn (looks spammy)
  • Don't use hashtags in the middle of sentences
  • Avoid super-generic hashtags (#business, #success)
  • Don't create new hashtags unless building a campaign

Engagement Responses

Comment Response Templates

When someone agrees with your post
"Appreciate you adding to this, [Name]. Curious - have you seen [related angle or question]? Would love to hear your experience."
When someone shares their own experience
"This is a great example, [Name]. The [specific thing they mentioned] really resonates. Mind if I ask - how did [follow-up question]?"
When someone respectfully disagrees
"Really appreciate the pushback, [Name]. You're making me think about [their point]. I wonder if [bridge between perspectives] - what do you think?"
When someone asks a question
"Great question, [Name]. Short answer: [concise response]. The longer answer depends on [context]. Happy to go deeper - DM me if useful."
When someone just says "Great post!"
"Thanks, [Name]! What's your take on [specific aspect of the post]? Would love to hear your perspective."
When someone tags others
"Thanks for looping in [tagged people], [Name]. Would love to hear their thoughts on this too."

DM Response Templates

When a founder reaches out for help
"Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out about [Company/Project]. I appreciate you thinking of ScaleUp Ventures. To see if we might be a good fit, could you share: 1. A one-liner on what you're building 2. Your current stage and biggest technical challenge 3. What kind of support you're looking for (architecture, prototype, assessment, etc.) I'll take a look and get back to you within [timeframe]. Looking forward to learning more."
When someone asks to "pick your brain"
"Hi [Name], appreciate you reaching out. I get a lot of these requests, so to be respectful of both our time - could you share 2-3 specific questions you'd love answered? If it's something I can help with, happy to either: - Reply here with thoughts - Suggest a resource - Find 15 mins if it makes sense Looking forward to hearing what's on your mind."
When declining a meeting request
"Hi [Name], thanks for thinking of me. Unfortunately, I can't commit to [meeting/call] right now due to current bandwidth. But I wanted to at least share: [One helpful resource, insight, or connection] Feel free to stay in touch - and best of luck with [their project/company]."
When someone asks for an intro
"Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out about an intro to [Person]. Before I connect you, a few quick questions: 1. What's the context/reason for the intro? 2. Is there something specific you're hoping to discuss? 3. Can I share your LinkedIn/email with them first? I like to make sure intros are warm on both sides. Let me know!"
Engagement Best Practices:
  • Respond within 2 hours of posting to boost algorithm visibility
  • Reply to every comment - even a quick acknowledgment matters
  • Ask follow-up questions - turns comments into conversations
  • Use names - personalization increases engagement
  • Be generous with DMs - but set clear boundaries
  • Never sell in DMs first - provide value, build relationship
