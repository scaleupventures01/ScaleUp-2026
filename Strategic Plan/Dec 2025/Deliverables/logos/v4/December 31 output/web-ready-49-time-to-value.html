Factors That Affect Timeline

What Speeds Things Up

  • Clear problem definition Reduces discovery time significantly
  • Single decision-maker Eliminates approval bottlenecks
  • Available for collaboration Faster feedback loops
  • Existing documentation Less time spent on discovery
  • Flexible on non-essential features Focus on what matters most

What Extends Timeline

  • Regulated industries Compliance review adds weeks (financial, healthcare)
  • Multiple stakeholders Each approval layer adds delay
  • Scope changes mid-project Requires re-planning and re-estimation
  • Integration complexity Legacy systems require more careful handling
  • Unclear success criteria Hard to know when you are done

Setting Honest Expectations

We are a boutique consultancy, not a volume shop. Our value is in strategic clarity and quality execution, not speed at all costs.

Quick Wins

Strategic decisions can deliver immediate value. A well-timed "don't build this" saves months of wasted effort and significant capital.

POC Reality

A working prototype typically takes 4-8 weeks. Add time for regulated industries or complex integrations. Rushing leads to rework.

Quality Over Speed

We would rather take an extra week to get it right than deliver something that needs to be redone. Your timeline should reflect reality.

Your Involvement Matters

Projects move fastest when you are available for feedback. Delays usually come from waiting on decisions, not from our side.
