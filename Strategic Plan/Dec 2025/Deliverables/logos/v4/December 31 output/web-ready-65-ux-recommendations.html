Phase 3 Website Build

UI/UX Recommendations

Strategic UX guidance for high-converting, trust-building website pages. Optimized for B2B service business buyers who value proof over promises.

1

Homepage

Hero Section Architecture

F-Pattern Visual Hierarchy Critical

Position headline top-left, value proposition below, primary CTA middle-left, trust signals bottom-left. Users scan F-pattern on web pages - place conversion elements along this natural eye path.

Headline: Problem + Promise (60 characters max)

"We Show You the Numbers Before You Spend a Dime" - leads with certainty positioning. Avoid generic "Transform Your Business" headlines that trigger skepticism.

Subheadline: Proof Statement (120 characters)

Immediately reference Two-Track model and lead guarantees. Example: "Ad campaigns with guaranteed lead numbers or app projects with 3-scenario ROI projections. We prove it before you buy."

Trust Signal Placement

Above Fold: Micro-Commitments

Three trust bullets immediately below CTA: "No upfront fees" / "ROI projection in 48 hours" / "No obligation to proceed". Reduces perceived risk before scroll.

Social Proof Ladder (Scroll Depth 20-40%)

Client logos first (visual credibility), then specific results ("$25-$33 CPL for HR tool validation"), then testimonials (emotional proof). Order matters: visual → quantitative → emotional.

4-Stage CTA Ladder Implementation

1
Awareness (0-20% scroll)
"See the Math" - Link to ROI calculator or case study math
2
Interest (20-50% scroll)
"Get Your Estimate" - Email-only form, low friction
3
Consideration (50-80% scroll)
"Book a 15-Min Strategy Call" - Calendly integration
4
Decision (80-100% scroll)
"Start Your Proof Test" - Final conversion point
Mobile Responsiveness Checklist
  • Hero headline remains 32px minimum on mobile (legibility)
  • CTA buttons minimum 48x48px touch targets (Apple HIG standard)
  • Trust signals stack vertically, maintain 16px spacing
  • Sticky CTA bar appears at 50% scroll on mobile (accessibility)
2

Services Pages

Information Architecture

Lead with Outcome, Not Process Critical

Web App Development headline: "Custom Web Applications That Generate Measurable ROI" not "Full-Stack Development Services". Buyers care about business outcomes first.

Pricing Transparency (Fold 1)

Display starting prices immediately: "Web Apps from $10K" / "Growth Marketing $1,500/mo" / "AI Agents from $5K". Reduces qualification friction by 40% (Baymard Institute).

Case Study Proximity

Position relevant case study within 2 scrolls of service description. Example: After Web App description, show Financial Trading Platform case (SEC/NFA compliance proof). Context-specific proof converts better than generic testimonials.

Conversion Rate Optimization

Multi-Step Form Design

Break "Get Your Estimate" into 3 steps: (1) Service needed, (2) Timeline/budget, (3) Contact info. Shows 25-30% higher completion vs. single long forms. Progress bar mandatory.

Exit Intent Offer

Trigger at scroll-up or tab-close: "Before You Go: Download Our ROI Framework [PDF]". Email capture for nurture sequence. 10-15% of abandoning visitors convert to leads.

Services Page Template Structure

Section 1: Outcome headline + starting price
Section 2: 3-benefit grid (What you get)
Section 3: Process overview (How it works)
Section 4: Relevant case study embed
Section 5: ROI calculator or estimator tool
Section 6: FAQ accordion (objection handling)
Section 7: Final CTA with guarantee statement

3

About Page

Narrative Structure

Origin Story: Problem → Insight → Mission

Open with founder frustration with dishonest vendors (relatability), discovery of proof-first model (differentiation), commitment to "Certainty First" (mission). Story arc builds emotional connection.

Values as Proof Points

Instead of generic values ("Integrity, Excellence"), tie to specific behaviors: "We recommend NOT proceeding if ROI projections don't show high-probability positive outcome." Makes values tangible.

Team Profile Design

Founder-Forward Positioning

Calvin profile takes 60% of fold 1. Include: (1) Professional headshot, (2) LinkedIn link, (3) Specific expertise (SEC/NFA compliance builds), (4) Personal note ("Father of 3, building businesses that prove value first").

Team Credibility Markers

For Patrick, Aaron, Roe: Show project involvement, not generic bios. "Patrick led Financial Trading Platform backend architecture" beats "10 years experience in development."

About Page Must-Haves
  • Company founding year and evolution timeline
  • Mission statement in founder's voice (first person)
  • Client types served (Startups, Established Businesses, Enterprises)
  • Geographic location (builds local trust if relevant)
  • Contact preference: "Talk to Calvin" CTA not generic "Contact Us"
4

Contact Page

Form Optimization

Progressive Disclosure Form Critical

Step 1: "What brings you here?" (radio buttons: Web App / Marketing / AI Agent / Other). Step 2: Contextual questions based on selection. Step 3: Contact info. Conditional logic reduces form length anxiety.

Field Validation: Real-Time, Inline

Email validation on blur (as user tabs away), not on submit. Show green checkmark when valid. Error states in red with specific fix instruction: "Email missing @" not "Invalid email."

Submit Button Microcopy

"Send My Request" beats "Submit". Action-oriented, personal language. Button disabled state shows "Sending..." with spinner during processing.

Alternative Contact Paths

Calendly Embed (Primary Path)

Show Calvin's calendar availability above form. "See available times and book directly" with embedded Calendly. 30% of users prefer self-scheduling over form submission.

Office Information Display

If physical location: Address with Google Maps embed, business hours, phone number with "Call now" mobile-tap link. If remote: Timezone indicator + "We serve clients nationwide."

Contact Page Layout (2-Column Desktop)

Left Column (60%): Form with progressive disclosure
Right Column (40%): Calendly embed, office info card, response time expectation ("We respond within 24 hours"), alternative contact methods (email, LinkedIn)

5

FAQ Page

Content Organization

Category-Based Accordion Structure

Group by buyer journey stage: (1) Getting Started, (2) Services & Pricing, (3) Process & Timeline, (4) Guarantees & Proof, (5) Working Together. Reduces cognitive load vs. alphabetical lists.

Anchor Link Navigation

Sticky sidebar (desktop) or dropdown (mobile) lets users jump to category. FAQ pages average 8+ scrolls - navigation is accessibility requirement.

Search Functionality (If 20+ FAQs)

Client-side JavaScript search with instant filtering. Placeholder: "Search for answers..." Auto-expands matching accordion items.

Answer Optimization

Proof-Forward Answers

Q: "How do you guarantee results?" A: Reference specific case metrics: "For ad campaigns, we guarantee lead numbers - like our HR Automation Tool campaign that delivered $25-$33 CPL with fee refunded if targets missed."

Link to Deep Content

Embed internal links: "See our full process here" (links to methodology page), "Read the case study" (links to relevant case). Extends time on site, builds SEO link equity.

Top 10 FAQs to Include (Objection Handling)
  • What makes you different from other agencies/consultancies?
  • How much do your services cost?
  • What is your Two-Track ROI model?
  • Do you offer guarantees? What happens if you miss targets?
  • How long does a typical project take?
  • Who owns the code/intellectual property?
  • Can you show me examples of your work?
  • Do you work with businesses in my industry?
  • What if I'm not sure what I need?
  • How do we get started?

Client Logos Collection

Display Architecture

Animated Scroll Carousel (Homepage)

Infinite horizontal scroll with 12-15 logos. Grayscale default, color on hover. Movement attracts eye without being distracting. Pause on hover for accessibility.

Static Grid (Dedicated Page)

6-column grid on desktop, 3-column tablet, 2-column mobile. Each logo links to case study if available, otherwise links to industry landing page.

Logo Sizing Standards

All logos normalized to same visual weight: Max height 60px, max width 180px. Maintain aspect ratio. White/light logos on navy background, dark logos on cream background.

Usage Guidelines

Permission Documentation

Only display logos with written client permission. Document approval in CRM/project folder. Legal protection against unauthorized use claims.

Context Labels

Add subtle industry tags: "Veterinary" / "Staffing" / "SaaS". Helps visitors identify relevant proof. 12px gray text below logo.

★

Universal UX Principles

Accessibility Standards (WCAG 2.1 AA Minimum)

Color Contrast Ratios

Navy (#0F1729) on Cream (#FAF8F5): 12.5:1 (AAA). Gold (#D4A853) on Navy: 4.8:1 (AA Large). All text meets minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text (18px+).

Keyboard Navigation

All CTAs, forms, and navigation accessible via Tab key. Focus states visible with 2px gold outline. Skip-to-content link for screen reader users.

Screen Reader Optimization

Semantic HTML5 (header, nav, main, article, footer). ARIA labels on all form fields. Alt text on all images describes content, not "image of..."

Motion Sensitivity

Respect prefers-reduced-motion media query. Disable carousel auto-scroll, parallax effects, and animations for users who request reduced motion.

Performance Budgets

Page Load Speed Targets

First Contentful Paint (FCP): <1.8s. Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): <2.5s. Time to Interactive (TTI): <3.8s. Measured on 4G mobile connection.

Asset Optimization

Images: WebP format with JPEG fallback, lazy loading below fold. Fonts: Preload Crimson Pro + DM Sans, subset to used characters. CSS/JS: Minify and compress, defer non-critical scripts.

Mobile-First Development

Design for 375px (iPhone SE) breakpoint first. Progressive enhancement for tablet (768px) and desktop (1024px+). 60%+ of B2B traffic is mobile during research phase.

Conversion Psychology Tactics

Social Proof Hierarchy

Most credible to least: Specific case study metrics > Named testimonials with title/company > Client logo > Generic review count. Always use highest available.

Scarcity Without False Urgency

Honest capacity communication: "We take 3-4 new clients per quarter" builds authentic scarcity. Avoid fake countdown timers or "Only 2 spots left" unless true.

Reciprocity Triggers

Give value before asking for contact info: Free ROI calculator, downloadable framework, or 48-hour assessment. Increases form completion by 20-35%.

7

Testing & Iteration Roadmap

Phase 1: Launch (Weeks 1-2)

Baseline Metrics Collection

Install Google Analytics 4, Hotjar (heatmaps + session recordings), Microsoft Clarity (free alternative). Track: Bounce rate, avg. session duration, scroll depth, CTA click rates, form starts vs. completions.

User Testing (5-8 Sessions)

Recruit 5 target buyers (early-stage founders), conduct moderated usability tests via Zoom. Task: "Find pricing for web app development." Identify friction points before optimization.

Phase 2: Optimize (Weeks 3-8)

A/B Test Priority Queue

(1) Homepage hero CTA copy, (2) Service page pricing display, (3) Contact form length (full vs. progressive), (4) About page founder story length. Run 2-week tests with 95% confidence threshold.

Heatmap Analysis

Review Hotjar heatmaps weekly. Look for: Rage clicks (broken elements), dead zones (content ignored), scroll depth drop-off points. Fix top 3 friction points per page.

Phase 3: Scale (Ongoing)

Conversion Funnel Monitoring

Weekly dashboard review: Homepage visits → Service page views → Contact form starts → Form completions → Qualified leads. Identify biggest drop-off stage and prioritize fix.

Quarterly UX Audit

Every 90 days: Run Lighthouse performance audit, check broken links, review accessibility with WAVE tool, update case studies/testimonials, refresh outdated content.
