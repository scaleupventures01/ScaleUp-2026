Why Early-Stage Technology Decisions Have Outsized Impact

Four dynamics make the first 18 months of a venture uniquely consequential for technology strategy. Understanding them helps you invest your limited resources wisely.

$

Build vs. Buy Decisions Set Your Burn Rate

We recently helped a client avoid a $150K+ custom build by identifying an existing solution that met their needs. A $5K strategic analysis. The temptation to build custom is strong, but often the right answer is "don't build this." Getting that call wrong early can burn runway you'll desperately need later.

Real client: $150K+ saved
A

Architecture Decisions Compound

The technical architecture you choose in month 3 becomes the foundation everything else builds on. Changing it in year 2 means rewriting core systems while trying to serve customers. We've taken clients from idea to full product launch in regulated industries, including a SEC/NFA compliant financial trading app.

Foundation for scale
T

Team Structure Determines Velocity

Should you hire a CTO? Contract developers? Build an offshore team? The answer depends on your stage, runway, and technical complexity. We help founders think through these decisions before they commit to structures that don't fit.

Right structure, right stage
I

Investors Notice Technical Maturity

When you're raising, investors evaluate your technical decisions as a proxy for judgment. A coherent architecture, realistic roadmap, and clear technical strategy signal a founder who thinks critically. We've helped startups prepare for investor conversations with technical credibility.

Technical credibility matters
