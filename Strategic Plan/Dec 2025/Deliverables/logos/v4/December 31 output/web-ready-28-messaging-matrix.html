"Certainty First."

The complete guide to adapting ScaleUp Ventures messaging across every customer touchpoint

Message Hierarchy

Priority order for all communications. Higher tiers appear first and most frequently.

1
Tier 1 - Core Promise
"Certainty First."
Our tagline and north star. Every communication should connect back to the promise of certainty in an uncertain business world. We prove it before you buy.
Usage: Headlines, email subject lines, proposal openers, sales call introduction, business cards, email signatures
2
Tier 2 - Value Proposition
Technology solutions for established businesses. Three services. Measurable outcomes.
We deliver web applications with ROI projections, growth marketing with guaranteed leads, and AI agents that save time. Technology that proves its value before you commit.
Usage: Hero subheadlines, email body, LinkedIn posts, proposal value sections, first sales call talking point
3
Tier 3 - Key Differentiator
Growth Marketing: Guaranteed Leads or Your Money Back
This is THE key differentiator. We commit to a lead number upfront. Miss it, and you don't pay our fee. Not projections - actual lead guarantees with money back. This separates us from every other agency.
Usage: Prominent in all marketing materials, proposal highlights, sales conversations, website hero sections
4
Tier 4 - Proof Points
SEC/NFA Compliant Financial Trading Platform - Built from idea to full compliance.
We took a client from concept to a fully SEC/NFA-compliant trading application - the highest regulatory bar in financial software. Complete product build plus go-to-market: persona research, targeting, ad management, and lead generation. This proves we can deliver complex, mission-critical applications that meet the strictest standards.
Usage: Social proof sections, capability demonstrations, proposal credentials, mid-funnel content, sales conversations
5
Tier 5 - Service-Specific Value
Web Apps ($10K+): ROI projections before you commit. Growth Marketing ($1,500/mo): Guaranteed leads. AI Agents ($5,000+): Save 20-25 hours/month.
Each service has specific, measurable value propositions. Web Application Development includes high/mid/low scenario modeling before development starts. Growth Marketing offers actual lead guarantees with fee refund if missed. AI Agent Development delivers time savings that compound over time.
Usage: Services pages, detailed proposals, discovery calls, case studies, thought leadership content

1. Website

Primary digital storefront. Must convert visitors to conversations within 60 seconds.

W
Website
Lead generation, credibility establishment, education

Primary Message

Technology solutions that prove their value before you commit. Web applications with ROI projections. Growth marketing with guaranteed leads. AI agents that save time.

Secondary Messages

  • Guaranteed leads or your money back - we commit to the number upfront
  • See the numbers before you commit - ROI projections for development projects
  • Save 20-25 hours per month with AI agents that automate repetitive work

Tone Adjustment

Professional yet accessible. Warm enough to feel human, sharp enough to feel expert. Write at 8th grade reading level. Use "you" more than "we."

CTA Style

Action + outcome. Never just "Learn More." Always specify what they'll get.

  • "Discuss Your Project"
  • "Get Guaranteed Leads"
  • "Explore AI Agents"
  • "See the Guarantee"
Example Copy
Homepage Hero

"Technology solutions for established businesses. Web applications with ROI projections before you commit. Growth marketing with guaranteed leads - miss the target, you don't pay our fee. AI agents that save 20-25 hours per month. Certainty First."

Growth Marketing Section

"Guaranteed Leads or Your Money Back. We commit to a lead number upfront. Miss it, and you don't pay our fee. Not projections. Not maybes. Actual leads with a money-back guarantee. That's how confident we are in our process."

Web Development Section

"See the numbers before you commit. We model high, mid, and low ROI scenarios before development starts. You know your potential return before investing a dollar. One client had a $150K+ budget ready - our $5K discovery saved them from building something the market didn't need."

What to Avoid
  • Industry jargon like "synergies," "leverage," "paradigm shift"
  • Passive voice ("Businesses are helped by our services")
  • Vague claims without specifics ("We provide excellent service")
  • Generic stock photos of handshakes or city skylines
  • Walls of text without headers, bullets, or visual breaks
  • CTAs that only say "Contact Us" or "Learn More"

2. Email

Nurturing relationships and driving conversations. Subject lines are 80% of the battle.

E
Email
Lead nurturing, client communication, thought leadership

Primary Message

Position every email as delivering value first. Lead with insight, follow with offer. Connect to one of our three services.

Secondary Messages

  • Growth marketing with guaranteed leads removes the risk from lead generation
  • ROI projections before development lets you make informed decisions
  • AI agents compound their value - savings grow 10-20% monthly

Tone Adjustment

Direct and personal. Write like a trusted advisor, not a marketing department. Use first names. Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences max. Mobile-first formatting.

CTA Style

Single, clear ask per email. Make the next step obvious and low-friction.

  • "Reply with 'yes' and I'll share how the guarantee works"
  • "Block 30 minutes to discuss your project: [link]"
  • "Hit reply and tell me what's eating your team's time"
Example Copy
Cold Outreach - Growth Marketing

"Subject: What if you only paid for leads that actually came in?

Hi [Name],

Most agencies promise results and deliver invoices. We do it differently.

We commit to a specific lead number upfront. Miss it, and you don't pay our fee.

Not projections. Not estimates. A guaranteed number with money back if we miss.

Would a 15-minute call to see how we calculate your lead guarantee be useful?

Either way, wishing you continued success."

Cold Outreach - Web Development

"Subject: The $150K decision that didn't happen

Hi [Name],

A business owner came to us with $150K+ budget ready. 12-18 months of development planned.

We proposed something different: $5K for a 4-week discovery phase.

What we found: market saturation, hidden costs, and 80% of what they wanted was achievable with existing solutions.

Our recommendation? Don't build.

That 'no' saved them $150K+ and 12+ months.

If you're considering a technology investment, would a conversation about how we validate ideas before building be valuable?"

Follow-up After Meeting

"Subject: Next steps on your lead guarantee

Hi [Name],

Great speaking with you today. Based on what you shared about your lead generation challenges, I believe we can commit to [X] leads per month with our guarantee.

Here's how it works: we hit the number, you pay. We miss it, you don't pay our fee.

I'll send over a detailed proposal by Thursday. Let's schedule a call for Monday to walk through the numbers together."

What to Avoid
  • Subject lines that scream "MARKETING EMAIL" (all caps, excessive punctuation)
  • Opening with "I hope this email finds you well"
  • Multiple CTAs competing for attention
  • Paragraphs longer than 3 sentences
  • Attaching heavy files that trigger spam filters
  • Using "touch base," "circle back," "ping," or other corporate cliches

3. LinkedIn

Thought leadership and network building. Demonstrate expertise through valuable content.

L
LinkedIn
Thought leadership, brand awareness, relationship building

Primary Message

Share actionable insights that prove expertise. Teach, don't sell. Every post should leave readers smarter about technology decisions.

Secondary Messages

  • Patterns from working with established businesses on technology solutions
  • Counter-intuitive truths about lead generation and development projects
  • Behind-the-scenes of how we calculate lead guarantees and ROI projections

Tone Adjustment

Conversational authority. Like a smart colleague who's solved this problem before. Strong opinions, loosely held. Okay to show personality.

CTA Style

Engagement-focused. Ask questions, invite discussion, encourage shares. Save hard CTAs for 1 in 10 posts.

  • "What's been your experience with lead generation guarantees?"
  • "Agree or disagree? Tell me in the comments."
  • "Repost if this would help someone considering a technology investment."
Example Copy
Financial Trading Platform Story

"SEC/NFA compliant. From scratch.

A client came to us with an idea for a financial trading platform. Not a simple app - one that needed to meet the highest regulatory bar in the industry.

We didn't just build the software. We delivered the complete package:

• Full SEC/NFA-compliant application
• Persona research and targeting strategy
• Ad management and lead generation
• Complete go-to-market execution

From concept to compliance to customers.

That's what 'Certainty First' looks like in action. We prove we can deliver before asking for your trust.

Complex problems. Complete solutions."

Lead Guarantee Story

"Why would a marketing agency guarantee their leads?

Because most agencies have it backwards.

They promise results. They deliver invoices. You hope it works out.

We flip that model.

We commit to a specific lead number upfront. Miss it, and you don't pay our fee.

Not projections. Not estimates. A guaranteed number with money back if we miss.

Why? Because if we're not confident enough to stake our fee on it, why should you stake your budget on it?

That's what 'Certainty First' actually means."

AI Agent Value Story

"20-25 hours saved per month.

That's what AI agents delivered for a solutions consulting firm we worked with.

But here's what's interesting: those savings compound.

Month 1: 20-25 hours saved.
Month 2: 22-27 hours (10-20% more as additional processes flow in).
Month 3: Even more.

The time savings grow because once your team sees what's possible, they find more processes to automate.

Not replacement. Delegation. Your team focuses on high-value work while AI handles the repetitive stuff.

That's the difference between AI as a buzzword and AI as a business tool."

What to Avoid
  • Bragging disguised as lessons ("Just closed another big deal...")
  • Engagement bait with no substance ("Agree?")
  • Selling in every post (kills reach and trust)
  • Corporate speak that sounds like a press release
  • Posting without a hook in the first line
  • Ignoring comments (engagement is a two-way street)

4. Proposals

The moment of decision. Every word must build confidence and reduce perceived risk.

P
Proposals
Conversion, value justification, risk reduction

Primary Message

We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you. This is the partnership that will deliver measurable results with reduced risk.

Secondary Messages

  • For Growth Marketing: Guaranteed leads with fee refunded if we miss
  • For Web Development: ROI projections (high/mid/low) before you commit
  • For AI Agents: Documented time savings that compound monthly

Tone Adjustment

Confident and reassuring. Written as a trusted advisor, not a vendor. Executive-level language. Focus on outcomes, not features.

CTA Style

Clear next step with timeline. Remove ambiguity. Make signing feel like the natural conclusion.

  • "Sign below to lock in your lead guarantee"
  • "Reply 'approved' and we'll schedule kickoff for [date]"
  • "Choose your service level and we'll begin discovery within 5 business days"
Example Copy
Proposal Opening

"Over the past two weeks, we've analyzed [Company Name]'s lead generation challenges, growth trajectory, and strategic objectives. What we found was a business with exceptional potential, constrained by unpredictable lead flow that is entirely fixable.

This proposal outlines how we'll work together to deliver guaranteed leads every month. Not projections. Not estimates. A specific number with our fee refunded if we miss."

Growth Marketing Investment Section

"Your investment: $1,500/month starting

Our guarantee: [X] qualified leads per month

How it works: We commit to this lead number upfront. If we miss it, you don't pay our fee for that month. You only pay for ad spend.

This isn't a clever marketing promise. It's how we operate. We're confident because our process works, and we're willing to stake our fee on it."

Web Development Investment Section

"Your investment: $[X] (starting at $10K)

Before we write a single line of code, you'll receive:
- High scenario ROI projection
- Mid scenario ROI projection
- Low scenario ROI projection

You'll know your potential return in cash or company/asset value before committing to the full build.

Case in point: A client came to us with $150K+ ready to spend. Our $5K discovery found market saturation and hidden costs. We recommended NOT to build. That single 'no' saved them $150K+ and 12+ months.

'Certainty First' means we'd rather earn $5K telling the truth than $150K building something that won't serve you."

What to Avoid
  • Leading with price before establishing value
  • Generic proposals not customized to the specific prospect
  • Feature lists instead of outcome descriptions
  • Hedge words like "might," "could," "possibly"
  • Leaving next steps ambiguous or open-ended
  • Assuming they'll read every word (use headers, bullets, bold text)

5. Sales Calls

The conversation that builds trust and uncovers fit. Listen more than you speak.

S
Sales Calls
Discovery, qualification, relationship building

Primary Message

We're here to understand your situation first. If we can help, we'll tell you exactly how - and back it with a guarantee. If we can't, we'll tell you that too.

Secondary Messages

  • Your challenges aren't unique. We've solved this pattern before with measurable results.
  • We prove it before you buy. For marketing, that means guaranteed leads. For development, ROI projections.
  • Working with us should feel like gaining a trusted advisor, not hiring a vendor.

Tone Adjustment

Curious and consultative. Ask questions that make them think. Reflect back what you hear. Show genuine interest in their business, not just the sale.

CTA Style

Natural next step. Close with clear action and specific timeline.

  • "Based on what you've shared, I can calculate your lead guarantee. Can we schedule a follow-up to present those numbers?"
  • "I'll send over a proposal with ROI projections by Friday. Let's schedule a call for Monday to walk through it together."
  • "Before we wrap, what questions do you have about how the guarantee works?"
Example Script Segments
Opening

"Thanks for making time today. Before I tell you about what we do, I'd love to understand more about your business. What prompted you to take this call? What's happening with leads or technology that made you think about bringing in outside help?"

Transition to Growth Marketing Pitch

"What you're describing - unpredictable leads, money spent on agencies that promise results but deliver invoices - that's almost universal. Here's what makes us different: we guarantee the leads. We commit to a specific number upfront. Miss it, and you don't pay our fee. Want me to share how we calculate what that number would be for your business?"

Handling Price Question

"Great question, and I want to give you a real answer, not a range. Growth marketing starts at $1,500 per month plus ad spend. But here's how to think about it: we guarantee the leads. If we commit to [X] leads and only deliver [Y], you don't pay our fee for that month. So the real question isn't what it costs - it's whether the guaranteed leads are worth the investment. Based on what you've shared, I believe they are."

What to Avoid
  • Talking more than 40% of the call time
  • Launching into pitch before understanding their situation
  • Answering questions they didn't ask
  • Being defensive about price or process
  • Ending without clear next steps and timeline
  • Pressuring, creating false urgency, or manipulative closes

6. Print Materials

Physical touchpoints that create lasting impressions. Quality signals quality.

M
Print Materials
Leave-behinds, credibility builders, event collateral

Primary Message

Professional, premium, confident. The materials should feel as trustworthy as our solutions.

Secondary Messages

  • Three services: Web Apps ($10K+), Growth Marketing ($1,500/mo), AI Agents ($5,000+)
  • Growth marketing with guaranteed leads - the key differentiator
  • Trusted by established business owners

Tone Adjustment

Formal and polished. More executive, less conversational. Every word earns its place. White space is intentional.

CTA Style

Simple and dignified. One clear contact method. QR codes to landing pages are acceptable.

  • "Get your lead guarantee: scaleupventures.com/marketing"
  • "[QR Code] Scan for case studies and client results"
  • "Discuss your project: [phone] or [email]"
Example Copy
Business Card Back

"Technology Solutions for Established Businesses

Certainty First."

One-Pager Header

"We prove it before you buy. Web applications with ROI projections. Growth marketing with guaranteed leads. AI agents that save 20-25 hours per month.

Certainty First | Know Before You Spend"

Folder/Pocket Insert

"Inside you'll find:
- How we guarantee leads (and refund our fee if we miss)
- Case study: The $150K decision we helped a client NOT make
- Our three services with pricing
- Your customized opportunity assessment

Questions? Your dedicated advisor: [Name, Contact]"

What to Avoid
  • Cheap paper stock or low-quality printing
  • Cramming too much information onto materials
  • Photos that look like obvious stock photography
  • Outdated statistics or information
  • Multiple phone numbers, emails, and contact methods
  • QR codes without clear purpose or destination

Repetition Guidelines

How often to repeat key messages across channels and touchpoints

Every Touch
Tagline: "Certainty First."
Should appear or be referenced in every piece of content. In email signatures, website headers, proposal footers, presentation slides, and verbal conversations.
  • Email signature
  • Website header/footer
  • Business cards
  • Proposal cover pages
  • Slide deck closing
2-3x Per Piece
Lead Guarantee
"Guaranteed leads or your money back" is THE key differentiator. State it at opening, reinforce at close. Use variations to prevent fatigue.
  • Hero headline + CTA area
  • Proposal intro + investment section
  • Sales call opening + close
1x Per Piece
Financial Trading Platform
Key proof point: SEC/NFA-compliant financial trading application - the highest regulatory bar in financial software. Complete delivery from concept through compliance plus full go-to-market: persona research, targeting, ad management, and lead generation. Proves capability for complex, mission-critical projects.
  • Emphasize regulatory compliance for trust
  • Use in proposals, capability demonstrations
  • LinkedIn case studies, sales conversations
At Decision Points
Service-Specific Value
Use strategically when prospect is evaluating options:
- Growth Marketing: "Guaranteed leads - miss the target, you don't pay our fee"
- Web Apps: "ROI projections (high/mid/low) before you commit"
- AI Agents: "Save 20-25 hours/month, savings compound over time"
  • Proposal closing section
  • After price is discussed
  • Follow-up to stalled deals
Monthly Rotation
Service Focus
Rotate through key services monthly: Growth Marketing, Web Development, AI Agents. Prevents message fatigue while maintaining consistency.
  • Month 1: Lead guarantee focus
  • Month 2: Web app ROI focus
  • Month 3: AI automation focus
  • Month 4: Cross-service focus
Never
What NOT to Repeat
Avoid repeating the same exact phrasing within a single piece. Vary language while keeping message consistent. Never repeat claims without evidence.
  • Same sentence twice on one page
  • Identical paragraphs in sequence
  • Claims without supporting proof

Objection Responses

Prepared responses for common objections. Acknowledge, reframe, provide evidence.

1
Price Objection
"This is more expensive than I expected."
Recommended Response

"I understand, and you're right to think carefully about this investment. Here's how we prove value before you fully commit: For growth marketing, we guarantee the leads. We commit to a specific number upfront - miss it, and you don't pay our fee. For web development, we provide ROI projections (high/mid/low) before you approve the full build. You know your potential return before investing."

Channel-Specific Variations:
Email
"I get it, the investment is significant. That's why we guarantee the leads - miss our target, you don't pay our fee. Here's a case study showing how this works: [link]. Worth 5 minutes to see the numbers?"
Sales Call
"Can I share why we're confident enough to guarantee leads? [Explain process]. We stake our fee on delivering results. What would that kind of certainty mean for your business?"
Proposal
Include guarantee details prominently: "We commit to [X] leads. Miss it, you don't pay our fee for that month."
2
Timing Objection
"Now isn't the right time."
Recommended Response

"I hear that a lot, and I'm curious what makes you say that? In my experience, there are two kinds of 'not now': genuine conflicts that we should respect, and the natural hesitation that comes with any significant decision. Which feels more true for you? Because if it's the second, I'd gently push back: with our lead guarantee, there's minimal risk. You only pay our fee if we deliver the leads we commit to."

Channel-Specific Variations:
Email
"Totally fair. Quick question: is it genuinely not the right time, or is it that the timing will never feel perfect? Either answer is valid; I just want to understand. When would be the right time to revisit?"
Sales Call
"What would need to change for it to be the right time? [Listen carefully - often reveals real objection]"
Follow-up
"I've attached a case study of a business that said the same thing. They started 3 months later and wished they'd started sooner. The leads we guaranteed? They needed them all along."
3
Capability Objection
"We already have a marketing agency/developer."
Recommended Response

"That's great, and I'm glad you have those relationships. Here's the distinction: do they guarantee their results? For marketing, we commit to a specific lead number upfront - miss it, and you don't pay our fee. Most agencies promise results and deliver invoices. We stake our fee on actually delivering. For development, we provide ROI projections before you commit - and we've recommended clients NOT to build when the numbers didn't support it."

Channel-Specific Variations:
Website
Include clear "How We're Different" section highlighting the lead guarantee vs. typical agency promises.
Sales Call
"Tell me about your current agency relationship. Do they guarantee their leads? [Usually reveals the gap]"
LinkedIn
Post content about "Agencies that promise vs. agencies that guarantee." Educate before objection arises.
4
Skepticism Objection
"How do I know this will actually work?"
Recommended Response

"You don't have to take a leap of faith. We prove it before you buy - and if we can't, we'll tell you.

For growth marketing: We guarantee the leads. We commit to a specific number upfront. Miss it, you don't pay our fee. That's how confident we are.

For development: We recommended a client with $150K+ budget NOT to build. Our $5K discovery found market saturation and hidden costs. We'd rather earn $5K telling the truth than $150K building something that won't work.

And I can connect you with clients who will tell you exactly what working with us was like."

Channel-Specific Variations:
Proposal
Include case studies, specific outcomes achieved, and contact info for references.
Email
"Fair question. Here's why we're confident: we stake our fee on delivering. [Case study link]. Want to talk to a current client?"
Sales Call
Offer specific reference call with similar business. Make it easy to verify claims.
5
Decision Authority Objection
"I need to discuss this with my partner/team."
Recommended Response

"Absolutely, that's the right thing to do with a decision like this. A few questions to help make that conversation productive: What concerns do you think they'll raise? What information would help them feel confident about the lead guarantee? And would it be helpful if I joined that conversation to answer questions directly?"

Channel-Specific Variations:
Email
"I've attached a one-pager designed for exactly this conversation. It explains how the lead guarantee works. Happy to customize if there are specific concerns."
Sales Call
"Who else needs to be part of this decision? Should we schedule a call that includes them?"
Follow-up
Provide executive summary document specifically highlighting the guarantee and risk reduction.
6
Guarantee Skepticism
"The guarantee sounds too good to be true."
Recommended Response

"I appreciate the healthy skepticism. Here's why we can make this guarantee: we've refined our process over years and know exactly what drives results. We audit your current situation, identify the opportunities, and only commit to numbers we're confident we can hit. We're not guaranteeing magic - we're guaranteeing the output of a proven process. And if we're wrong? You don't pay our fee. We take the risk, not you."

Channel-Specific Variations:
Website
Include detailed FAQ section about how the guarantee works, with specific scenarios.
Sales Call
"What specifically makes it feel too good to be true? [Understand the concern and address directly]"
Proposal
Include clear terms: "If we commit to X leads and deliver fewer than X, our fee for that month is $0. You only pay ad spend."
