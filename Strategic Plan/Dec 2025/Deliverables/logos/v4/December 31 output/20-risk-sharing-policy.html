Philosophy Behind Risk-Sharing

Our target clients are 30-55 year old business owners who have been seeking proof. They have money but they are skeptical. A bold guarantee is often the difference between them saying "yes" and "let me think about it."

The Trust Deficit

Experienced business owners have a deeply ingrained skepticism toward agencies and consultants. They have heard promises before. They have paid retainers that delivered nothing. They have watched contractors disappear after the check cleared.

Our guarantee philosophy addresses this reality head-on:

  • Alignment over promises: We structure deals so our success depends on their success. This is not just marketing speak; it is structural.
  • Skin in the game: We put our fees at risk. When we stand to lose money if we fail, clients believe we will work harder not to fail.
  • Transparency over optimism: We show exactly how we calculated projections. No black boxes. No "trust us."
  • Reasonable scope: We guarantee what we control and are honest about what we do not control.

The Core Principle

A guarantee should create certainty for the client while creating clarity for us. If we cannot define when a guarantee applies and when it does not, we have not thought hard enough.

Strategic Intent

Our guarantee is a competitive weapon. In a market where skeptics compare three or four options, the consultant willing to share risk often wins. But we must be careful: an unlimited guarantee can bankrupt us. A toothless guarantee convinces no one. The sections that follow define the precise balance.

Types of Guarantees to Consider

Not all guarantees are created equal. Each type carries different risk profiles, legal implications, and psychological impact on skeptical buyers.

Recommended

Satisfaction Guarantee

What it means: If the client is not satisfied with deliverables, they receive a refund (full or partial).

Risk level: Moderate - subjective but controllable

Best for: Discovery phases, strategic plans, initial engagements

Legal clarity: High - easy to define and enforce

Recommended

Milestone Guarantee

What it means: Payment released only when specific, measurable milestones are achieved.

Risk level: Low - based on deliverables we control

Best for: Implementation phases, system builds, training programs

Legal clarity: Very high - objective and verifiable

Conditional

Performance Guarantee

What it means: If specific performance metrics are not achieved, client receives compensation.

Risk level: High - depends on external factors

Best for: Established clients with proven implementation

Legal clarity: Moderate - requires precise definitions

Use Cautiously

ROI Guarantee

What it means: We guarantee a specific return on the client's investment.

Risk level: Very high - many variables outside our control

Best for: Only when we have extensive control and data

Legal clarity: Low - complex to define and enforce

Recommended Guarantee Stack

For most engagements, we recommend a tiered approach that combines guarantee types:

Phase Guarantee Type Specific Terms
Discovery & Strategy 100% Satisfaction Guarantee Full refund if strategic plan does not meet stated objectives within 14 days of delivery
Implementation Milestone Guarantee Payment released only upon completion of defined deliverables with acceptance criteria
Optimization Conditional Performance Guarantee If KPIs not improved by X% within 90 days, additional optimization at no charge
Ongoing ROI Alignment Portion of fee tied to measurable results (not a pure guarantee, but aligned incentives)

Guarantee We Never Offer

We never offer an unconditional money-back guarantee with no time limit or criteria. This invites abuse and creates unlimited liability. Every guarantee must have: (1) specific criteria, (2) a time window, (3) client responsibilities, and (4) defined exclusions.

What ScaleUp Can and Cannot Guarantee

Legal clarity requires honest delineation. We guarantee what we control. We do not guarantee what we do not control. This protects us and builds credibility with skeptical clients.

What We CAN Guarantee

Deliverable-Based Guarantees

  • Completion of defined deliverables: Strategic plans, process documentation, system implementations, training materials
  • Quality standards: Deliverables will meet specified criteria (e.g., "comprehensive," "actionable," "customized to your business")
  • Timeline adherence: Deliverables completed within agreed timeframes, or specified compensation
  • Revision cycles: Specified number of revision rounds included
  • Methodology application: We will apply our proven frameworks as described

Effort-Based Guarantees

  • Access and responsiveness: Guaranteed response times for communication
  • Meeting commitments: Specified number of strategy sessions or check-ins
  • Resource allocation: Named team members dedicated to the engagement
  • Expertise application: Senior-level attention, not delegated to juniors

What We CANNOT Guarantee

Outcome-Based Limitations

  • Specific revenue numbers: "You will make $1M" (too many external variables)
  • Market response: How customers will react to changes
  • Competitor behavior: Actions of competitors that affect results
  • Economic conditions: Recessions, industry disruptions, global events
  • Client execution: Implementation of recommendations after handoff
  • Third-party performance: Vendors, employees, partners the client selects

Legal Framework for Guarantees

Statement Type Legally Binding? Recommended Approach
"We guarantee delivery by [date]" Yes Include with defined remedy if missed
"We guarantee you'll be satisfied" Yes (subjective) Define satisfaction criteria in writing
"We guarantee [X]% revenue increase" Yes (risky) Avoid or heavily condition
"We project [X]% improvement" No (if properly disclaimed) Use projections with clear disclaimers
"Our clients typically see..." No (testimonial) Safe if accurate and documented

Required Legal Disclaimer

All projections, estimates, and performance expectations are based on analysis of available data and historical patterns from similar engagements. Actual results may vary based on market conditions, implementation quality, competitive factors, and other variables outside the control of ScaleUp Ventures. Projections do not constitute guarantees of specific outcomes.

Rather than guaranteeing specific outcomes, we demonstrate value through rigorous ROI modeling. This gives clients confidence without exposing us to uncontrollable risk.

How We Present ROI to Clients

We always present three scenarios with clear assumptions:

Scenario Assumptions Typical Range How We Use It
Conservative Minimum expected improvement; partial implementation; market headwinds 1.5x - 2x ROI This is what we reference in guarantees
Moderate Expected improvement; full implementation; stable market 3x - 5x ROI This is our realistic expectation
Optimistic Maximum improvement; excellent execution; favorable conditions 7x - 10x+ ROI This is aspirational, not promised

The "Worst Case Acceptable" Standard

When presenting ROI to skeptical buyers, we ask: "Even if we hit only the conservative scenario, would the investment be worthwhile?" If the answer is yes, the engagement makes sense regardless of which scenario materializes. This removes the pressure of guaranteeing specific outcomes.

ROI Documentation Requirements

  • Baseline metrics documented before engagement begins
  • Improvement assumptions stated explicitly with sources
  • Three-scenario model presented in writing
  • Client signs acknowledgment of projections (not guarantees)
  • Quarterly measurement against projections during engagement
  • Post-engagement ROI calculation shared with client
"I want to show you three scenarios. The conservative scenario assumes things don't go perfectly - and even in that case, you're looking at recovering your investment within [X] months. That's the floor. The moderate scenario, which is what we typically see, puts you at [Y]x return. And if everything breaks right, we've seen clients hit [Z]x. I'm not going to promise you the optimistic number - but I am going to show you exactly how we calculated each of these."

Non-Negotiable Minimums

We require at least 25% upon signing for any engagement. This covers our opportunity cost, initial mobilization, and protects against clients who sign and then stall indefinitely. This portion is non-refundable after the first scheduled work session.

What Happens if Projections Are Not Met

This is where most consultants hide. We address it head-on because skeptical clients seek proof by vague accountability. Clear recourse builds trust.

Tiered Response Framework

Tier 1

Results Below Conservative Projection

Trigger: Results between 50-99% of conservative projection

Response:

  • Extended engagement time at no additional cost
  • Additional optimization sprint included
  • Revised strategy with root cause analysis
Tier 2

Significant Underperformance

Trigger: Results below 50% of conservative projection

Response:

  • Performance fee portion waived entirely
  • Credit toward future engagement (50% of base fee)
  • Joint post-mortem and documented learnings
Tier 3

Delivery Failure

Trigger: Core deliverables not completed as specified

Response:

  • Full refund of milestone payment for missed deliverable
  • No further obligation from client
  • Option to continue with revised scope

Measurement and Attribution Process

Before any recourse triggers, we must establish fair measurement:

Step Timing What Happens
Baseline Documentation Before engagement Both parties sign off on current metrics and data sources
Projection Agreement Before engagement Both parties sign off on projections and assumptions
Monthly Check-ins Throughout engagement Track progress, identify issues early, adjust if needed
90-Day Measurement 90 days post-implementation Formal comparison against projections with same data sources
Attribution Analysis If underperformance occurs Joint review to determine cause (us, client, external)

The Attribution Conversation

When results fall short, we ask: "What happened?" Possible causes include:

  • Our failure: Bad strategy, poor execution, missed deadlines
  • Client factors: Delayed implementation, resource constraints, scope changes
  • External factors: Market shifts, competitive moves, economic changes

Recourse terms apply differently based on attribution. We own our failures. We do not own factors outside our control.

"If we don't hit the conservative projection, here's what happens. First, we figure out why together - was it something we did wrong, something that changed on your end, or something in the market neither of us could control? If it's on us, we make it right. If the conservative number isn't there at 90 days and it's our execution, the performance portion of our fee goes away and you get credit toward additional work. I can put that in writing."

Client Responsibilities for Guarantee to Apply

A guarantee is a two-way commitment. We cannot guarantee results if the client does not hold up their end. This section defines what clients must do for guarantees to remain in effect.

Core Client Responsibilities

  • Timely Information: Provide requested data, documents, and access within 5 business days of request
  • Decision Authority: Designate a decision-maker with authority to approve recommendations
  • Meeting Attendance: Attend all scheduled sessions or provide 48-hour notice for rescheduling
  • Implementation Commitment: Implement agreed recommendations within specified timeframes
  • Resource Allocation: Assign internal resources as specified in engagement plan
  • Feedback Timeliness: Provide feedback on deliverables within agreed review periods
  • Honest Communication: Share relevant business challenges, constraints, and context
  • Payment Timeliness: Process payments within agreed terms

Specific Responsibility Requirements by Phase

Phase Client Must Within
Discovery Complete intake questionnaire and provide historical data 7 days of signing
Discovery Make key stakeholders available for interviews First 2 weeks
Strategy Review and provide feedback on draft strategy 7 days of delivery
Strategy Approve final strategy to proceed with implementation 14 days of revision
Implementation Provide system access and credentials as specified Before work begins
Implementation Assign internal implementation lead Before work begins
Optimization Maintain tracking and measurement systems Ongoing

Guarantee Void Conditions

Guarantees become void if the client:

  • Fails to implement recommendations without documented business reason
  • Delays approval or feedback beyond specified windows without extension agreement
  • Makes material changes to scope, strategy, or implementation without consultation
  • Withholds material information that would have changed our recommendations
  • Terminates internal resources critical to implementation without replacement
  • Breaches payment terms (more than 30 days past due)

How We Present This to Clients

We frame responsibilities as partnership, not adversarial terms:

"For our guarantee to work, we need to be partners in this. Here's what that means on your side. These aren't gotchas - they're the things that, in our experience, determine whether an engagement succeeds or fails. When clients do these things, we hit projections. When they don't, we don't. It's that simple."

Responsibility Tracking

Throughout the engagement, we document:

  • Requested information and date received
  • Scheduled meetings and attendance
  • Deliverables sent and feedback received
  • Implementation milestones and client completion
  • Any delays or issues and how resolved

This documentation protects both parties and creates clear accountability if questions arise about guarantee applicability.

Exclusions and Limitations

Clear exclusions prevent disputes and protect against unreasonable claims. Skeptical clients actually respect precise boundaries - it shows we have thought this through.

Standard Exclusions

Force Majeure

Guarantees do not apply during or after:

  • Natural disasters affecting operations
  • Pandemic-related business disruptions
  • Government actions (new regulations, shutdowns)
  • War, civil unrest, or terrorism
  • Major infrastructure failures (power, internet)

Material Business Changes

Guarantees do not apply if client:

  • Sells or merges the business during engagement
  • Changes ownership or control structure
  • Pivots to a different business model
  • Reduces workforce by more than 25%
  • Files for bankruptcy or reorganization

Third-Party Dependencies

Guarantees exclude failures caused by:

  • Technology vendors client selects
  • Contractors or agencies client hires
  • Employees client manages
  • Partners or suppliers client works with
  • Platforms (Google, Facebook, etc.) changing rules

Market Conditions

Guarantees do not cover:

  • Industry-wide downturns
  • Significant competitor actions
  • Changes in customer behavior/preferences
  • New market entrants or disruptors
  • Economic recession or inflation spikes

Liability Limitations

Limitation Type Our Position Rationale
Maximum Liability Cap Limited to fees paid in the engagement Standard professional services limitation; prevents catastrophic exposure
Consequential Damages Excluded (lost profits, lost opportunities, reputational harm) Impossible to quantify; would make consulting uninsurable
Indirect Damages Excluded (downstream effects, third-party claims) Cannot control ripple effects of business decisions
Punitive Damages Excluded Professional services are not subject to punitive awards in most jurisdictions
Time Limitation Claims must be raised within 90 days of engagement completion Allows closure; prevents indefinite liability

Essential Legal Language

IN NO EVENT SHALL ScaleUp Ventures' AGGREGATE LIABILITY EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES ACTUALLY PAID BY CLIENT UNDER THIS AGREEMENT. ScaleUp Ventures SHALL NOT BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION, LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, USE, GOODWILL, OR OTHER INTANGIBLE LOSSES, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES WERE FORESEEABLE OR WHETHER ScaleUp Ventures WAS ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

Specific Exclusions by Engagement Type

Strategy Engagements

  • We do not guarantee adoption of recommendations by client's team
  • We do not guarantee board or investor approval of strategies
  • We do not guarantee accuracy of third-party data used in analysis

Implementation Engagements

  • We do not guarantee performance of systems we do not build
  • We do not guarantee results in environments we cannot access
  • We do not guarantee timelines if dependent on client approvals that are delayed

Ongoing Advisory

  • We do not guarantee client will follow advice given
  • We do not guarantee outcomes of decisions client makes independently
  • We do not guarantee results if client terminates before recommended duration

How to Present the Guarantee in Sales

The guarantee is a sales tool - but only if presented correctly. Done wrong, it sounds like desperation. Done right, it builds immediate trust with skeptical buyers.

When to Introduce the Guarantee

Timing Approach Why It Works
Early in conversation Mention it briefly as a differentiator, then move on Plants the seed without making it seem desperate
When discussing price Frame it as "here's how we de-risk your investment" Justifies premium pricing with reduced risk
When they express skepticism Bring it up directly as evidence of confidence Addresses objection with action, not words
When comparing to competitors Ask "What guarantees are they offering?" Highlights differentiation without being salesy

Scripts for Common Scenarios

Opening Hook (first mention)

"One thing you should know upfront: we structure our engagements so that we share the risk with you. If we don't deliver what we promised, we don't get paid for it. That's not something you'll hear from most consultants. I'll explain exactly how that works when we get to the proposal stage."
Addressing Skepticism

"I understand you've been seeking proof. That's exactly why we built our guarantee the way we did. We don't ask you to trust us - we ask you to hold us accountable. Payment is tied to milestones. If we miss them, you don't pay. If results don't materialize, we have skin in the game too. I can show you exactly how that works in writing."
Justifying Premium Price

"Yes, we're more expensive than [competitor]. Here's the difference: if they don't deliver, you've lost your money. If we don't deliver, we lose ours. Our guarantee means your downside is capped, but your upside isn't. For someone who's been in business as long as you have, that math should make sense."
During Proposal Review

"Let me walk you through the guarantee section specifically. We've broken this into tiers. Tier 1: if we don't hit the conservative projection, we keep working at no additional cost. Tier 2: if we significantly underperform and it's on us, the performance portion of our fee goes away. Tier 3: if we flat-out don't deliver the core work, you get a refund. Those are real dollars at stake for us. Any questions about how that works?"

Objection Handling

Objection Response
"Sounds too good to be true" "Fair concern. Let me show you the specific conditions. There's nothing hidden - but there are responsibilities on both sides. That's what makes it real."
"What's the catch?" "The catch is that you have to hold up your end too. If you don't implement what we build, or you delay us past the timeline, the guarantee adjusts. It's a partnership, not a magic wand."
"I've heard guarantees before" "I'm sure you have. Ask me for the specifics. What exactly triggers it? What are the exclusions? If I can't answer those clearly, don't work with me."
"Can you just guarantee [X] revenue?" "I can't guarantee a number I don't fully control. But I can guarantee our work, tie my fee to milestones, and put real money at risk if projections aren't met. That's meaningful accountability."

What NOT to Say

  • Never promise specific revenue/profit numbers as a guarantee
  • Never say "money-back guarantee" without specifying conditions
  • Never compare to competitors' guarantees unless you know them exactly
  • Never position it as "we're so confident" without backing it up
  • Never make the guarantee the entire value proposition

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from both internal team members and clients, with clear answers that maintain consistency in how we present and apply our guarantee policy.

Q What exactly does "risk-sharing" mean in practice?
It means we structure deals so our payment depends on our performance. Specifically: (1) Payments are tied to milestones, not just time, (2) A portion of our fee can be withheld if projections are not met, and (3) We provide refund options for work that does not meet standards. We do not guarantee specific revenue outcomes, but we do guarantee our deliverables and put real money at risk.
Q If I am unhappy with the work, can I get a full refund?
For strategy and discovery work, yes - within 14 days of delivery, if the work does not meet the specifications we agreed to upfront. For implementation work, refunds apply to specific milestones that were not completed as specified. We do not offer unconditional refunds with no criteria - there must be a legitimate reason tied to our failure to deliver what was promised.
Q What happens if the conservative projection is not met?
First, we determine why together. If it is our failure (bad strategy, poor execution), we take responsibility: the performance portion of our fee is waived, and we provide additional work at no cost. If external factors caused it (market changes, economic conditions) or if the client did not implement recommendations, different terms apply. The goal is fair accountability, not blame-shifting.
Q Do I have responsibilities for the guarantee to apply?
Yes. This is a partnership. For guarantees to remain in effect, you need to: provide information when requested, make decisions in a timely manner, implement recommendations we agree on, and attend scheduled meetings. If you delay us significantly or choose not to implement recommendations, the guarantee adjusts accordingly. All responsibilities are documented in your agreement.
Q Why do you not just guarantee specific revenue results?
Because we do not control all the variables that determine revenue. We control our strategy, our deliverables, our expertise, and our execution. We do not control your market, your competitors, your team's execution after handoff, or economic conditions. A consultant who guarantees specific revenue numbers is either naive or misleading you. We guarantee what we control and put real money at risk on outcomes.
Q How do you measure whether projections were met?
We establish baseline metrics before the engagement begins - both parties sign off on current numbers and data sources. We use the same sources and methodology to measure results at the 90-day mark. This prevents disputes about measurement. If we cannot agree on baseline metrics upfront, we do not include performance-based elements in the engagement.
Q What are the main exclusions from the guarantee?
The main exclusions are: (1) Force majeure events like natural disasters or pandemics, (2) Major business changes like selling the company or changing business models, (3) Client failure to implement recommendations or meet their responsibilities, (4) Third-party failures (vendors, employees, platforms), and (5) Market-wide conditions affecting your industry. Full exclusions are listed in your agreement.
Q Is the guarantee different for different types of work?
Yes. Strategy work has a satisfaction guarantee (refund if you are not satisfied within 14 days). Implementation work has milestone guarantees (payment tied to deliverable completion). Optimization work may have performance-based elements (portion of fee tied to measurable improvement). Each type is designed for what makes sense in that context.
Q How long do I have to make a guarantee claim?
For satisfaction guarantees on deliverables: 14 days from delivery. For performance-based elements: within 30 days of the 90-day measurement date. For general claims: within 90 days of engagement completion. These time limits are important for closure and are standard in professional services.
Q What makes your guarantee different from other consultants?
Three things: (1) We define it precisely - you know exactly what triggers it, what the exclusions are, and what recourse you have, (2) We have real money at stake - our fee structure means we lose income if we underperform, and (3) We document everything - baselines, projections, responsibilities, and measurements are all in writing before we start. Most consultants either have no guarantee or have one so vague it is meaningless.

Q [Internal] What if a client tries to abuse the guarantee?
