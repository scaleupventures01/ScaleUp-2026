Graduated CTA Strategy

A thoughtful approach to building trust with potential clients through genuine value and honest conversation.

Awareness
Stage 2

1

Awareness Stage

Zero Risk
Buyer Psychology: Curious but skeptical
Goal: Educate without selling

Strategic Rationale

At this stage, potential clients are exploring options. They need to understand your approach and see if there's alignment before going further. These CTAs let them learn about your work without pressure, building familiarity through helpful content.

See Our Work
Work Examples CTA
Show real project types
Where to Use
  • Homepage hero section (secondary CTA)
  • Blog post sidebars
  • LinkedIn company page
  • About page
What Happens Next
Visitor lands on work examples page. Shows project types like architecture consulting, prototype development, and strategic advisory. Honest descriptions of scope and outcomes without inflated metrics.
Button Variants
  • "See Our Work"
  • "View Project Examples"
  • "Explore What We Do"
Example Projects
Financial trading app POC, startup architecture consulting, build-vs-buy decision support
Read Our Perspective
Thought Leadership CTA
Share your approach
Where to Use
  • Blog and articles section
  • LinkedIn posts
  • Newsletter signup pages
  • About page footer
What Happens Next
Visitor reads blog posts or articles about your methodology. Content focuses on lessons learned from real projects. Optional newsletter signup at end of articles.
Button Variants
  • "Read Our Blog"
  • "See Our Approach"
  • "Learn How We Think"
Content Examples
When to not build, PM infrastructure lessons, navigating regulated industries
Meet the Team
Personal Connection CTA
Build trust through transparency
Where to Use
  • About us section
  • Homepage secondary area
  • Contact page
  • LinkedIn profile links
What Happens Next
Visitor learns about the people behind the consultancy. Honest backgrounds, real experience. Shows the human side of working together.
Button Variants
  • "Meet the Team"
  • "About Us"
  • "Who We Are"
Key Elements
Real photos, honest bios, LinkedIn links, relevant experience highlights
2

Interest Stage

Low Risk
Buyer Psychology: Actively researching
Goal: Provide personalized value

Strategic Rationale

These potential clients have explored your content and want to learn more. They're interested in understanding if you can help with their specific situation. These CTAs offer a way to engage more directly while keeping things low-pressure.

Tell Us About Your Project
Project Inquiry CTA
Start a real conversation
Where to Use
  • Services pages
  • After reading case studies
  • Contact page secondary CTA
  • Blog post footers
What Happens Next
Simple form captures context about their situation. No commitment required. We respond with honest assessment of whether we can help and what that might look like.
Button Variants
  • "Share Your Situation"
  • "Tell Us More"
  • "Describe Your Challenge"
Form Fields
Name, email, brief description of situation or challenge, timeline (if any)
Ask a Question
Direct Question CTA
No strings attached
Where to Use
  • Homepage footer
  • Services pages
  • FAQ section
  • Contact page
What Happens Next
They send a question, we answer it. Simple email exchange. If it leads to a conversation, great. If not, they still got value from the interaction.
Button Variants
  • "Ask Us Anything"
  • "Send a Question"
  • "Get a Quick Answer"
Response Approach
Genuine, helpful answers within 24 hours. No sales pitch unless they ask for it.
See How We Work
Process Transparency CTA
Set expectations clearly
Where to Use
  • Services pages
  • FAQ section
  • About page
  • Pre-engagement content
What Happens Next
Shows our typical engagement process: initial conversation, scoping, proposal, work, delivery. Honest about timelines, pricing ranges, and what to expect at each stage.
Button Variants
  • "Our Process"
  • "How We Work"
  • "What to Expect"
Example Content
Decision analysis ($5K), startup advisory (hourly), POC development (project-based)
3

Consideration Stage

Medium Risk
Buyer Psychology: Evaluating vendors
Goal: Build preference and trust

Strategic Rationale

These potential clients are seriously considering working with you. They want to understand scope, fit, and what engagement would look like. These CTAs facilitate real conversations to determine if there's mutual fit.

Start a Conversation
Initial Call CTA
No-pressure discovery
Where to Use
  • Contact page primary CTA
  • Services page
  • After reading work examples
  • Homepage hero
What Happens Next
Brief form or calendar link for a 30-minute call. We listen to their situation, share relevant experience, and honestly assess if we can help. No obligation to proceed.
Button Variants
  • "Let's Talk"
  • "Schedule a Call"
  • "Start a Conversation"
Call Format
30 minutes, video or phone, focused on understanding their situation
Request a Reference
Reference Request CTA
Hear from past clients
Where to Use
  • Work examples page
  • After initial conversation
  • During proposal review
  • FAQ section
What Happens Next
We connect them with a past client who had a similar situation. Brief, candid conversation about their experience working with us. No scripted testimonial.
Button Variants
  • "Talk to a Past Client"
  • "Request a Reference"
  • "Hear From Our Clients"
Available References
Startup founders, product leaders, clients from regulated industries
Discuss Your Situation
Deep Dive CTA
Scoping conversation
Where to Use
  • After initial call
  • Email follow-up
  • Services page (secondary)
  • Contact page
What Happens Next
Longer conversation (45-60 min) to understand their specific situation in depth. We discuss potential approaches, timeline, and rough scope. Ends with clear next steps if there's fit.
Button Variants
  • "Go Deeper"
  • "Let's Scope This"
  • "Discuss Details"
Outcome
Clear proposal or honest "not a fit" conversation
4

Decision Stage

Commitment
Buyer Psychology: Ready to act
Goal: Reduce friction to close

Strategic Rationale

These potential clients are ready to work together. The goal is to make starting easy and set clear expectations. For an early-stage consultancy, a small initial engagement often makes more sense than a large commitment.

Start Small
Initial Engagement CTA
Low-risk first step
Where to Use
  • Proposal emails
  • After scoping call
  • Services page
  • Pricing discussions
What Happens Next
A defined, bounded engagement to start. Could be a decision analysis, architecture review, or short advisory sprint. Clear scope, clear deliverable, clear price. Builds trust for larger engagements if there's fit.
Button Variants
  • "Start with a Sprint"
  • "Begin with Analysis"
  • "Let's Start Small"
Example Engagements
Build-vs-buy decision ($5K), architecture review (1 week), prototype sprint (2-4 weeks)
Get a Proposal
Proposal Request CTA
Clear next step
Where to Use
  • After scoping conversation
  • Follow-up emails
  • Contact page
  • Services page
What Happens Next
We send a clear proposal with scope, timeline, deliverables, and pricing. No hidden fees or vague terms. They review, ask questions, and decide. Simple.
Button Variants
  • "Request a Proposal"
  • "Get Pricing"
  • "See the Details"
Proposal Content
Scope, approach, timeline, deliverables, pricing, terms. Plain language.

Implementation Best Practices

1

Be Honest

Every CTA should deliver on its promise. No bait-and-switch. If someone expects a conversation, give them a conversation, not a sales pitch.

2

One Clear Action

Each page should have one obvious next step. Make it easy for people to know what to do if they want to learn more or get in touch.

3

Respect Their Time

Don't ask for more information than you need. A simple contact form is often better than a 15-field questionnaire.

4

Follow Through

Respond promptly. If you say you'll get back in 24 hours, do it. Early-stage consultancies win on responsiveness and care.
