# ScaleUp Ventures: Email Signature CTAs

**Document Purpose:** Proof-forward email signature CTAs that drive website traffic
**Brand Voice:** Watchful, Measured, Decisive
**Target Audience:** Skeptical 50-65 year old business owners
**Last Updated:** December 31, 2025

---

## Strategic Context

Email signatures are passive marketing assets that work with every message sent. For ScaleUp Ventures, they serve a specific function: plant the seed of our proof-first methodology with every email touchpoint.

**Key Considerations for This Audience:**
- Skeptical of marketing claims (50% have been burned by agencies)
- Respond to concrete outcomes, not promises
- Value straightforward language over polish
- Trust actions more than words

The CTA must be proof-forward without being salesy. It should invite curiosity, not demand attention.

## 5 Email Signature CTA Options

### Option 1: "See the ROI Before You Spend"

**Full CTA Line:**
> See the ROI before you spend | scaleupventures.co

**Why It Works:**
- Directly states the core differentiator
- Action-oriented ("see") rather than passive
- Implies no-risk evaluation
- Speaks to the burned business owner's primary fear

**Best For:** All outbound communication, especially cold or warm outreach

---

### Option 2: "Proof First. Always."

**Full CTA Line:**
> Proof First. Always. | scaleupventures.co

**Why It Works:**
- Aligns with tagline for brand consistency
- Declarative and confident
- Three words = maximum memorability
- The period after "Always" signals finality and conviction

**Best For:** Brand-building, ongoing client communication, signature consistency

---

### Option 3: "We prove it before you buy."

**Full CTA Line:**
> We prove it before you buy. | scaleupventures.co

**Why It Works:**
- Plain language that a 60-year-old business owner immediately understands
- First-person "we" establishes accountability
- Differentiates from agencies that promise and disappear
- Echoes the core brand promise verbatim

**Best For:** Prospects in evaluation stage, proposal follow-ups

---

### Option 4: "Your numbers. Your decision."

**Full CTA Line:**
> Your numbers. Your decision. | scaleupventures.co

**Why It Works:**
- Empowers the recipient rather than selling to them
- Implies transparency and control
- Appeals to business owners who value autonomy
- Subtle nod to ROI modeling without stating it

**Best For:** Decision-maker communication, executive-level outreach

---

### Option 5: "Results you can measure before you invest."

**Full CTA Line:**
> Results you can measure before you invest. | scaleupventures.co

**Why It Works:**
- Outcome-focused language
- "Measure" signals accountability and data
- "Before you invest" addresses the risk concern directly
- Longer format for signatures that allow more text

**Best For:** Formal proposals, contract communication, detailed correspondence

---

## Recommended Primary Choice

### Primary Recommendation: Option 2 - "Proof First. Always."

**Rationale:**

1. **Brand Consistency:** Aligns directly with the approved tagline, reinforcing brand recognition across all touchpoints.

2. **Brevity:** At three words plus one, it is the shortest option - critical for email signatures where space and attention are limited.

3. **Tone Alignment:** Matches the brand voice attributes of confident, accountable, and straightforward without being aggressive or salesy.

4. **Memorability:** The repetition of the tagline across website, signature, and collateral builds cumulative brand recall.

5. **Audience Fit:** Skeptical business owners do not want to be sold - they want signals of substance. "Proof First" signals substance without asking for anything.

**Secondary Recommendation:** Option 3 - "We prove it before you buy."

Use this alternative when the recipient is in active evaluation mode or following up on a proposal. The first-person "we" and direct statement of the value proposition works well for prospects who need reassurance.

## Implementation Notes

### Formatting Guidelines

1. **CTA Placement:** Always as the final line of the signature, separated by a blank line from contact information.

2. **Link Treatment:** The URL should be a clickable hyperlink. Do not use "www." - the cleaner "scaleupventures.co" is more professional.

3. **Separator:** Use a vertical bar " | " between the CTA and URL. This creates visual separation without adding clutter.

4. **Font Consistency:** CTA should match the signature font. Do not use a different font, color, or size for the CTA.

5. **No Images in CTA:** Keep the CTA as text only. Email clients handle images inconsistently, and text-based CTAs are more reliable.

### When to Use Each Option

| Context | Recommended CTA |
|---------|-----------------|
| Standard outbound email | Proof First. Always. |
| Proposal follow-up | We prove it before you buy. |
| Cold outreach | See the ROI before you spend |
| Executive communication | Your numbers. Your decision. |
| Formal contract correspondence | Results you can measure before you invest. |

### Testing Recommendation

Track which CTA variant generates the most website clicks over a 90-day period. If using email tracking tools, compare:
- Click-through rate by CTA variant
- Conversion from email signature click to website inquiry

This data will inform future optimization.
