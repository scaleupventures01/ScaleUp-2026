# ScaleUp Ventures: Words We Use / Words We Avoid

**Document Type:** Brand Voice Reference
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** December 31, 2025
**Purpose:** Language guidelines for all ScaleUp Ventures communications

---

## Why Language Matters

ScaleUp Ventures serves data-driven business owners aged 30-55 who need to see proof before committing. They can smell marketing speak from a mile away. Every word choice either builds or erodes trust.

**Our Voice Attributes:**
- **Watchful** - We observe carefully before acting
- **Measured** - We quantify, we prove, we show the math
- **Decisive** - When we speak, we speak with conviction

**Core Principle:** If a phrase could appear on any agency's website, it should not appear on ours.

## Part 1: Words and Phrases We Use

### Category: Proof and Evidence

| Word/Phrase | When to Use | Example in Context |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Model** (as verb) | Describing our ROI analysis process | "We model your return before you invest a dollar." |
| **Documented** | Referencing our track record or deliverables | "Documented results from similar engagements show 3-4x return." |
| **Projection** | Discussing expected outcomes with specificity | "The projection shows payback within 90 days based on your current metrics." |
| **Based on your numbers** | Grounding recommendations in client data | "This recommendation is based on your numbers, not industry averages." |
| **Measurable** | Describing outcomes we commit to | "Every engagement includes measurable success criteria." |

### Category: Trust and Relationship

| Word/Phrase | When to Use | Example in Context |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Talk to our clients** | Inviting prospect validation | "Don't take our word for it. Talk to our clients." |
| **We've seen this before** | Establishing pattern recognition | "We've seen this before with staffing agencies at your stage." |
| **Honest assessment** | Introducing candid feedback | "An honest assessment: this project won't ROI at current pricing." |
| **Long-term** | Describing our relationship model | "We structure for long-term partnership, not one-time projects." |
| **Accountable** | Stating our commitment model | "We're accountable to the outcomes we project." |

### Category: Action and Progress

| Word/Phrase | When to Use | Example in Context |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Implementation** | Describing execution phase | "Implementation begins once we agree on success criteria." |
| **Payback period** | Discussing ROI timeline | "The payback period on this investment is 4-6 months." |
| **Milestone** | Breaking down project phases | "Each milestone has defined deliverables and success metrics." |
| **Next step** | Moving conversations forward | "The next step is a 30-minute call to review your current metrics." |
| **Decision point** | Identifying when clients need to act | "You're at a decision point: invest in lead generation or continue relying on referrals." |

### Category: Expertise and Method

| Word/Phrase | When to Use | Example in Context |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **In our experience** | Sharing pattern observations | "In our experience, veterinary practices underinvest in online booking." |
| **Typically** | Setting realistic expectations | "Typically, a project of this scope takes 8-12 weeks." |
| **Specifically** | Adding precision to recommendations | "Specifically, we'd focus on your after-hours call handling." |
| **We recommend against** | Advising clients away from bad investments | "We recommend against that approach - the ROI doesn't justify the cost." |
| **The math shows** | Grounding strategy in data | "The math shows you're leaving $15K/month on the table in missed leads." |

## Part 2: Words and Phrases We Avoid

### Category: Consultant-Speak (Vague, Sounds Impressive, Means Nothing)

| Avoid This | Why It Fails | Use Instead |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Leverage** | Overused corporate jargon that signals inauthenticity | "Use" or be specific: "Your existing customer list can drive 40% of new leads" |
| **Synergy** | Peak consultant-speak; immediately triggers skepticism | Describe the specific benefit: "These two systems share data automatically" |
| **Holistic** | Vague; doesn't tell the client what they're actually getting | "Integrated" or list the specific components |
| **Optimize** | Overused; doesn't specify the outcome | State the metric: "Reduce cost per lead from $85 to $45" |
| **Best-in-class** | Meaningless superlative; every competitor claims this | "Documented 3x return for similar clients" |

### Category: Hype and Empty Promises

| Avoid This | Why It Fails | Use Instead |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Revolutionary** | Hyperbole that data-driven owners see through immediately | Describe the specific improvement: "Cuts response time from 4 hours to 15 minutes" |
| **Game-changing** | Over-promises and triggers skepticism | "This will add $X to your monthly revenue" |
| **Transform your business** | Vague transformation promises don't satisfy owners who need to see the numbers | "Add $50K in annual revenue through automated lead capture" |
| **Unlock your potential** | Self-help language that doesn't belong in B2B | "Generate 30% more leads from your existing traffic" |
| **Skyrocket** | Exaggeration that responsible advisors don't use | Provide the actual projection with supporting data |

### Category: Generic Marketing Language

| Avoid This | Why It Fails | Use Instead |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Solutions** | So generic it means nothing | Name the specific deliverable: "website," "automation," "lead system" |
| **Innovative** | Everyone claims innovation; it's become meaningless | Describe what's actually new and why it matters to them |
| **Cutting-edge** | Tech buzzword that alienates non-tech-native clients | "Current" or explain the practical benefit |
| **Seamless** | Oversimplifies complexity; sounds like a sales pitch | "Integrated" or acknowledge the work involved |
| **Ecosystem** | Tech jargon that obscures meaning | "Your tools," "your systems," "your existing setup" |

### Category: Pressure and Manipulation

| Avoid This | Why It Fails | Use Instead |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Limited time offer** | Pressure tactics erode trust with skeptical buyers | Let the ROI model speak for itself; if it's compelling, they'll act |
| **Act now** | Artificial urgency insults intelligent business owners | "Here's what the delay costs you each month: [specific number]" |
| **Exclusive** | Often meaningless; triggers skepticism | If something is genuinely limited, explain why honestly |
| **You need to** | Commanding language that offends experienced owners | "The data suggests" or "We recommend" |
| **Trust us** | Asking for trust instead of earning it | "Here's the evidence" or "Talk to our clients" |

## Part 3: Before and After Transformations

### Example 1: Opening a Sales Email

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "We offer revolutionary solutions that will transform your business and unlock its true potential. Our innovative team leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver best-in-class results."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "We model ROI before you invest a dollar. For staffing agencies your size, we've documented 3x return on lead generation projects. I can show you the math if you're interested."

**Why It Works:** Specific. Proof-based. Invites verification rather than demanding trust.

---

### Example 2: Describing Our Process

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "We take a holistic approach to optimize your digital ecosystem, creating synergy between your marketing and operations to deliver transformative results."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "We start by building a financial model using your actual numbers - not industry benchmarks. You'll see exactly how you make your money back before you spend it. Then we implement in 8-12 week phases, each with measurable milestones."

**Why It Works:** Concrete timeline. Real deliverable. Accountable language.

---

### Example 3: Handling Objections About Price

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "While we're not the cheapest option, we deliver best-in-class solutions that provide exceptional value. Our innovative approach ensures you get maximum return on your investment."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "Fair question. At $25K, payback comes in 4-5 months based on your current lead-to-close rate. After that, you're looking at $8K-$10K monthly in additional revenue. I can walk you through the model and you can stress-test the assumptions."

**Why It Works:** Acknowledges the price concern directly. Provides specific numbers. Invites scrutiny.

---

### Example 4: Following Up After a Proposal

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "Just following up on our game-changing proposal. We're excited about the synergies we identified and believe this could be revolutionary for your business. Let us know if you have any questions!"

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "Wanted to follow up on the proposal. The projection shows $47K annual value against a $22K investment - I'd be happy to walk through the assumptions if anything seems off. Also happy to connect you with a current client in your industry if that would help."

**Why It Works:** References specific numbers. Offers validation through client reference. No artificial enthusiasm.

---

### Example 5: Website Hero Section

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "Innovative Solutions for Business Growth. We leverage cutting-edge technology to help you unlock your company's full potential. Partner with us to transform your operations."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "See your return before you invest. We build a custom ROI model using your actual numbers. You'll know exactly how you make your money back - before you spend a dollar."

**Why It Works:** Immediate proof-based positioning. Plain language. Addresses the core need of data-driven business owners.

---

### Example 6: Case Study Headline

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "How We Delivered Revolutionary Results for a Thriving Staffing Agency"

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "Staffing Agency: $18K Investment, $67K First-Year Return. Here's the Math."

**Why It Works:** Numbers in the headline. Invites scrutiny with "here's the math." No superlatives.

---

### Example 7: Describing Our Team

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "Our passionate team of rockstar experts delivers innovative solutions with cutting-edge technology. We're not just consultants - we're trusted partners in your success journey."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "The founder has 15 years of experience in development and growth strategy, supported by a team of specialists in marketing, automation, and implementation. Every client project involves direct founder oversight."

**Why It Works:** Specific experience. Clear structure. No self-congratulating adjectives.

---

### Example 8: Explaining AI Capabilities

**BEFORE (Off-Brand):**
> "We leverage cutting-edge AI and machine learning to deliver transformative automation solutions that revolutionize your operational efficiency."

**AFTER (On-Brand):**
> "We use AI to handle your after-hours calls and book appointments directly - that's about 15 hours a week your team gets back. Your staff focuses on higher-value work; the system handles the repetitive tasks."

**Why It Works:** Tangible benefit (15 hours/week). Business outcome focus. No tech jargon requiring translation.

## Quick Reference Card

### The 5-Second Test

Before publishing any content, ask:

1. **Would a skeptical 45-year-old business owner roll their eyes?** If yes, rewrite.
2. **Does it include a specific number, timeline, or verifiable claim?** If no, add one.
3. **Could a competitor copy-paste this onto their website?** If yes, make it more specific.
4. **Does it ask for trust or demonstrate evidence of trustworthiness?** Choose evidence.
5. **Would I say this in a face-to-face conversation with a skeptical prospect?** If it sounds too "marketing," rewrite.

### Words That Signal Authenticity

These words/phrases immediately signal that we're different:

- "Based on your actual numbers"
- "Talk to our clients"
- "Here's the math"
- "We recommend against this project"
- "The projection shows"
- "In 8-12 weeks"
- "Documented results"

### Phrases That Immediately Undermine Trust

These words/phrases trigger skepticism with our audience:

- Revolutionary, game-changing, transform
- Leverage, synergy, holistic, ecosystem
- Best-in-class, cutting-edge, innovative
- Trust us, believe in, partner with us
- Unlock, skyrocket, supercharge
- Limited time, act now, exclusive offer

## Application by Channel

### Website Copy
- Lead with proof statements
- Every claim gets a number or a client reference
- "See the math" as a recurring motif

### Email Outreach
- Subject lines reference specific outcomes, not vague value
- Body text acknowledges skepticism directly
- Close with offer to verify (talk to clients, review projections)

### Proposals
- Structure around outcomes and success criteria
- ROI model prominently featured
- Plain language for non-technical owners

### Social Media / LinkedIn
- Share client results with permission
- Teach rather than sell
- Acknowledge industry challenges honestly

### Sales Conversations
- Ask more than tell
- Use client's own language and metrics
- End with "what questions do you have about this projection?"
