CONCEPT 1: THE KEYSTONE (REFINED)

PRIMARY FOCUS

Strategic Foundation: The keystone is the wedge-shaped stone at the apex of an arch—the LAST stone placed, and the one that locks everything else in place. For ScaleUp's clients, proof is the keystone. Without it, nothing holds together.

CEO Feedback: "Nice concept, but the image doesn't translate. The navy lines behind it don't make sense to me."
Solution: Remove ALL flanking elements. The keystone stands alone. Clean. Simple. Powerful.

Version A: Point-Up (Growth)

Version B: Point-Down (Stability)

Version C: Dimensional

Same trapezoid shape with subtle 3D effect - darker gold edge on right side to create impression of actual stone without being illustrative.

CONCEPT 2: THE PIVOT (REFINED)

PRIMARY FOCUS

Strategic Foundation: A pivot is the single point where transformation happens. For ScaleUp's audience, it's the moment doubt becomes certainty. They've seen the proof. Now everything rotates around that evidence.

CEO Feedback: "The pivot itself might be good, but execution unclear."
Solution: Strip to absolute essentials. Two bars. One point. That's it. NO orbital lines. NO extra complexity.

Simplified Geometry

/ / / O--------- THREE ELEMENTS ONLY: 1. Horizontal bar (navy) - extends LEFT 2. Angled bar (navy) - extends UP-RIGHT at 35-40° 3. Gold circle at meeting point Both bars: SAME width/weight Gold circle: 2x bar width NO orbital lines, NO motion trails

CONCEPT 3: THE BENCHMARK (NEW)

NEW CONCEPT

Strategic Foundation: A benchmark is a surveyor's mark—a point of known position and elevation from which all other measurements are taken. Before you can build anything, you need a benchmark. A fixed point of truth.

This speaks directly to skeptical business owners: Before investing in growth, they need ONE reliable data point. ScaleUp provides that benchmark.

Visual Metaphor

/\ / \ /----\ Gold line = the benchmark / \ (the point of known truth) /________\ Triangle: Navy outline or solid Horizontal line: Gold, crosses at ~1/3 height

Alternate: Simplified

/\ / \ ====== Gold line extends beyond triangle Triangle sits ABOVE benchmark line Cleaner, more logo-like

Why it works for 60-year-old business owners: Familiar concept (surveyors, engineers). Implies precision, not guesswork. Foundation before building.

CONCEPT 4: THE CORNERSTONE (NEW)

NEW CONCEPT

Strategic Foundation: Different from keystone—the cornerstone is the FIRST stone laid. It determines the alignment for the entire structure. Everything else is built in reference to it.

The proof-of-concept IS the cornerstone. The first solid thing laid down. Everything else is built in reference to that initial proof.

Visual Metaphor

___________ | | | | Navy square or rectangle | | Gold accent on corner |___________| ^^^ gold corner Could be: corner notch, corner triangle, or gold corner radius Optional: 5-10° rotation for dynamism

Alternate: Stacked Reference

________ | Navy | |________|____ | Gold | |________| Two overlapping rectangles Gold (front) = the proof Navy (back) = what comes after

Evaluation Criteria

1. The Squint Test

At 16px thumbnail, is it recognizable and distinct?

2. The Fax Test

In pure black and white, does it still work?

3. The Trust Test

Would a skeptical 60-year-old think "these people are serious"?

4. The Story Test

Can you explain it in one sentence?

5. The White Background Test

Does it look confident on pure white?
