Section 2: Target Audience Psychology

Demographic Profile

  • Age: 30-55+ years old
  • Status: Established business owners
  • Net worth: $2M-$20M
  • Experience: 20-40 years in business

Psychographic Profile

  • Skeptical - Burned by consultants before
  • Conservative - Risk-averse with investments
  • Results-oriented - Need proof, not promises
  • Relationship-driven - Value personal trust

What They REJECT

  • Flashy, trendy design ("looks like a startup")
  • Complex or clever marks (requires explanation)
  • Anything that feels "digital-first" or "tech-bro"
  • Gradients, shadows, 3D effects (feels gimmicky)
  • Sans-serif fonts without gravitas

What They TRUST (Research-Backed)

  • Symmetry - Creates sense of order and reliability
  • Larger typography - Confident, not hiding
  • Clean layouts - Nothing to hide, clear communication
  • Serif or refined sans-serif - Heritage and authority
  • Blue and Gold combinations - Trust + premium quality
  • Geometric shapes - Especially squares/rectangles = stability

A

THE KEYSTONE

The stone that locks everything in place (Priority 1)

Strategic Rationale

The keystone is the wedge-shaped stone at the apex of an arch—the LAST stone placed, and the one that locks everything else in place. Without it, the arch collapses.

Connection to Brand:
  • ScaleUp's proof-first approach IS the keystone
  • Without verified proof, business transformation collapses
  • The keystone represents the "moment of certainty"

Visual Specification

THE SIMPLIFIED KEYSTONE (Point-Down - Traditional Orientation) _____________________ \ / \ / \ / \ / \___________ / PROPORTIONS: - Top edge: 100 units - Bottom edge: 60 units - Height: 80 units - Side angles: ~14 degrees from vertical FILL: Solid Gold (#D4A853) OUTLINE: None CORNERS: Sharp (0 radius) BACKGROUND: Pure White (#FFFFFF)

Version Variants

Version Description
A1: Pure Keystone Just the gold trapezoid. Most minimal expression.
A2: Dimensional Main face Gold (#D4A853), right edge Muted Gold (#B8956E). Creates impression of substance.
A3: With Outline Gold fill with thin navy outline for more defined edge.
B

THE PIVOT

Where transformation happens (Priority 2)

Strategic Rationale

A pivot is the single point where transformation happens—where doubt becomes certainty. The horizontal represents "stuck," the ascending represents "growth," and the gold point represents "the proof moment."

The Visual Story:
  • LEFT BAR → Where client WAS (stuck, uncertain, seeking proof)
  • GOLD POINT → Where ScaleUp intervened (the proof)
  • RIGHT BAR → Where client IS GOING (growth, certainty)

Visual Specification

THE SIMPLIFIED PIVOT / / / / O--------- GEOMETRY: - Horizontal bar: Navy (#0F1729), extends LEFT - Ascending bar: Navy (#0F1729), extends UP-RIGHT at 35-40° - Gold circle: At the meeting point (#D4A853) PROPORTIONS: - Each bar: 100 units length - Bar thickness: 14 units - Gold circle diameter: 28 units (2x bar thickness) - Angle: 35-40 degrees from horizontal

Construction Rules

  1. Bars do NOT overlap — they MEET at the gold point
  2. Both bars identical weight — same element, transformed state
  3. Bar ends are squared — clean, professional
  4. Gold point is visual anchor — draws the eye
  5. Negative space creates openness — ascending feel

THE BENCHMARK

The point of known truth (Backup)

Strategic Rationale

A benchmark is a surveyor's mark—a point of known position from which all other measurements are taken. Before building anything, you need a benchmark.

Connection to Brand: Before investing in growth, clients need ONE reliable data point. ScaleUp provides that benchmark—verified proof from which all business decisions can be measured.

Visual Specification

THE BENCHMARK /\ / \ / \ ======== (gold) GEOMETRY: - Equilateral triangle: Navy (#0F1729) outline OR solid - Horizontal line: Gold (#D4A853) - Line crosses at 1/3 height from bottom - Line extends slightly beyond triangle edges

Section 6: Evaluation Criteria

The 5 Tests (All Must Pass)

SQUINT TEST

At 16px favicon, is mark recognizable?

FAX TEST

In pure B&W, does it work?

TRUST TEST

Would a skeptical 45-year-old think "serious"?

STORY TEST

Can you explain meaning in one sentence?

WHITE TEST

Does it look confident on pure white?

Quality Checklist (From Research)

  • Single distinctive element (like Deloitte's green dot)
  • Works at favicon size (16px)
  • Works in pure black and white
  • No more than 2 colors
  • No gradients, shadows, or effects
  • Geometric precision, clean edges
  • Immediately professional impression
  • Does NOT require explanation to understand
  • Timeless (not trendy)
  • Background is pure white (#FFFFFF)

Section 7: What We Learned from Rejections

V1 Rejection: "Looks like junior designer work"

Root Cause: Missing creative ideation layer; jumped from requirements to generic shapes

Fix Applied

Developed meaningful metaphors (Keystone, Pivot) with strategic connection

V2 Rejection: "Navy lines don't make sense"

Root Cause: Added complexity without clear purpose

Fix Applied

Stripped to absolute essentials; one concept = one clear idea

V2 Rejection: "Needs white background"

Root Cause: Used cream/gold backgrounds that limit application

Fix Applied

WHITE #FFFFFF is now MANDATORY specification
