# Client Discovery Interview Techniques for Brand Identity Projects

## Executive Summary

Client discovery is the critical foundation of successful brand identity projects. This research compiles best practices from top designers, behavioral science principles, and proven frameworks for uncovering the emotional truth of a brand while avoiding the miscommunication that leads to rejected designs.

---

## Part 1: Why Discovery Matters

### The Business Case for Thorough Discovery

- **60% fewer revisions** are reported in projects with comprehensive client briefs
- Organizations investing in structured discovery experience scope creep in only **28% of projects** vs. 40% for those that don't
- Discovery questionnaires act as "a cheat code for better client-agency relationships"

### What Discovery Achieves

1. **Uncovers the brand's "soul"** - the essence that makes one brand distinct from another
2. **Aligns stakeholders** - ensures everyone is on the same page before design begins
3. **Reduces miscommunication** - prevents the back-and-forth that kills projects
4. **Builds trust** - establishes a collaborative relationship from the start

> "Brand discovery is the excavation phase where the essence of a brand is unearthed, laying bare its core values, identity, and the unique proposition it offers to the world."

## Part 2: Learning from Master Designers

### Paula Scher (Pentagram) - Research-First Approach

Paula Scher's discovery philosophy centers on comprehensive research before any design work:

> "With all clients, you want to know as much about them as you possibly can before you start designing."

**Her Key Discovery Elements:**
- Client's goals, wants, and needs
- Current problems and challenges
- Who the client is at their core
- What their audience expects of them

**Her Guiding Principle:**
> "You're both creating a visual language for how they are inside, and how they want to be outside."

**Design Philosophy:**
- Design should be a "state of play" - creativity requires mental freedom
- First ideas are rarely the best; explore many directions
- Process should be intuitive and fluid, incorporating experiences
- The public should recognize a brand "without even seeing the logo"

### Aaron Draplin (Draplin Design Co.) - Pragmatic Craftsmanship

Draplin brings a "blue-collar" design ethos with:
- Background research and geometric structure analysis
- Typography and color choice exploration
- Pride and craftsmanship in every project
- Building beloved brands from simple, powerful identities (Field Notes example)

## Part 3: The Core Discovery Framework

### The 9-Step Brand Discovery Process

**Step 1: Define Brand Purpose**
- WHAT: What products/services do you offer?
- HOW: What unique methods and values set you apart?
- WHY: What deeper cause drives your brand beyond profit?

**Step 2: Craft Brand Vision**
- Where do you envision your brand in 5-10 years?
- What long-term aspirations guide your direction?
- What impact do you want on your industry and community?

**Step 3: Identify Brand Values**
- Which 3-5 core principles define your culture?
- What behaviors exemplify each value?
- How will you communicate these consistently?

**Step 4: Understand Target Audience**
- Demographics: age, location, income, education
- Psychographics: interests, lifestyle, values
- What pain points does your brand solve?

**Step 5: Conduct Market Research**
- Who are your main competitors?
- What are their strengths and weaknesses?
- Complete a SWOT analysis

**Step 6: Establish Awareness Goals**
- What is your current brand awareness level?
- Set measurable awareness targets
- Identify strategies for growth

**Step 7: Define Brand Personality**
- Describe your brand using human characteristics
- What tone and communication style reflects your identity?

**Step 8: Develop Brand Voice**
- How should your brand sound across platforms?
- Create guidelines for consistent messaging

**Step 9: Create Brand Tagline**
- Develop memorable words encapsulating your essence
- Test with your audience and refine

## Part 4: The Complete Question Library

### Company & Business Questions (14 Essential)

1. What is your company/organization/product/service name?
2. Can you describe your business?
3. What services or products do you provide?
4. How big is your company? (employees, revenue)
5. How long has your company been established?
6. **Why was your company started in the first place, and what was the motivation?**
7. Who are your main competitors?
8. How do your competitors market themselves?
9. What sets your company apart from the competition?
10. What are the strengths of your company?
11. What are your weaknesses?
12. What are the long-term goals of the company? Where do you see it in 5, 10, 30 years?
13. **If you had to describe your business in one word, what would it be and why?**
14. What are your company's values and/or mission statement?

### Branding & Identity Questions (11 Essential)

15. What is the current logo?
16. Do you have a strapline or slogan that goes along with your logo?
17. Why are you looking to change the logo?
18. **What words describe how you feel when looking at your current logo and branding?**
19. What do you like and dislike about the current logo?
20. **What do you want the new logo to accomplish?**
21. What three attributes would you like your target audience to think of when looking at your new branding?
22. Which is a better fit for your brand: Traditional or modern?
23. Which is a better fit for your brand: Friendly or corporate?
24. Which is a better fit for your brand: High end or cost-effective?
25. Why does your current branding use those colors, fonts, etc.?

### Target Audience Questions (9 Essential)

26. Who is the primary target audience?
27. What is the target audience's age group?
28. Are they mainly male or female?
29. Where do most of your audience live?
30. What is the average household income of your target audience?
31. How do most of your customers find out about your company?
32. How do you plan to communicate with your target audience?
33. **If your customers had to describe your company in one word, what would it be and why?**
34. Are there any new markets you'd like to break into?

### Design Preference Questions (8 Essential)

35. What colors or color palettes does the company typically use, and why?
36. Where will the logo be mainly used? Print, web, etc.?
37. Are there any elements from the existing logo you'd like to keep, and why?
38. Are there any restrictions to consider when designing the new logo?
39. Is there anything that must be included (existing brand elements, words, icons)?
40. Looking at your rivals' branding, what logos do you think work, and why?
41. Are there any logos you don't think work, and why?
42. **In your opinion, what defines a successful logo?**

### Project Management Questions (8 Essential)

43. Do you have a budget in mind for the new logo?
44. Do you have a deadline that needs to be considered?
45. **Who will be the internal decision-makers on this project?** (feedback and approvals)
46. Will there be anyone else involved? Any third-party sub-contractors or agencies?
47. How frequently would you like to meet? Weekly? Monthly?
48. How many revisions or concepts would you like to see?
49. What form would you like the final work to take?
50. Is there anything else you'd like to add that we haven't already covered?

## Part 5: Uncovering Emotional Truth

### The 5 Questions That Reveal Brand Values

These questions, designed specifically for emotional depth, help uncover what a brand truly stands for:

**1. Why were you motivated to start your business?**
- Identifies core values that inspired the business's founding
- Reveals whether driven by passion, innovation, or personal experience
- Creates brands that resonate authentically

**2. What makes you different than your competitors?**
- Reveals unique selling proposition (USP)
- Determines how you provide unique value
- Develops values people will remember

**3. What problems does your ideal client face, and how do you solve them?**
- Ensures brand values speak to audience needs
- Allows crafting values that address specific frustrations
- Connects brand purpose to real-world impact

**4. What are they risking if they don't work with you?**
- Explores consequences beyond immediate problems
- Articulates deeper solutions through brand values
- Addresses long-term impacts of unresolved issues

**5. How do you want clients to feel when they work with you?**
- Focuses on emotional outcomes
- Reveals core business values through desired feelings
- Uncovers what you truly value as a business

### Jobs-to-Be-Done Framework for Brand Identity

Understanding the three components helps create complete, differentiated brand identity:

**Functional Jobs**
- What practical task does the brand help accomplish?
- What utility does it provide?
- Example: A vacuum cleaner cleans carpets quickly

**Emotional Jobs**
- What internal feelings or experiences does the brand deliver?
- How does the brand make customers feel confident, safe, or satisfied?
- Example: A fitness app makes users feel confident

**Social Jobs**
- How does the brand help customers be perceived by others?
- What identity, status, or belonging does it signal?
- Example: Tesla owners are seen as socially responsible

> "Brands that align with social jobs easily become part of a customer's identity."

### Brand Archetype Framework (Carl Jung)

The 12 archetypes provide a psychological framework for brand personality:

1. **The Hero** - Courage, achievement
2. **The Innocent** - Purity, optimism
3. **The Everyman** - Belonging, authenticity
4. **The Rebel** - Liberation, disruption
5. **The Explorer** - Freedom, discovery (The North Face)
6. **The Creator** - Innovation, imagination
7. **The Ruler** - Control, leadership
8. **The Magician** - Transformation, vision
9. **The Lover** - Passion, intimacy (Victoria's Secret)
10. **The Caregiver** - Nurturing, protection
11. **The Entertainer** - Joy, spontaneity
12. **The Sage** - Wisdom, intelligence (Google)

**Discovery Process for Archetypes:**
1. Interview your brand as if it were a live person
2. Ask questions of stakeholders
3. Interview staff AND customers
4. Identify disparities between internal and external perceptions
5. Decide whether to realign brand or adjust target audience

## Part 6: Workshop Exercises for Brand Discovery

### 6 Core Workshop Exercises

**1. Brand Audit**
- Evaluate competitive position through SWOT analysis
- Ask: "What does your brand do better than competitors?"
- Identify threats and opportunities

**2. Positioning Statement**
- Clarify market differentiation
- Examine target audience, competition, and brand strengths
- Explain "who your audience is" and "what separates your brand"

**3. Brand Identity Exploration**
- Explore how components build recognition
- Ask: "Does our audience recognize elements like our logo?"
- Check: "Do marketing communications use consistent messaging?"

**4. Brand Persona Development**
- Define personality through customer understanding
- Prompt: "What personality traits should the brand embody?"
- Exercise: "If the brand were a person, what would they do?"

**5. Messaging Framework**
- Establish communication guidelines
- Ask: "What have been our most successful campaigns?"
- Identify where target audiences engage most

**6. Brand Story Creation**
- Create emotional connection
- Determine: "How should the story make our audience feel?"
- Identify what core values resonate with customers

### Interactive Workshop Techniques

**Word Card Exercise**
- Provide cards with alternative words on each side
- Participants determine which words are most relevant to brand values
- Examples: "modern" vs. "traditional," "reserved" vs. "welcoming," "serious" vs. "fun"

**Sliding Scale Exercise**
- Participants place markers on a sliding scale
- Questions: Where does the brand sit between "elite" and "mass market"?
- Is the brand more "serious" or "playful"?

**Celebrity Comparison Exercise**
- Show photos of four different celebrities
- Teams brainstorm ways the brand is like or unlike each celebrity
- Reveals personality traits and associations

**Visual Preference Exercise**
- Show curated selection of logos from various industries
- Ask clients which three resonate with them
- Note common elements in chosen logos

## Part 7: Avoiding Miscommunication and Rejection

### Red Flags During Discovery

**Communication Warning Signs:**
- "We've been burned by freelancers before" - may indicate trust issues or micromanagement
- "We want to move fast but we're not totally sure what we need" - leads to shifting goals
- "This should be super easy for someone like you" - devalues your work
- "You're the designer, just design something" - no respect for discovery process
- Constant complaints about previous designers/developers

**Scope Creep Signals:**
- "Can we just add one more page?"
- "This won't take long, right?"
- Incomplete design briefs
- Vague requirements
- Too many decision-makers (design by committee)

**Budget Warning Signs:**
- Immediate haggling over rates
- "Exposure" offered as payment
- Avoiding budget discussions
- Requests for "quick discounts"

### Prevention Strategies

**Before the Project:**
1. Use comprehensive client questionnaires
2. Create detailed project briefs
3. Establish transparent timelines
4. Use contracts that outline scope, revision limits, payment structure
5. Require 25-50% deposit before any design work

**Contract Clauses to Include:**
- "The scope of this agreement includes only the deliverables outlined in [Exhibit A]. Any additional work will be quoted and billed separately."
- Revision limit clauses
- Pause clauses for delayed feedback
- Kill fees

**During the Project:**
1. Maintain frequent check-ins
2. Use structured discovery sessions
3. Define feedback loops clearly
4. Document all decisions
5. Track version changes across drafts

### The Creative Brief: Your Shield Against Rejection

**Essential Elements:**
1. Company information
2. Brand guidelines (fonts, colors, imagery)
3. Project objectives
4. Target audience insights
5. Scope of work
6. Timelines and checkpoints
7. Budget constraints
8. Design preferences (logo type, color preferences, style, typography)
9. Imagery or symbols to include
10. What to avoid

**Best Practices:**
- Ask clients to react to specific examples rather than describe preferences
- Ground discussions in business purpose, not personal preferences
- Document everything in the brief instead of keeping it in your head
- List brands whose visual style inspires AND brands to avoid being associated with

### Handling Rejection Constructively

**The Right Approach:**
- Build relationship on trust and open communication
- Seek access throughout the design process
- Exchange information continuously

**When Designs Are Rejected:**
> "If you have a good relationship with the client, then it shouldn't be persuasion but convincing. The difference is that convincing is showing evidence and reason as to why and how your idea and concept works."

**Strategies:**
1. Show both options - client's preferred way and yours
2. Let client feel in control
3. Ask for specific reasoning if they reject your approach
4. Work toward compromise based on evidence

## Part 8: 72 Brand Strategy Questions (Complete Reference)

### Brand Positioning (10 Questions)
1. What customer pain points do we uniquely solve?
2. What uniquely sets our brand apart in our industry?
3. How can we leverage our unique selling proposition effectively?
4. How should we communicate our differentiation across channels?
5. Can our history contribute to our differentiated position?
6. What product or service aspects should we highlight?
7. How can we position ourselves against an enemy?
8. What emotional benefits does our differentiation offer customers?
9. How can storytelling reinforce our unique position?
10. How can our position evolve with the market?

### Brand Personality (8 Questions)
1. How would we describe our brand's human traits?
2. What tone of voice represents our brand's personality?
3. How do we ensure consistent personality reflection across platforms?
4. How can we ensure our personality is weaved through communication?
5. How does our verbal identity distinguish us from competitors?
6. How would we describe our tone of voice?
7. How can we adapt our language for different audience segments?
8. How can we use personnel or characters as a personality vehicle?

### Target Audience (9 Questions)
1. How do we define our ideal customer beyond basic demographics?
2. What psychographics capture our customers' behaviors?
3. What unique needs differentiate our target audience from general market?
4. How can our brand resonate more with our audience's lifestyle?
5. What emotional connections can we establish with our audience?
6. How does our target audience currently perceive our brand?
7. What are our audience's key decision-making factors?
8. How can customer feedback refine our audience targeting?
9. How do we cater to our target audience's evolving needs?

### Competitor Analysis (8 Questions)
1. How do competitors position themselves in the market?
2. What are our closest competitors' strengths and weaknesses?
3. How are we different from competitors in our audience's eyes?
4. What competitor trends should we learn from or avoid?
5. How can we engage our audience differently than competitors?
6. What market gaps are our competitors missing?
7. What else can we learn from our competitors' activities?
8. How can we stand apart from our competitors visually?

### Communication Strategy (6 Questions)
1. How can we tailor communication across different audience touchpoints?
2. What are the most effective channels for reaching our audience?
3. How do we integrate communication across platforms for cohesive message?
4. What feedback mechanisms improve our communication strategy?
5. How can content educate and engage our audience effectively?
6. What role does storytelling play in our communication strategy?

### Visual Identity (6 Questions)
1. What characteristics should shape our brand's identity design?
2. How does our visual identity reflect our personality and values?
3. How can we make our logo and design more memorable?
4. How do we adapt our visual identity across mediums consistently?
5. What role do color, typography, and imagery play in perception?
6. How can we consider our position in our visual design?

### Customer Experience (6 Questions)
1. How do we design an immersive brand experience for customers?
2. What sensory elements can we incorporate into brand experience?
3. How can technology create interactive brand experiences?
4. How do we personalize experiences to increase engagement?
5. How does experience design foster deeper emotional connections?
6. How do we measure the impact of our experience design?

### Offer Strategy (7 Questions)
1. How can we align our offerings with target audience needs?
2. What innovative features or services can enhance our offerings?
3. How do we communicate our offerings' value more effectively?
4. How do market trends impact our offer strategy?
5. What strategies increase customer loyalty and retention?
6. How can we use customer feedback to improve our offerings?
7. How do we differentiate our offerings from competitors?

### Marketing Strategy (7 Questions)
1. How can we align our marketing with our overall brand strategy?
2. How should we use data and analytics to refine our marketing?
3. How can we integrate customer feedback into marketing efforts?
4. What role does influencer marketing play in our strategy?
5. How should we ensure an agile, adaptable marketing strategy?
6. What partnerships or collaborations could enhance our marketing?
7. What steps should we take to bring our brand to market?

## Part 9: Quick-Reference Interview Guide

### Pre-Meeting Preparation
- [ ] Review any existing brand materials
- [ ] Research competitors beforehand
- [ ] Prepare curated logo examples to show
- [ ] Set up recording (with permission) for accurate notes

### The Discovery Meeting Structure

**Opening (5-10 minutes)**
- Build rapport
- Explain the discovery process and its importance
- Set expectations for the meeting

**Business Understanding (20-30 minutes)**
- Company history and motivation
- Products/services overview
- Competitive landscape
- Growth goals

**Audience Deep-Dive (15-20 minutes)**
- Target audience definition
- Pain points and solutions
- Customer journey
- Emotional connections

**Brand Essence (20-30 minutes)**
- Values and mission
- Brand personality
- How they want to be perceived
- What makes them different

**Visual Direction (15-20 minutes)**
- Current brand review
- Logo examples reactions
- Style preferences (modern/traditional, playful/serious)
- Colors and typography preferences
- What to avoid

**Project Parameters (10-15 minutes)**
- Budget and timeline
- Decision-makers
- Revision expectations
- Deliverable requirements

**Closing (5 minutes)**
- Summarize key points
- Clarify next steps
- Leave room for anything not covered

### The Single Most Important Question

> "How do you want clients/customers to feel when they work with you?"

This question reveals:
- Core business values
- Emotional positioning
- Brand personality
- Success metrics
