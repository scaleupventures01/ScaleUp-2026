Section 01

Key Strategic Insights

Insight #1
Your Margin Problem is a Pricing Problem, Not a Market Problem
Your 3% net margin in 2024 wasn't because the market won't pay - it's because you didn't enforce pricing discipline. Projects where you used AI-augmented delivery and held firm on pricing achieved 50% margins. The fix is operational discipline, not a new business model. This means $100K at 40-50% margins is absolutely achievable with your existing capabilities.
Insight #2
Paid Ads Give You Control Over Consulting Growth
You have zero proactive business development today - 100% referrals. The solution: leverage Roe to run paid ads for ScaleUp's consulting business. Unlike relying on a salesperson, paid ads give you direct control over lead flow - you can scale up or down based on capacity, test messaging yourself, and see immediate ROI data. With your ROI-focused positioning as the hook, paid ads can generate 2-3 qualified opportunities per month.
Insight #3
Prevyu Has a Viability Question That Can Be Answered in 60 Days
The day trading market is massive (10M+ active traders in the US), and your "pre-trade discipline" positioning is genuinely novel. But you've never tested willingness to pay. The good news: you can definitively answer whether Prevyu is viable with a focused 60-day sprint and $2-3K in ad spend. Define success criteria upfront, then make a clear go/no-go decision.
Insight #4
OfferPath Has Proven Unit Economics - Now Validating Conversion
UPDATE (January 2026): LinkedIn test succeeded AND full financial model is now confirmed:

Test Results ($333 spend): 11 leads at $30.27 CPL (22% under target), LinkedIn CPL of $26.38, 39% open rate, Actual CAC of $302.73 vs. $368 max viable (18% under).

Financial Model: $694/mo blended MRR ($499-$999 tiers), 53% gross margin (better than assumed 40%), 2.1x LTV:CAC ratio, $624K-$810K 2-year profit potential. Score improved from 99 → 167 (+68 points).

Note: OfferPath is a B2C product targeting individual professionals. LinkedIn works because the buyer IS the user (professional self-improvement).

January Validation: Work 11 leads + $200 more spend. Decision Jan 31: If 1+ convert, continue scaling. If 0 convert, sunset.
Insight #5
Your W-2 is a Strategic Asset, Not a Limitation
Most founders making your decision are desperate - they need the business to work or they can't pay rent. You have W-2 stability covering personal expenses. This means you can afford to take calculated risks (Prevyu validation) that a desperate founder couldn't. It also means you can walk away from bad-fit clients and hold firm on pricing.

Risk Management Plan

Risk 1: Paid Ads Fail to Generate Pipeline
40% Probability High Impact
Prevention
  • Clear KPIs from Day 1 (100 clicks/week, 3-5 consultations/week, 2 qualified opps/month)
  • Weekly ad performance reviews with accountability
  • Strong messaging with ROI-focused positioning
  • A/B test multiple ad creatives and audiences
Early Warning Signs
  • <1% CTR in first two weeks
  • Zero consultations booked after 30 days
  • High cost per click (>$5) without conversions
Response If It Happens
  • Week 4: Diagnose root cause (creative? targeting? landing page?)
  • Week 6: Test different platforms (LinkedIn vs Facebook vs Google)
  • Week 8: If still failing, pivot to founder-led LinkedIn outreach
Risk 2: Pricing Discipline Breaks Down
50% Probability High Impact
Prevention
  • Document pricing framework now (before pressure hits)
  • Require founder approval for ANY project below $15K
  • Build ROI model into every proposal (justifies premium)
  • Practice saying "This isn't a fit for us" to low-budget prospects
Early Warning Signs
  • First proposal is under $15K "just this once"
  • Client pushback leads to 20%+ discount
  • Scope creep without price adjustment
Response If It Happens
  • Immediate: Document what happened and why
  • Require 48-hour cooling off before accepting sub-$15K deals
  • Review ROI model before any pricing negotiation
Risk 3: Prevyu Validation is Inconclusive
35% Probability Medium Impact
Prevention
  • Define GO/NO-GO criteria clearly upfront
  • Commit to the March 15 decision date publicly
  • Require hard data, not "gut feel" for decision
Early Warning Signs
  • Numbers close to thresholds (e.g., 45 users instead of 50)
  • Engagement inconsistent week-to-week
  • Survey results are ambiguous
Response If It Happens
  • If genuinely ambiguous: Extend validation 30 days with $500 max budget
  • Set HARD deadline of April 15 for final decision
  • Default to NO-GO if still ambiguous - bias toward action
