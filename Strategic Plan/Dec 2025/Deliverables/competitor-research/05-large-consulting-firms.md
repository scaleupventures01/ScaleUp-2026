# Competitive Research: Large Consulting Firms

## Overview

This research analyzes how large consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture, BCG, Bain, and the Big Four) overlap or compete with ScaleUp Ventures, a boutique consulting firm serving SMBs with $20K+ projects in development, growth, and process improvement.

**Key Finding:** Large consulting firms present **minimal direct competitive threat** to ScaleUp Ventures due to fundamental differences in target market, minimum engagement sizes, and service delivery models. However, their "lite" offerings and AI-focused services warrant monitoring.

---

## Executive Summary

| Factor | Large Consulting Firms | ScaleUp Ventures | Competitive Overlap |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Minimum Project Size** | $500K - $1M+ | $20K+ | **None** |
| **Target Client Revenue** | $1B+ (typically Fortune 500) | SMBs | **None** |
| **Typical Engagement** | 6-12 months | Weeks to months | **Minimal** |
| **Hourly Rates** | $350 - $1,200/hr | Lower/competitive | **None** |
| **AI/Automation Services** | Enterprise-scale only | SMB-focused | **None** |
| **Digital Transformation** | Enterprise focus | SMB focus | **None** |

#### Competitive Threat Level: **NONE**
ScaleUp's $20K project minimum is 25-50x below McKinsey's entry point.

## Why SMBs Don't Use Large Consulting Firms

Research identifies several structural barriers preventing SMBs from engaging large consulting firms:

### 1. Cost Prohibitive
- Minimum engagements of $500K+ vs. SMB budgets of $20-100K
- Hourly rates of $350-1,200 vs. boutique rates of $100-250

### 2. Frameworks Designed for Enterprise
"When McKinsey or Deloitte consulting teams arrive at a mid-size company, they bring frameworks designed for organizations with 10,000+ employees and billion-dollar IT budgets."

### 3. The "Handoff Problem"
"Perhaps the most significant failure of Big 4 consulting for mid-size companies is the handoff problem. These firms excel at strategy and pilot development but often disappear when it's time for full implementation."

### 4. Over-Staffing
"You get very sophisticated thought leaders that you can engage directly [with boutique firms]. They don't have to come with a big team, which is the only way that you can buy them if they were working inside McKinsey."

### 5. Misaligned Incentives
Large firms prioritize Fortune 500 relationships over smaller engagements.

## AI/Automation Consulting Landscape

### Market Overview
- Global AI Consulting market: $14B (2024), projected $72.8B by 2030 (31.6% CAGR)
- Large enterprises dominate: 63.1% market share in 2024
- Tier 1 vendors (IBM, Accenture, Deloitte, PwC): 50-55% market share

### Large Firm AI Investments
| Firm | Investment | Focus |
|------|------------|-------|
| Accenture | $3B | Enterprise AI transformation |
| Deloitte | $3B | Healthcare, retail AI |
| McKinsey | 40% of projects AI-related | Enterprise strategy |
| PwC | $1B | Generative AI integration |

### SMB AI Consulting Gap
- "Small and mid-sized enterprises are growing fastest in AI adoption - projected 25.70% CAGR from 2025-2032"
- "Cloud-based AI tools and more affordable consulting packages are making AI accessible to smaller players"
- **40% of AI consulting deals under $5M now go to boutiques** (up from 15% in 2023)

### Opportunity for ScaleUp
The AI consulting market for SMBs is underserved by large firms, creating a significant opportunity for boutique firms like ScaleUp to capture this growing segment.

## Competitive Threat Assessment

### Direct Competitive Threat: **MINIMAL**

| Firm | Threat Level | Rationale |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| McKinsey | None | $500K+ minimums, Fortune 500 focus |
| BCG | None | $500K+ minimums, enterprise only |
| Bain | None | Fortune 1000 exclusive |
| Deloitte | Low | EGC services possible overlap, but different focus |
| Accenture | Low-Medium | 100+ employee minimum creates small overlap |
| PwC | Low | M&A/transaction focus |
| EY | Very Low | Enterprise audit/tax focus |
| KPMG | Very Low | No SMB presence |

### Indirect Competitive Considerations

1. **Brand Perception:** SMBs may believe they "should" hire a big name, but cost quickly disqualifies this option.

2. **Trickle-Down Talent:** Former Big Four consultants starting boutique practices could be competitors.

3. **"Lite" Product Development:** Watch for future down-market moves, particularly in AI services.

4. **Acquisition Risk:** Large firms occasionally acquire successful boutique consultancies.

## Why SMBs Should Choose ScaleUp Over Large Firms

Based on research, boutique firms like ScaleUp offer significant advantages:

### 1. Right-Sized Engagement
- $20K+ projects vs. $500K+ minimums
- Appropriate for SMB budgets and scope

### 2. Personalized Service
"Unlike larger firms that may rely on standardized frameworks, boutique consultants take the time to analyze your unique challenges and goals."

### 3. Senior Partner Access
"Boutique consulting firms tend to have smaller teams and closer access to partners."

### 4. Implementation Focus
"Boutique firms generally can't beat McKinsey, Bain, and BCG on scale, networks, or reputation, but they can outflank the MBB firms by delivering services the major strategy players can't or don't offer."

### 5. Speed and Agility
"Small AI-first boutique firms are challenging McKinsey, BCG, and Deloitte with superior speed and specialization."

### 6. Cost Efficiency
- Nearshore alternatives offer "45% lower cost" than Big Four
- Boutiques can provide specialized expertise without enterprise overhead

---

## Monitoring Recommendations

### Watch For:
1. **Accenture down-market expansion** - They've already moved to 100-employee minimum
2. **AI "lite" products** from large firms targeting mid-market
3. **OpenAI and tech company consulting services** - "OpenAI launches consulting division...Making McKinsey, Accenture, Big 4s nervous"
4. **Big Four spin-off boutiques** focused on mid-market

### Competitive Intelligence Triggers:
- Any announcement of SMB-specific practice groups
- Acquisition of boutique firms in the SMB space
- New "lite" or "express" service tiers
- AI automation tools marketed to small businesses

---

## Conclusion

Large consulting firms operate in an entirely different market segment than ScaleUp Ventures. The structural barriers - minimum engagement sizes of $500K+, enterprise frameworks, and Fortune 500 focus - effectively eliminate direct competition.

**ScaleUp's Competitive Position:** Strong. The firm occupies a protected market niche that large consulting firms cannot profitably serve. The primary competitive considerations are:
- Other boutique consulting firms
- Freelance consultants and fractional executives
- In-house capabilities at SMBs
- Emerging AI tools that could disintermediate consulting

The greatest opportunity lies in the growing SMB AI consulting market, where 40% of deals under $5M now go to boutique firms - a segment large firms are structurally unable to address.
