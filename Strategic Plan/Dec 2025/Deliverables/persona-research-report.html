Section 03

Hypothesis Testing (Devil's Advocate)

Critical Analysis of Your Hypotheses
Hypothesis 1: Small Legal Firms as Target
Validated with Results Guarantee
Your hypothesis: "New legal firms need clients. Offer lead gen with a results guarantee to eliminate their risk."

Original concern: New law firms typically have <$100K in startup capital. They can't afford $15-20K projects.

How the results guarantee changes this: A $3-5K lead gen package WITH a money-back guarantee flips the risk equation:
  • New firm's risk = $0 (if you don't deliver, they get refunded)
  • Your risk = manageable (you control the ads, you know CPL economics)
  • $3-5K is affordable even for new firms when risk is eliminated

The pitch: "We guarantee you X qualified leads in 30 days or your money back."

The expand path: Deliver leads → Build trust → "Now let's improve your website to convert more of these leads" → Automation as they scale.

Verdict: NEW legal firms are viable with results guarantee model. This opens a market competitors won't touch.
Hypothesis 2: Lead Gen as Entry Point
Validated with Refinement
Your hypothesis: "Pitch lead gen services, but bundle with 'we need to improve your landing page first' - then scale out to full website."

Why this works: Lead gen is the sexy pitch - it's ROI-focused, measurable, and gets attention. But bundling landing page work addresses the devil's advocate concern (can't run ads to a bad site). The landing page becomes a lower-friction entry point ($3-8K vs $15-25K for full website).

The refined funnel:
  1. PITCH: Lead Gen / Growth Marketing (the attraction)
  2. ENTRY: Landing Page ($3-8K - proves value, low risk)
  3. EXPAND: Full Website ($15-25K)
  4. DELIVER: Lead Gen / Paid Ads ($2-5K/mo retainer)
  5. UPSELL: AI / Automation ($10-20K)

Key insight: You're not selling website work first - you're selling growth. The landing page is positioned as "what we need to make your growth investment work" rather than a standalone project.
Hypothesis 3: $20K-$50K Revenue Startups
Not Validated
Your hypothesis: "Startups making $20K-$50K might be very interested in ROI-based growth marketing."

Devil's advocate: This is a phantom market. A $20K project for a $50K revenue startup = 40% of annual revenue. No rational buyer does this. They're interested, yes. But they can't afford you.

Verdict: Small revenue startups can't afford $15K+ projects. Focus elsewhere.
Hypothesis 4: Funded Startups Need Dev Partners
Not Validated - Market Shifted
Original assumption: "Non-technical founders with funding need agencies to be their dev team."

Research findings (2025 data):
  • 88% of YC S25 startups are AI-native with technical founders
  • AI tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Loveable) enable non-technical founders to build themselves
  • VCs now require functional products - "Pre-Seed on a deck is nearly dead"
  • Non-technical solo founders: <0.6% acceptance rate at top accelerators
  • Investors view agency-dependency negatively - signals lack of technical capability

Verdict: The funded startup market has fundamentally changed. Non-technical founders either learn to use AI tools or don't get funded. REMOVED as Persona 2 - replaced with technology-cautious industry (Insurance).
Hypothesis 5: Technology-Cautious Industries
Strongly Validated
Your hypothesis: "Focus on older business owners NOT using technology, scared of being disrupted by AI/digital competitors."

Research findings:
  • Independent Insurance Agencies: Average agent age is 60; high-digital agencies grow 70% faster
  • Construction/Trades: Average owner 42+; only 28% use CRM; massive AI disruption fear
  • Manufacturing: Aging workforce; legacy systems; digital transformation pressure

Why this works for ScaleUp: These owners CANNOT use AI tools themselves (unlike funded startup founders). They have budget, fear of disruption, and genuinely need external help to modernize.

Verdict: Insurance agencies selected as new Persona 2. Perfect fit: business owners (avg age 60), proven budget, 78% increasing tech spend, trust-based sales matches ScaleUp's approach.
